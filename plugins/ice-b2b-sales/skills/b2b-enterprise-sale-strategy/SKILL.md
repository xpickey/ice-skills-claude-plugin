## Solution Selling Framework

### Phase 1: DISCOVER — Deep Client Understanding

The foundation of every enterprise deal. Never skip this phase.

**Stakeholder Power Mapping (Thai Context)**
```
C-Level (ผู้บริหารระดับสูง)
├── CEO/MD — Vision, strategic alignment, final sign-off
├── CFO — ROI, TCO, budget approval, compliance
├── CIO/CTO — Technical fit, architecture, integration
├── COO — Process improvement, operational efficiency
└── CHRO — Change management, organizational readiness

Middle Management (ผู้จัดการระดับกลาง)
├── VP/Director Finance — Day-to-day pain owner
├── VP/Director IT — Implementation feasibility
├── VP/Director Operations — Business process impact
└── VP/Director Procurement — Vendor evaluation, compliance

Influencers & Gatekeepers
├── External Consultants — Often Big Four, trusted advisors
├── Board Members — Family connections, business networks
├── Existing Vendor Relationships — Incumbent advantage/threat
└── Industry Peers — Reference checks, reputation
```

**Discovery Questions Framework (Solution Selling)**
1. **Situation**: Current systems, processes, pain points, org structure
2. **Problem**: Business impact of current state, cost of inaction
3. **Implication**: What happens if nothing changes in 12-24 months?
4. **Need-Payoff**: What would ideal state look like? What's the value?

### Phase 2: CONSULT — Advisory-Led Engagement

Position yourself as a **trusted advisor**, not a vendor. Use consulting frameworks:

- **Design Thinking Workshop**: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test
- **Business Case Development**: Current state cost → Future state value → Gap = Opportunity
- **Maturity Assessment**: Where is the client today vs. industry best practice?
- **Transformation Roadmap**: Quick wins → Foundation → Optimization → Innovation

### Phase 3: ARCHITECT — Solution Design

Build solutions around **business outcomes**, not product features:

| Client Says | Don't Sell | Do Sell |
|---|---|---|
| "We need new ERP" | ERP modules list | Finance transformation with real-time insights |
| "Our reporting is slow" | EPM tool features | Decision velocity improvement with 70% faster close |
| "Too many manual processes" | Automation features | Digital workforce enabling strategic focus |
| "We're growing fast" | Scalable architecture | Growth-ready platform that won't slow you down |

### Phase 4: PARTNER — Win-Win Deal Structuring

**Pricing Strategy Principles**
- Never race to the bottom on price — compete on value
- Structure deals for mutual long-term benefit
- Consider: Implementation + License + AMS + Training + Change Management
- Offer phased approaches to reduce client risk
- Build in success metrics tied to business outcomes

**Negotiation with Thai Business Culture**
- Respect hierarchy (เกรงใจ) — never embarrass senior decision-makers
- Build relationships before business (สร้างความสัมพันธ์ก่อนทำธุรกิจ)
- Patience is strategic — Thai enterprise deals often take 6-18 months
- Face-to-face meetings are essential — even in digital age
- Gift of knowledge > gift of entertainment — position yourself as educator

### Phase 5: SUSTAIN — Post-Sale Excellence & Growth

**Account Growth Model**
```
Year 1: Deliver → Trust → Reference
Year 2: Expand → Cross-sell → Deeper integration
Year 3: Transform → Strategic partner → Innovation co-creation
Year 4+: Ecosystem → Advisory board → Market leadership together
```

## Objection Handling Playbook

### Top 10 Enterprise Objections in Thailand

1. **"แพงเกินไป" (Too expensive)**
   → Reframe: "What's the cost of NOT transforming?" + TCO comparison over 5 years

2. **"เราไม่พร้อม" (We're not ready)**
   → Respond: "That's exactly why we start with assessment, not implementation"

3. **"ระบบเก่ายังใช้ได้" (Current system still works)**
   → Challenge: "Works ≠ Competitive. Where will your industry be in 3 years?"

4. **"เคยล้มเหลวมาแล้ว" (We failed before)**
   → Empathize: "Many organizations have. Let's analyze why and design for success"

5. **"คู่แข่งเสนอถูกกว่า" (Competitor offered cheaper)**
   → Clarify: "Let's compare scope-for-scope, not just price. What's included?"

6. **"ต้องเอาเข้า Board ก่อน" (Need board approval)**
   → Enable: "Let us help build the business case that makes board approval easy"

7. **"ไม่มีคนพอ" (Not enough people)**
   → Solve: "Our implementation methodology includes dedicated change management and knowledge transfer"

8. **"ต้องดูอีกหลายเจ้า" (Need to evaluate more vendors)**
   → Welcome: "Absolutely. We encourage comparison. Here's our differentiation framework"

9. **"Timeline ไม่ทัน" (Timeline won't work)**
   → Adapt: "Let's design a phased approach — quick wins in Phase 1, full value in Phase 2"

10. **"เราจะทำเองได้ไหม" (Can we do it ourselves?)**
    → Honest: "Some parts yes. For complex transformation, experience accelerates success by 3x"

## Proposal & RFP Strategy

### Winning Proposal Structure (Big Four Standard)

```
1. Executive Summary (1-2 pages) — "Why Us, Why Now"
2. Understanding of Client Needs — Prove you listened
3. Proposed Solution — Business outcomes first, technology second
4. Implementation Approach — Methodology, phases, milestones
5. Team & Experience — Named consultants, relevant references
6. Investment Summary — Value-based, phased, transparent
7. Risk Mitigation — Proactive risk identification and response
8. References & Case Studies — Similar industry/scale
9. Why [Our Company] — Differentiation, commitment, vision
```

### RFP Response Best Practices
- Answer every question directly — never skip or be vague
- Lead with business understanding, follow with technical detail
- Include Thailand-specific references whenever possible
- Price competitively but never cheapest — sell value
- Offer site visits, proof-of-concept, or design thinking workshops
