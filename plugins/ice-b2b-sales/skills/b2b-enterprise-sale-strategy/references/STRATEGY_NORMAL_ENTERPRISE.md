## 1. Thailand Mid-Market & SME+ Landscape

### Segment Definition
```
NORMAL ENTERPRISE (Non-Listed / Mid-Market / SME+)
├── Revenue: 100M - 5B THB annually
├── Employees: 50 - 2,000 staff
├── IT Team: 2-15 people (often no dedicated CIO)
├── Current Systems: Excel-heavy, local accounting, basic ERP, or outgrown systems
├── Decision-Maker: Owner/MD directly or Owner + Finance Director
└── Budget for IT: 5-50M THB for transformational projects

Typical Company Types:
├── Family Businesses (ธุรกิจครอบครัว) — 2nd/3rd generation scaling up
├── Fast-Growth Companies — Startups that outgrew basic systems
├── Regional Champions — Strong in specific geography or niche
├── Export-Oriented Manufacturers — Need compliance, multi-currency
├── Service Companies — Professional services, logistics, hospitality
└── Distribution / Trading Companies — Complex supply chain needs
```

### Key Business Triggers for ERP/IT Investment
- **Growth Pain**: Manual processes can't keep up with business growth
- **Regulatory Compliance**: e-Tax, Transfer Pricing, BOI reporting, PDPA
- **Succession Planning**: Next generation wants modern systems
- **Export Requirements**: International customers demand digital integration
- **Audit Findings**: Auditors recommend better financial controls
- **Competitive Pressure**: Competitors are digitizing faster
- **M&A Activity**: Acquiring or being acquired — need standardized systems

## 2. Decision-Making Characteristics

### Owner-Driven Decision Model
```
OWNER / FOUNDER (เจ้าของกิจการ)
│ THE decision-maker — everything starts and ends here
│ Concerns: ROI, business disruption, trust in vendor, total cost
│
├── FINANCE DIRECTOR / CONTROLLER (ผู้อำนวยการฝ่ายการเงิน)
│   │ Often the project champion or most impacted user
│   │ Concerns: Month-end close, reporting, audit, compliance
│   │
│   └── Accounting Team — End users, resistance risk
│
├── IT MANAGER (ผู้จัดการฝ่าย IT)
│   │ Often 1-3 people, generalist, wearing many hats
│   │ Concerns: Can we maintain this? Training? Integration?
│   │
│   └── Sometimes outsourced to external IT vendor
│
├── OPERATIONS / WAREHOUSE MANAGER
│   │ Impacted by SCM/inventory modules
│   │ Concerns: Disruption to daily operations
│   │
└── EXTERNAL ADVISORS
    ├── Auditor (สำนักงานสอบบัญชี) — Huge influence, trusted voice
    ├── Banker — Sometimes recommends systems for better reporting
    └── Business Friends / Network — "What system do you use?"
```

### Critical Insight: The Auditor Effect
In Thai mid-market, the **external auditor** (ผู้สอบบัญชี) is often the most trusted business
advisor. If the auditor recommends an ERP system, the owner listens. Build relationships with
major audit firms (Big Four + medium firms like Baker Tilly, Grant Thornton, BDO, PKF, Mazars).

### Speed of Decision
- Owner says yes → deal moves fast (days/weeks)
- Owner says "let me think about it" → could be months
- Owner says "talk to my IT" → either genuine or polite deflection
- Key: Get to the owner early, build trust personally

## 3. Sales Strategy & Velocity Selling

### The Normal Enterprise Sales Cycle
```
LEAD GEN → QUALIFY → DEMO → PROPOSE → NEGOTIATE → CLOSE → IMPLEMENT
  │          │         │        │          │          │         │
 1-2wk     1-2wk     1-2wk   1-2wk      1-4wk     1wk      3-9mo
                                                    
Total Cycle: 2-4 months (vs 6-18 months for large enterprise)
```

### Lead Generation Strategies

**Inbound (Pull):**
- SEO/SEM targeting "ระบบ ERP สำหรับธุรกิจขนาดกลาง", "โปรแกรมบัญชี enterprise"
- Social media content (LinkedIn, Facebook — Thai businesses use both)
- Webinars: "How to choose the right ERP for your growing business"
- Content marketing: Success stories, comparison guides, ROI calculators
- Google Ads targeting industry-specific ERP searches

**Outbound (Push):**
- Database of BOI-promoted companies (fast-growing, export-oriented)
- Department of Business Development (DBD) company data mining
- Industry association member lists (Federation of Thai Industries, Thai Chamber)
- Referrals from auditors, bankers, business consultants
- Event sponsorship at industry trade shows
- Cold outreach via LinkedIn to Finance Directors (warm intro preferred)

**Partner Channels:**
- Auditing firms — referral agreements
- Business consultants — co-selling arrangements
- Technology vendors — Oracle/SAP/MS partner programs
- Industry associations — preferred vendor status
- Banks — digital transformation lending + system recommendation

### Qualification Framework — BANT+C (Modified for Thai SME+)

| Criteria | Question | Go/No-Go |
|---|---|---|
| **B**udget | Is there budget or willingness to invest? | Min 3M THB for basic ERP |
| **A**uthority | Are we talking to the owner/decision-maker? | Must access owner within 2 meetings |
| **N**eed | Is there a genuine business pain? | Must articulate specific problems |
| **T**imeline | When do they need this? | Within 12 months = active pursuit |
| **C**omplexity | How complex is their business? | Right-size solution to complexity |

### Demo Strategy
For normal enterprise, the demo is often THE deciding moment:

**Demo Best Practices:**
- Keep it under 2 hours — owner's attention span is limited
- Show THEIR industry scenario, not generic demo data
- Focus on pain points they mentioned in discovery
- Show financial close process, reporting, and dashboards (finance team loves this)
- Include mobile capabilities (owners check business on phones)
- End with: "If we could deliver this in 4-6 months, would this solve your problem?"
- Always have a Thai-speaking demo presenter — English demos lose mid-market clients

## 4. Solution Right-Sizing

### Product-to-Segment Mapping

| Company Profile | Recommended Solution | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 50-200 employees, single entity | NetSuite, SAP B1, Odoo, MS BC | Affordable, quick deploy, good fit |
| 200-500 employees, multi-entity | NetSuite, Oracle Cloud, SAP BYD, MS F&O | Scalability, consolidation |
| 500-2000 employees, complex | Oracle Cloud ERP, SAP S/4 RISE, MS F&O | Enterprise-grade, industry depth |
| Manufacturing focus | SAP B1, Oracle Cloud SCM, Infor | MRP, production planning, shop floor |
| Service/project-based | NetSuite, MS F&O, Oracle Cloud | Project accounting, billing |
| Distribution/trading | SAP B1, NetSuite, Odoo | Inventory, multi-warehouse, logistics |
| Fast-growing tech company | NetSuite, Odoo | Cloud-native, subscription model |

### Implementation Approach for Normal Enterprise
```
PHASE 1: CORE FINANCE (2-3 months)
├── General Ledger, AP, AR, Fixed Assets
├── Thai Tax Compliance (VAT, WHT, e-Tax)
├── Financial Reporting & Dashboards
└── Bank Integration (if available)

PHASE 2: OPERATIONS (2-3 months)
├── Procurement / Purchase Orders
├── Inventory Management
├── Sales Order Processing
└── Basic SCM / Warehouse

PHASE 3: ADVANCED (2-3 months, optional)
├── Budgeting & Planning
├── Advanced Analytics / BI
├── CRM Integration
└── Custom Workflows
```

## 5. Pricing & Packaging

### Pricing Models for Normal Enterprise

**Model 1: All-Inclusive Package (Most Popular)**
```
PACKAGE PRICE: Fixed fee for defined scope
├── Software License/Subscription: Included
├── Implementation: Included (defined modules, users)
├── Data Migration: Included (standard migration)
├── Training: Included (defined hours)
├── Go-Live Support: Included (1-2 months)
├── Year 1 Support: Optional add-on
└── Total: Predictable, easy for owner to approve
```

**Model 2: Subscription-Based (Growing Trend)**
```
MONTHLY/ANNUAL SUBSCRIPTION
├── Software: Per user per month
├── Implementation: Spread over 12-24 months
├── Support: Included in subscription
└── Appeal: Lower upfront, operational expense vs capital expense
```

**Model 3: Phased Investment**
```
YEAR 1: Core implementation (60% of total investment)
YEAR 2: Advanced features + optimization (25%)
YEAR 3: Innovation + expansion (15%)
└── Appeal: Manages cash flow, proves value before full commitment
```

### Pricing Psychology for Thai Owner-Operators
- **Show total value first, then investment** — never lead with price
- **Compare to cost of current state** — "You're spending X on manual processes annually"
- **Reference similar-sized companies** — "Companies your size typically invest Y"
- **Offer early-decision incentives** — "Sign before quarter-end for Z% savings"
- **Never discount without getting something** — trade discount for reference, timeline, scope

## 6. Channel & Partner Strategy

### Partner Ecosystem for Normal Enterprise

**Technology Partners (ISV/VAR Model):**
- Be a certified partner of Oracle, SAP, Microsoft, NetSuite
- Maintain trained consultants with certifications
- Leverage vendor marketing development funds (MDF)
- Co-sell with vendor sales teams — they have client lists you don't

**Referral Partners:**
- **Accounting Firms**: Formalize referral agreements with fee/commission
- **Business Consultants**: Co-propose (they do strategy, you do systems)
- **Industry Associations**: Become preferred/recommended vendor
- **Banks**: Partner on "digital transformation" lending programs
- **Complementary Software Vendors**: BI, HR, POS — cross-refer

**Implementation Partners (for scale):**
- Build a bench of freelance consultants for peak demand
- Partner with smaller firms for specific industry expertise
- Offshore/nearshore model for cost-effective delivery

## 7. Scaling Through Referrals & Community

### The Referral Flywheel
```
DELIVER EXCELLENTLY
      │
      ▼
CLIENT BECOMES ADVOCATE
      │
      ▼
REFERRAL TO PEER COMPANY → NEW CLIENT → DELIVER EXCELLENTLY → ...
```

### Building the Referral Engine
- Ask for referrals at key milestones (go-live, 6-month review, 1-year anniversary)
- Create a "Customer Advisory Board" — quarterly meetup of your best clients
- Produce joint case studies (client gets PR, you get marketing)
- Host "User Group" events — your clients network with each other
- Incentivize referrals: discount on next year's support, free consulting days
- LinkedIn testimonials — easy ask, high visibility

### Community Building (Thai Market)
- Monthly webinars: "ERP Best Practices for Growing Thai Businesses"
- Line Official Account — Thai businesses prefer Line over email for quick comms
- Facebook Group for your user community
- Annual customer appreciation event (งานขอบคุณลูกค้า) — very Thai, very effective
- Regional roadshows (EEC, Northern, Southern industrial zones)

### Success Metrics for Normal Enterprise Sales
| Metric | Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Win Rate | 40-55% | Higher than large enterprise |
| Average Deal Size | 3-20M THB | Depending on product/scope |
| Sales Cycle | 2-4 months | Speed is key advantage |
| Customer Satisfaction | > 4.2/5.0 | Drives referrals |
| Referral Rate | 30%+ of new pipeline | Sign of healthy practice |
| Net Revenue Retention | > 110% | Upsell, cross-sell, expand |
