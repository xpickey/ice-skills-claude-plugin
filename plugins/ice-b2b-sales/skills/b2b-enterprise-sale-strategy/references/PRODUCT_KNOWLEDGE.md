## 1. Oracle Cloud Applications

### Oracle Cloud ERP (Fusion Cloud)
- **Positioning**: Best-of-breed cloud ERP for finance-centric organizations
- **Modules**: GL, AP, AR, FA, Cash Management, Expenses, Revenue Management, Intercompany
- **Strengths**: Continuous innovation (quarterly updates), AI/ML embedded, strong financial controls, multi-currency/entity, regulatory compliance
- **Thai Localization**: WHT, VAT, e-Tax XML, Thai language, PromptPay integration
- **Target**: Large enterprise, complex multi-entity, finance transformation
- **License Model**: SaaS subscription (per user or per module)
- **Implementation**: 4-12 months typical, Oracle Cloud methodology (Soar/Activate)

### Oracle Cloud EPM (Enterprise Performance Management)
- **Positioning**: Market leader in planning, budgeting, consolidation, and close
- **Modules**: Planning (PBCS/EPBCS), Financial Consolidation (FCCS), Account Reconciliation (ARCS), Narrative Reporting, Profitability & Cost Management, Enterprise Data Management (EDM), Tax Reporting
- **Strengths**: Best-in-class planning engine (SmartView/Excel integration), scenario modeling, what-if analysis, predictive planning with AI, works on top of ANY ERP
- **Sales Angle**: EPM is the #1 wedge product — doesn't require replacing ERP, adds immediate value
- **Target**: Any organization needing better planning, budgeting, or consolidation — especially conglomerates

### Oracle Cloud SCM
- **Positioning**: End-to-end supply chain from procurement to manufacturing to logistics
- **Modules**: Procurement, Inventory, Order Management, Manufacturing, Maintenance, Logistics, PLM, IoT
- **Strengths**: Integrated with Oracle ERP, strong manufacturing/discrete, IoT-ready
- **Target**: Manufacturing, distribution, complex supply chain operations

### Oracle Cloud HCM
- **Positioning**: Complete hire-to-retire HR platform
- **Modules**: Core HR, Talent Management, Workforce Management, Payroll, Learning
- **Thai Localization**: Thai social security, provident fund, tax calculation

### Oracle Cloud CX (Customer Experience)
- **Modules**: Sales, Service, Marketing, Commerce, CPQ
- **Positioning**: B2B CX — strong in configure-price-quote and complex selling

### Oracle OCO (Oracle Consulting Organization)
- Oracle's own professional services arm
- Competitor AND partner — they implement Oracle products directly
- Strategy: Position as local expertise, faster, more flexible vs Oracle OCO

## 2. SAP Portfolio

### SAP S/4HANA Cloud (via RISE with SAP)
- **Positioning**: Intelligent enterprise ERP, industry-specific
- **RISE with SAP**: Bundled offering — S/4HANA Cloud + BTP + Business Network + Analytics
- **Strengths**: Deep industry solutions (25+ industries), massive install base, process mining, Joule AI
- **Weaknesses (selling against)**: Complex licensing, expensive, long implementations, upgrade fatigue
- **Thai Market**: Strong in manufacturing, automotive, FMCG, chemicals
- **License Model**: RISE = subscription bundle; perpetual still available on-premise

### SAP BTP (Business Technology Platform)
- **Positioning**: Extension, integration, and innovation platform
- **Components**: Integration Suite, Extension Suite, AI, Analytics, Data Management
- **Sales Angle**: Often sold with S/4 to enable custom extensions without modifying core

### SAP GROW with SAP
- **Positioning**: Mid-market entry — simplified S/4HANA Cloud for growing companies
- **Target**: Companies too large for B1, ready for S/4 but want simpler path
- **Strengths**: Pre-configured best practices, faster deployment, lower cost than RISE

### SAP Business One (B1)
- **Positioning**: Entry-level ERP for small-medium businesses
- **Strengths**: Affordable, quick to deploy (2-4 months), good manufacturing/distribution
- **Thai Market**: Strong partner ecosystem, many local add-ons (Thai tax, Thai payroll)
- **Target**: 10-200 employees, single entity, manufacturing/distribution/trading
- **License Model**: On-premise perpetual or SAP B1 Cloud subscription

### SAP Business ByDesign (BYD)
- **Positioning**: Mid-market cloud ERP (between B1 and S/4)
- **Status**: Limited investment from SAP — being sunset in favor of GROW with SAP
- **Caution**: Selling BYD is risky — recommend GROW or B1 instead

### SAP HANA (Database)
- **Positioning**: In-memory database platform
- **Context**: Required for S/4HANA, optional for other workloads
- **Competitive Note**: Oracle Autonomous Database and Oracle Exadata compete here

## 3. Microsoft Dynamics Portfolio

### Microsoft Dynamics 365 Finance & Operations (F&O)
- **Positioning**: Enterprise-grade ERP integrated with Microsoft ecosystem
- **Strengths**: Deep integration with Microsoft 365, Power Platform, Azure, Copilot AI
- **Modules**: Finance, Supply Chain, Commerce, HR, Project Operations
- **Thai Localization**: Available through ISV add-ons (Thai tax, WHT, e-Tax)
- **Target**: Organizations heavily invested in Microsoft ecosystem
- **License Model**: Per user per month subscription

### Microsoft Dynamics 365 Business Central (BC)
- **Positioning**: SMB ERP — successor to Navision
- **Strengths**: Familiar Microsoft UX, strong partner ecosystem, affordable
- **Target**: 20-500 employees, service/distribution/manufacturing
- **Thai Market**: Growing partner ecosystem, Thai localization available
- **License Model**: Essentials (finance + SCM) or Premium (+ manufacturing + service)

### Microsoft Dynamics (Legacy: Navision / AX / GP)
- **Navision (NAV)**: Predecessor to BC — many Thai companies still running it
- **AX (Dynamics AX)**: Predecessor to F&O — upgrade path to D365 F&O
- **Great Plains (GP)**: Being sunset — migration opportunity to BC or F&O
- **Sales Angle**: Huge upgrade/migration market for legacy Dynamics users

### Microsoft Power Platform
- **Power BI**: Business intelligence and analytics
- **Power Apps**: Low-code application development
- **Power Automate**: Workflow automation (RPA, process flows)
- **Copilot Studio**: AI-powered assistants
- **Sales Angle**: Often the entry point — Power BI/Apps lead to D365 conversations

## 4. Oracle NetSuite

- **Positioning**: #1 cloud ERP for mid-market and fast-growing companies
- **Strengths**: True cloud (born-in-cloud), rapid deployment, SuiteSuccess methodology, strong financial core, multi-subsidiary, multi-currency
- **Modules**: ERP (Financials, Inventory, Order Mgmt), CRM, eCommerce (SuiteCommerce), HCM, PSA
- **SuiteSuccess**: Industry-specific pre-configured editions (reduces implementation time)
- **Thai Localization**: SuiteApp for Thai tax, WHT, VAT, Thai language, e-Tax
- **Target**: 50-2000 employees, multi-entity, international operations, high-growth
- **Competitive Position**: vs SAP B1 (more modern, cloud-native) / vs Oracle Cloud (faster, simpler) / vs Odoo (more enterprise-ready)
- **License Model**: Annual subscription per module + user

## 5. Infor ERP

- **Positioning**: Industry-specific cloud ERP
- **Key Products**: CloudSuite Industrial (SyteLine), CloudSuite Distribution, CloudSuite Fashion, CloudSuite Food & Beverage, CloudSuite Automotive
- **Strengths**: Deep industry functionality, strong in manufacturing verticals
- **Weaknesses**: Smaller ecosystem than Oracle/SAP/MS, limited Thai partner network
- **Thai Market**: Niche presence — mostly in manufacturing (automotive, electronics)
- **Target**: Mid-to-large manufacturers wanting industry-specific depth
- **Owned by**: Koch Industries (private) — stable but less visible than public vendors

## 6. Odoo ERP

- **Positioning**: Open-source, modular, affordable ERP for SMEs
- **Strengths**: Extremely affordable, modular (buy only what you need), large app ecosystem, modern UX, active community
- **Modules**: Accounting, Sales, CRM, Inventory, Manufacturing, HR, Website, eCommerce, Project, Marketing
- **Versions**: Community (free, open-source) vs Enterprise (paid, full features)
- **Thai Market**: Growing rapidly among cost-conscious SMEs, active Thai community
- **Target**: 10-500 employees, budget-conscious, willing to accept less industry depth
- **Competitive Position**: Price disruptor — forces Oracle/SAP/MS to justify premium
- **Risks**: Less robust for complex multi-entity, limited enterprise-grade controls, customization can create technical debt

---

## 7. Salesforce CRM

- **Positioning**: World's #1 CRM platform
- **Products**: Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, Commerce Cloud, Platform (Force.com), Tableau (Analytics), MuleSoft (Integration), Slack
- **Strengths**: Best-in-class CRM, massive ecosystem (AppExchange), powerful platform
- **Thai Market**: Strong in financial services, telecom, retail, real estate
- **Sales Angle**: CRM first → then expand to ERP conversation (or vice versa)
- **Integration**: Works with any ERP — often paired with Oracle/SAP/MS for full stack
- **Competitive Note**: Salesforce is often NOT a direct competitor but a complement. If client needs CRM + ERP, position your ERP alongside Salesforce

## 8. Competitive Comparison Quick Reference

### By Company Size & Complexity

| Company Size | Simple Needs | Moderate | Complex |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Small (10-50)** | Odoo, SAP B1 | SAP B1, NetSuite | — |
| **Mid (50-500)** | NetSuite, MS BC | NetSuite, MS F&O, SAP GROW | Oracle Cloud, SAP RISE |
| **Large (500-5000)** | — | Oracle Cloud, SAP RISE, MS F&O | Oracle Cloud + EPM, SAP RISE + BTP |
| **Enterprise (5000+)** | — | — | Oracle Cloud, SAP RISE |

### By Key Differentiator

| If Client Values... | Best Fit | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Financial depth & control | Oracle Cloud ERP | Best-in-class financials, EPM integration |
| Manufacturing depth | SAP S/4 or Infor | Deep MRP, shop floor, industry processes |
| Microsoft ecosystem | MS Dynamics 365 | Power Platform, Teams, Azure integration |
| Speed to value | NetSuite SuiteSuccess | Pre-configured, rapid deployment |
| Lowest cost | Odoo Enterprise | Open-source base, modular pricing |
| CRM excellence | Salesforce | Unmatched CRM + ecosystem |
| Planning & budgeting | Oracle EPM | Market-leading planning engine |
| Industry-specific | Infor CloudSuite | Deep vertical functionality |

### Common Competitive Battlecards

**Oracle vs SAP**: Oracle wins on cloud-native architecture, innovation speed, and financial depth. SAP wins on manufacturing breadth and installed base loyalty. Position Oracle as "future-ready" vs SAP as "legacy transformation."

**Oracle vs Microsoft**: Oracle wins on financial sophistication and ERP depth. Microsoft wins on ecosystem integration (Office, Teams, Azure). Position Oracle when finance/SCM complexity is high; concede to MS when client is deeply Microsoft-committed.

**NetSuite vs SAP B1**: NetSuite wins on cloud-native, multi-subsidiary, and modern UX. SAP B1 wins on local partner ecosystem and manufacturing add-ons. Position NetSuite for growth-oriented, multi-entity companies.

**Any Enterprise ERP vs Odoo**: Enterprise ERP wins on security, controls, audit trail, compliance, and support. Odoo wins on cost and flexibility. Position enterprise ERP when client has regulatory requirements, complex processes, or audit scrutiny.
