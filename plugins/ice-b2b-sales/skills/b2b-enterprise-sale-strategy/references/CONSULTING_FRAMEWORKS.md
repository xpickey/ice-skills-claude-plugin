## 1. Strategic Frameworks for Sales Advisory

### When to Use Consulting Frameworks in Sales
- **During Discovery**: Structure client's problems using frameworks
- **In Proposals**: Demonstrate structured thinking and methodology
- **In Workshops**: Facilitate client thinking with proven frameworks
- **In Negotiation**: Build business cases with consultant-grade rigor
- **Post-Sale**: Deliver implementation with consulting quality standards

### The Consulting Mindset in Sales
```
TRADITIONAL SALES              CONSULTATIVE SALES
─────────────────              ─────────────────
"Let me show you               "Let me understand
 our product"                   your business first"
        │                              │
Feature presentation            Structured discovery
        │                              │
Price negotiation               Value articulation
        │                              │
Close the deal                  Solve the problem
        │                              │
Move to next deal               Build the relationship
```

## 2. BCG Frameworks Applied to Enterprise Selling

### BCG Growth-Share Matrix (for Account Prioritization)
Use to prioritize accounts in your pipeline:

| Quadrant | Description | Sales Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Stars** (High growth, High share) | Large active opportunities | Invest heavily, win at all cost |
| **Cash Cows** (Low growth, High share) | Existing clients, renewals | Protect, upsell, maximize value |
| **Question Marks** (High growth, Low share) | New market opportunities | Selective investment, prove value |
| **Dogs** (Low growth, Low share) | Low-probability deals | Minimize effort or deprioritize |

### BCG Value Creation Framework (for Business Case Building)
Structure every business case around value creation:
```
REVENUE GROWTH          MARGIN IMPROVEMENT         ASSET EFFICIENCY
├── New markets         ├── Cost reduction          ├── Working capital
├── New products        ├── Process automation       ├── Fixed asset utilization
├── Customer retention  ├── Procurement savings      ├── IT rationalization
└── Pricing optimization└── Shared services          └── Cash cycle improvement
```

### BCG Digital Acceleration Index (for Digital Transformation Sales)
Assess client's digital maturity to scope the right solution:
- **Level 1 — Nascent**: Paper/Excel-based, no integrated systems
- **Level 2 — Emerging**: Basic ERP/accounting, some automation
- **Level 3 — Connected**: Integrated ERP, basic analytics, cloud adoption
- **Level 4 — Intelligent**: AI/ML, predictive analytics, real-time dashboards
- **Level 5 — Autonomous**: Fully digital, self-optimizing processes

## 3. McKinsey Frameworks Applied to Enterprise Selling

### McKinsey 7S Framework (for Organizational Readiness Assessment)
Use to assess if the client is ready for transformation:

| Element | Assessment Question | Sales Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** | Is digital transformation in their strategy? | If yes, budget likely exists |
| **Structure** | Is org structure ready for change? | Complex = longer sales cycle |
| **Systems** | What's current tech landscape? | Determines migration complexity |
| **Shared Values** | Does culture support innovation? | Low = need change management |
| **Style** | Leadership style — top-down or collaborative? | Affects engagement approach |
| **Staff** | Do they have skilled IT/finance people? | Gaps = training/AMS opportunity |
| **Skills** | What capabilities are missing? | Skills gap = bigger deal scope |

### McKinsey Three Horizons (for Transformation Roadmap Selling)
Position your solution across three time horizons:
```
HORIZON 1 (0-12 months): Optimize Current Operations
├── Fix immediate pain points
├── Implement core finance/SCM
└── Establish data foundation

HORIZON 2 (12-24 months): Build New Capabilities
├── Advanced analytics & reporting
├── Process automation (RPA, workflows)
└── Integrated planning & budgeting

HORIZON 3 (24-36 months): Create Future Advantage
├── AI/ML-driven insights
├── Predictive operations
└── Innovation & digital business models
```

### MECE Principle (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive)
Apply to every proposal structure, problem definition, and solution design:
- Break problems into non-overlapping categories
- Ensure all relevant areas are covered
- Demonstrates rigorous thinking to sophisticated buyers

### Pyramid Principle (Barbara Minto)
Structure all client-facing communications:
```
ANSWER FIRST (Executive Summary)
├── Supporting Argument 1
│   ├── Evidence / Data
│   └── Evidence / Data
├── Supporting Argument 2
│   ├── Evidence / Data
│   └── Evidence / Data
└── Supporting Argument 3
    ├── Evidence / Data
    └── Evidence / Data
```

## 4. PwC Advisory Methodology for Sales

### PwC BXT Framework (Business, Experience, Technology)
Position every solution at the intersection of three dimensions:

- **Business**: What business outcome does this enable? (Revenue, cost, risk)
- **Experience**: How does this improve user/customer experience?
- **Technology**: What technology enables the transformation?

Sales Application: Never sell technology alone. Always lead with business outcome,
enhance with experience improvement, support with technology capability.

### PwC Fit-for-Growth Framework
Use when client is cost-conscious or in cost-cutting mode:
```
CUT COSTS TO GROW
├── Identify costs that don't support strategy → Eliminate
├── Identify costs that support strategy → Invest
├── Optimize everything else → Automate with ERP/EPM
└── Reinvest savings into growth initiatives
```

### PwC Risk-Based Approach
Position ERP/EPM as risk mitigation:
- **Financial Risk**: Poor controls, manual errors, fraud exposure → ERP financial controls
- **Compliance Risk**: Tax, regulatory, audit findings → ERP compliance features
- **Operational Risk**: Supply chain disruption, inefficiency → SCM/ERP integration
- **Strategic Risk**: Outdated technology, inability to scale → Cloud transformation

## 5. Deloitte Digital Transformation Sales Approach

### Deloitte Digital Maturity Model
Use to benchmark client and scope transformation:

| Dimension | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategy** | No digital strategy | Department-level | Enterprise-wide | Digital-first |
| **Technology** | Legacy, fragmented | Partial cloud | Cloud-first | AI/ML embedded |
| **People** | Limited digital skills | Pockets of capability | Digital culture | Digital DNA |
| **Process** | Manual, siloed | Partially automated | Integrated, data-driven | Autonomous |
| **Data** | Inconsistent, siloed | Centralized, clean | Analytics-driven | Predictive/prescriptive |

### Deloitte's "Tech-Enabled Transformation" Approach
Position every deal as business transformation enabled by technology:
1. **Envision**: What's the future state? (Design thinking workshops)
2. **Design**: How do we get there? (Solution architecture, roadmap)
3. **Deliver**: Build it right (Implementation methodology)
4. **Operate**: Run it better (Managed services, continuous improvement)

## 6. KPMG Governance & Risk-Led Selling

### KPMG Connected Enterprise Framework
Position ERP as the backbone of a "Connected Enterprise":
- **Front Office**: Customer engagement (CRM, commerce)
- **Middle Office**: Operations, supply chain, manufacturing
- **Back Office**: Finance, HR, procurement
- **Insight Layer**: Analytics, planning, reporting (EPM)
- **Foundation**: Data, integration, security, compliance

### Governance-Led Sales Approach
Particularly effective for regulated industries and public companies:
- Lead with governance, risk, and compliance (GRC)
- Position ERP/EPM as enabler of audit-ready processes
- Use regulatory requirements as urgency drivers (TFRS/IFRS, PDPA, ESG)
- Connect with internal audit teams — they often champion system upgrades

```
Discovery          Problem           Solution          Demo /           Proof of

site visits,       presentation      with client

```

### Design Thinking Workshop as Sales Tool
Offer free 1-day workshops to qualified prospects:

**Workshop Agenda:**
- Morning: Empathize & Define (understand client's world, prioritize problems)
- Afternoon: Ideate & Prototype (co-create solution vision, sketch future state)
- Close: Present findings and proposed next steps

**Why This Works:**
- Positions you as thought leader, not vendor
- Creates emotional investment from client team
- Surfaces real needs that standard demos miss
- Produces deliverable (workshop report) that becomes your proposal foundation
- Differentiates from competitors who just do product demos

## 8. Key Deliverable Templates

### Executive Briefing Document (1-2 pages)
```
1. SITUATION: Where the client is today (current state, challenges)
2. COMPLICATION: Why change is needed now (market forces, risks, triggers)
3. RESOLUTION: What we propose (solution overview, outcomes, investment)
4. NEXT STEPS: Clear call to action with timeline
```

### Business Case Framework
```
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
2. CURRENT STATE ANALYSIS
   ├── Process Costs (quantified)
   ├── Inefficiency Impact
   └── Risk Exposure
3. PROPOSED SOLUTION
   ├── Solution Overview
   ├── Expected Benefits (quantified)
   └── Implementation Approach
4. FINANCIAL ANALYSIS
   ├── Total Investment
   ├── Benefits Over 5 Years
   ├── ROI / Payback Period
   └── Sensitivity Analysis
5. RISK ANALYSIS & MITIGATION
6. RECOMMENDATION
```

### Maturity Assessment Report
```
1. ASSESSMENT OVERVIEW (methodology, scope, participants)
2. MATURITY SCORECARD (visual radar chart across dimensions)
3. KEY FINDINGS (by area: Finance, SCM, HR, IT, Data)
4. INDUSTRY BENCHMARK (how client compares to peers)
5. RECOMMENDED ROADMAP (prioritized initiatives, timeline)
6. INVESTMENT ESTIMATE (order of magnitude for planning)
```
