### Enterprise IT Decision Characteristics
- **Budget Authority**: CIO/CFO for < 50M THB; CEO/Board for > 50M THB
- **Decision Timeline**: 4-12 months from first engagement to contract
- **Evaluation Style**: Structured RFP with scoring, POC/pilot common
- **Influencer Network**: External consultants (Big Four), peer companies, vendor references
- **Procurement Style**: Professional procurement team, negotiation-heavy

## 2. Conglomerate & MNC Decision-Making

### Thai Conglomerate Decision Pattern
```
FAMILY/OWNER LEVEL (ระดับเจ้าของ)
│ Strategic direction, major investment approval
│ Often influenced by: Trusted advisors, industry peers, global trends
│
├── GROUP CEO / PRESIDENT
│   │ Translates vision into mandate
│   │ Key relationship: Must believe in transformation vision
│   │
│   ├── BUSINESS UNIT CEO/MD
│   │   │ P&L owner, implementation sponsor
│   │   │ Key concern: Business disruption, ROI, timeline
│   │   │
│   │   ├── CFO — Budget holder, ROI gatekeeper
│   │   ├── CIO/CDO — Technology strategy, architecture fit
│   │   ├── COO — Process impact, operational continuity
│   │   └── CHRO — Change management, talent readiness
│   │
│   └── GROUP IT / SHARED SERVICES
│       │ Platform standardization, vendor management
│       │ Key role: Can block or enable group-wide deals
│       │
│       └── Enterprise Architecture Team — Technical gatekeepers
```

### MNC Decision Pattern
- **Local Decision**: Country GM/MD can approve within delegation of authority
- **Regional Decision**: APAC HQ (Singapore/HK/Tokyo) for larger investments
- **Global Decision**: HQ mandate — "our global ERP is SAP/Oracle" — local follows
- **Strategy**: Understand where the real decision is made. If global mandate,
  focus on local implementation excellence rather than product selection

### Key Insight: Dual-Track Engagement
For conglomerates, always engage at TWO levels:
1. **Group Level**: Strategic partnership, platform standardization, volume licensing
2. **BU Level**: Specific project needs, quick wins, proof of value

## 3. Sales Strategy & Account Planning

### Strategic Account Plan Template

```
ACCOUNT: [Company Name]
SEGMENT: Large Enterprise — [Conglomerate / MNC / Listed Company]

1. ACCOUNT OVERVIEW
   ├── Industry & Business Model
   ├── Revenue & Growth Trajectory
   ├── Strategic Priorities (from annual report, investor presentations)
   ├── Current IT Landscape (ERP, CRM, EPM, custom systems)
   └── Key Business Challenges

2. RELATIONSHIP MAP
   ├── Executive Sponsors (who champions us?)
   ├── Technical Champions (who validates our solution?)
   ├── Economic Buyers (who controls budget?)
   ├── Blockers (who might resist?)
   └── Coaches (who gives us inside intelligence?)

3. OPPORTUNITY IDENTIFICATION
   ├── Known Projects (in pipeline)
   ├── Latent Needs (pain points not yet projects)
   ├── Trigger Events (M&A, new CEO, regulatory change, IPO)
   └── Competitive Threats (incumbent vendor renewal, new entrant)

4. WIN STRATEGY
   ├── Value Proposition (tailored to this account)
   ├── Competitive Positioning (why us vs alternatives)
   ├── Proof Points (references, POC plan, case studies)
   └── Deal Structure (pricing approach, phasing, risk sharing)

5. ACTION PLAN
   ├── Next 30 Days: [specific actions]
   ├── Next 90 Days: [milestones]
   └── Next 12 Months: [strategic objectives]
```

## 4. Relationship Architecture

### Multi-Threading Strategy
Never depend on a single relationship. Build connections across:

```
OUR TEAM                          CLIENT TEAM
─────────                         ───────────
Managing Director    ←──────→     CEO/MD
Sales Director       ←──────→     CFO/CIO
Solution Architect   ←──────→     IT Director/Enterprise Architect
Project Manager      ←──────→     Business Process Owner
Account Manager      ←──────→     Procurement Manager
Delivery Lead        ←──────→     Project Sponsor
```

### Relationship Building Activities for Thai Large Enterprise

**Formal Channels:**
- Executive briefings at your office or theirs
- Industry roundtables (invite multiple prospects, thought leadership)
- Design thinking workshops (free, value-added, positions you as advisor)
- Benchmarking studies (compare client to industry peers)
- Technology days / Innovation showcases
- Joint conference presentations (position client as industry leader)

**Informal Channels (Thai Context):**
- Golf (still important for senior executives in Thailand)
- Dinner / social events (relationship deepening, not deal-making)
- Industry association events (สภาอุตสาหกรรม, หอการค้า)
- Alumni networks (same university bonds are strong in Thailand)
- Charity / CSR events (shows shared values)

### Trust Building Progression
```
Transaction → Consultation → Partnership → Strategic Alliance
    │              │              │              │
  "Vendor"    "Advisor"      "Partner"     "Extension of
   status      status         status       their team"
    │              │              │              │
  Price-based   Value-based   Outcome-based  Innovation-based
  selling       selling       selling        co-creation
```

## 5. Deal Structuring & Negotiation

### Enterprise Deal Components
```
TOTAL CONTRACT VALUE (TCV)
├── SOFTWARE / LICENSE
│   ├── SaaS Subscription (annual recurring)
│   ├── On-premise License (perpetual + maintenance)
│   └── Cloud Infrastructure (IaaS/PaaS consumption)
│
├── IMPLEMENTATION SERVICES
│   ├── Consulting & Advisory
│   ├── Configuration & Development
│   ├── Data Migration
│   ├── Integration
│   ├── Testing (SIT, UAT, performance)
│   └── Training & Change Management
│
├── MANAGED SERVICES / AMS
│   ├── Application Support (L1/L2/L3)
│   ├── Enhancement & Optimization
│   └── Managed Cloud Operations
│
└── OPTIONAL VALUE-ADD
    ├── Innovation Lab / POC
    ├── Process Mining / Assessment
    └── Digital Transformation Advisory
```

### Negotiation Strategy for Thai Large Enterprise

**Preparation (80% of negotiation success):**
- Know your BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)
- Understand client's budget constraints and approval process
- Prepare concession strategy — what can you trade vs. give away?
- Identify non-monetary value items (extended warranty, free training, etc.)

**Thai Negotiation Cultural Rules:**
- First meeting is NOT for hard negotiation — build rapport
- Thai executives negotiate firmly but with politeness (สุภาพแต่หนักแน่น)
- Procurement teams will push hard on price — expect 15-30% negotiation
- Build in negotiation room in initial pricing (but not so obvious)
- Final negotiations often happen executive-to-executive
- Written agreement culture is formal — everything must be in contract

**Win-Win Deal Structures:**
- **Phased Implementation**: Reduce Year 1 cost, spread investment
- **Success-Based Pricing**: Tie portion of fee to measurable outcomes
- **Volume Commitment**: Discount for multi-year or multi-BU commitment
- **Innovation Credits**: Include free advisory days for future planning
- **Reference Agreement**: Discount in exchange for being a reference client

## 6. Competitive Displacement Strategy

### Replacing Incumbent ERP
The hardest sale is displacing an existing ERP. Strategy:

**Step 1: Don't attack the incumbent directly**
- Instead, identify gaps the incumbent can't fill (new regulations, cloud, analytics)
- Position as "complementary" initially — "We help you get more from your investment"

**Step 2: Land with a wedge**
- Start with a module or function the incumbent doesn't cover well
- EPM/Planning is often the best wedge (analytics on top of any ERP)
- Cloud HR or Procurement can also be effective entry points

**Step 3: Expand from strength**
- Deliver exceptional results on the wedge
- Build internal advocates through user satisfaction
- Wait for the natural replacement cycle (ERP refresh every 7-12 years)

**Step 4: Full platform proposal**
- When client is ready, present comprehensive transformation business case
- Leverage existing relationship and proven delivery track record
