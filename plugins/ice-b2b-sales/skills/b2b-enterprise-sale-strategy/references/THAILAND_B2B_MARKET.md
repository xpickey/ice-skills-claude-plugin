## 1. Thailand Enterprise IT Market Overview

### Market Size & Growth
- Thailand enterprise IT spending: ~200-250B THB annually (growing 5-8% YoY)
- ERP market: ~15-20B THB (cloud ERP growing 20%+ YoY)
- Cloud adoption accelerating post-COVID: 60%+ of new ERP projects are cloud-first
- Government IT budget: ~30-40B THB annually (significant portion for digital government)
- Key growth drivers: Thailand 4.0, EEC, digital government, PDPA compliance, ESG reporting

### Market Maturity Segments
```
HIGHLY MATURE (5-10% of market, but 40%+ of spend):
├── TOP 50 SET-listed companies
├── Major banks and financial institutions
├── Large MNCs with regional HQ in Thailand
└── Already on Oracle/SAP/MS — upgrade/expansion opportunities

MODERATELY MATURE (15-20% of market):
├── SET-listed mid-cap companies
├── Large Thai private companies (1B+ THB revenue)
├── Regional MNC operations
└── Moving from on-premise to cloud — migration opportunities

EMERGING (30-40% of market):
├── SME+ companies (100M-1B THB revenue)
├── Fast-growing businesses
├── Family businesses in succession
└── First-time ERP — greenfield opportunities

EARLY STAGE (30-40% of market):
├── Traditional SMEs
├── Excel/paper-based operations
└── Basic accounting software (Express, SAP B1, local packages)
```

## 2. Thai Business Culture in B2B Sales

### Core Cultural Values That Impact Sales

**เกรงใจ (Kreng-jai) — Consideration / Reluctance to impose**
- Thai decision-makers rarely say "no" directly
- "Let me think about it" often means "no" — read the signals
- Don't push too hard in meetings — follow up gently afterward
- Give face-saving options when presenting alternatives

**สัมพันธภาพ (Relationships First)**
- Trust before transaction — always invest time in relationship building
- Personal connection with the owner/MD is often more important than the proposal
- Referrals from trusted connections carry enormous weight
- Long lunches, dinners, and social time are business investments, not wasted time

**ลำดับชั้น (Hierarchy & Respect)**
- Always address senior people with proper titles (คุณ, ท่าน, Dr., etc.)
- In meetings, the most senior person speaks last and decides
- Don't contradict or correct senior people in public — do it privately
- Bring your senior person to match their senior person (level matching)

**หน้า (Face / Reputation)**
- Never cause embarrassment in front of others
- If a client made a bad technology choice before, don't criticize it — help them evolve
- Public praise builds loyalty — recognize client achievements openly
- Losing face can permanently damage a relationship

**สบายๆ (Sabai-Sabai — Easygoing)**
- Meetings may start late — be patient, never show frustration
- Small talk before business is expected and important
- Don't be overly aggressive or "hard sell" — Thai buyers react negatively
- Humor and warmth are valued in business relationships

### Communication Preferences
| Channel | Usage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Line** | Primary business messaging | Used by 90%+ of Thai professionals. Groups for projects |
| **Email** | Formal communication | For proposals, contracts, official correspondence |
| **Phone** | Quick decisions, urgent matters | Preferred by older executives |
| **Face-to-Face** | Relationship building, key decisions | Still the gold standard for important discussions |
| **Zoom/Teams** | Post-COVID standard for routine meetings | Acceptable but not preferred for first meetings |
| **LinkedIn** | Professional networking | Growing but still secondary to personal introductions |

## 3. Key Industry Verticals in Thailand

### Manufacturing (อุตสาหกรรมการผลิต)
- **Size**: Largest vertical for ERP — 30%+ of enterprise IT spend
- **Concentration**: Eastern Seaboard (EEC), Rayong, Chonburi, Samut Prakan, Pathum Thani
- **Sub-sectors**: Automotive, Electronics, Food & Beverage, Petrochemicals, Textiles
- **ERP Drivers**: Supply chain optimization, quality management, Industry 4.0, ESG
- **Dominant Vendors**: SAP (automotive/chemicals), Oracle (large process), Infor (discrete)

### Financial Services (บริการทางการเงิน)
- **Size**: Second largest — high IT spend per company
- **Players**: 15 commercial banks, 20+ insurance companies, 30+ securities firms, 50+ fintechs
- **Regulators**: BOT (Bank of Thailand), SEC, OIC
- **ERP Drivers**: IFRS9/TFRS9, regulatory reporting, digital banking, PDPA
- **Dominant Vendors**: Oracle (core banking adjacent), SAP (some banks), Salesforce CRM

### Retail & Consumer (ค้าปลีกและสินค้าอุปโภคบริโภค)
- **Players**: Central, The Mall, CP ALL, Lotus's, Big C, Home Product Center
- **ERP Drivers**: Omnichannel, inventory optimization, customer analytics, loyalty
- **Dominant Vendors**: Oracle (large retailers), SAP, NetSuite (fast fashion, D2C)

### Energy & Utilities (พลังงานและสาธารณูปโภค)
- **Players**: PTT Group, EGAT, GULF, Banpu, B.Grimm, BCPG
- **ERP Drivers**: Asset management, project accounting, ESG/carbon, regulatory
- **Dominant Vendors**: SAP (oil & gas), Oracle (utilities), industry-specific

### Real Estate & Construction (อสังหาริมทรัพย์และก่อสร้าง)
- **Players**: Land & Houses, AP, Sansiri, SC Asset, Supalai, Quality Houses
- **ERP Drivers**: Project management, cost control, cash flow, multi-entity
- **Dominant Vendors**: Oracle, SAP, MS Dynamics (mid-market developers)

### Healthcare (สาธารณสุข)
- **Players**: BDMS, Bumrungrad, Thonburi, Princ, Chularat
- **ERP Drivers**: Financial consolidation, procurement, supply chain, compliance
- **Dominant Vendors**: Oracle, SAP (some groups), industry-specific HIS systems

## 8. Practical Sales Etiquette in Thailand

### Meeting Protocol
- **First Meeting**: Business card exchange (give/receive with both hands), small talk 10-15 min, brief company introduction, understand their world — NO hard selling
- **Follow-up**: Send Line request (not just email), thank-you message within 24 hours
- **Dress Code**: Business formal for first meetings (even in casual companies)
- **Language**: Have Thai-speaking team members; English for MNCs/sophisticated clients
- **Gift Giving**: Small branded gifts are fine; avoid lavish gifts (anti-bribery)

### Building Long-Term Client Relationships
- Remember personal details (family, hobbies, birthdays)
- Congratulate on company milestones and achievements
- Share useful industry insights without being asked
- Be available when they need help — even outside business hours
- Introduction to your network — connect them with valuable contacts
- Annual relationship review — not just sales pipeline review

### Sales Team Structure for Thailand Market
```
ACCOUNT TEAM (ideal for large enterprise pursuit)
├── Account Director — Senior relationship owner, executive access
├── Solution Architect — Technical credibility, demo excellence
├── Industry Expert — Speaks the client's business language
├── Delivery Lead — Proves implementation capability
└── Pre-Sales Support — Proposal writing, pricing, admin

SALES TEAM (for normal enterprise velocity)
├── Sales Manager — 6-10 active deals, relationship + closing
├── Pre-Sales Consultant — Demo, technical validation, proposal
└── Inside Sales / SDR — Lead generation, qualification, scheduling
```
