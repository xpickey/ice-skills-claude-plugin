## 1. Thailand Government Procurement Landscape

### Regulatory Framework
- **พ.ร.บ.การจัดซื้อจัดจ้างและการบริหารพัสดุภาครัฐ พ.ศ. 2560** (Government Procurement Act 2017)
- **ระเบียบกระทรวงการคลังว่าด้วยการจัดซื้อจัดจ้าง พ.ศ. 2560** (MoF Procurement Regulation)
- **e-GP System** (ระบบจัดซื้อจัดจ้างภาครัฐด้วยอิเล็กทรอนิกส์) — mandatory electronic procurement
- **EGA/DGA Guidelines** for IT procurement — Digital Government Agency standards

### Procurement Methods
| Method | Thai Name | Threshold | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **e-Bidding** | วิธีประกวดราคาอิเล็กทรอนิกส์ | > 500K THB | Standard for IT projects |
| **e-Market** | วิธีตลาดอิเล็กทรอนิกส์ | Commodity items | Standardized products |
| **Specific Method** | วิธีเฉพาะเจาะจง | ≤ 500K THB or sole source | Urgent, specialized, or low-value |
| **Selection** | วิธีคัดเลือก | Complex, specialized | High-complexity consulting/IT |

### Government IT Budget Cycle
```
OCT-DEC: Budget planning for next fiscal year (ทำแผนงบประมาณ)
JAN-MAR: Budget defense at สำนักงบประมาณ (Bureau of the Budget)
APR-JUN: Parliamentary approval process
JUL-SEP: Final budget allocation — CRITICAL procurement window
OCT onwards: New fiscal year begins — spending starts
```

**Strategic Insight**: Engage government clients 12-18 months before their procurement window.
Budget insertion (การบรรจุงบประมาณ) happens in OCT-DEC for the following fiscal year.

## 2. Key Stakeholders & Decision-Making

### Government Decision Hierarchy
```
Minister / รัฐมนตรี
├── Permanent Secretary / ปลัดกระทรวง — Ultimate procurement authority
├── Deputy Permanent Secretary / รองปลัด — Day-to-day oversight
│   ├── Director General / อธิบดี — Department-level authority
│   │   ├── Deputy DG / รองอธิบดี — Project sponsor
│   │   │   ├── Division Director / ผอ.กอง — Procurement committee
│   │   │   │   ├── Section Chief — TOR writer, specs owner
│   │   │   │   └── IT Staff — Technical evaluation
│   │   │   └── Procurement Division / กองพัสดุ — Process compliance
│   │   └── CIO / ผู้บริหารเทคโนโลยีสารสนเทศระดับสูง — IT strategic direction

Special Committees:
├── TOR Committee (คณะกรรมการจัดทำ TOR) — Writes requirements
├── Evaluation Committee (คณะกรรมการพิจารณาผล) — Scores proposals
└── Inspection Committee (คณะกรรมการตรวจรับ) — Accepts deliverables
```

### Influence Map Strategy
- **Formal Power**: Permanent Secretary, Director General — final approval
- **Technical Influence**: CIO, IT Director — shapes TOR requirements
- **Budget Influence**: Finance Division, Bureau of the Budget liaison
- **Political Influence**: Minister's Secretary, Policy Advisors
- **External Influence**: Consulting firms doing master plans, DGA/ETDA advisors

**Key Principle**: In government, the person who writes the TOR often determines the winner.
Build relationships with TOR committee members 6-12 months before procurement.

## 3. Sales Strategy & Playbook

### Phase 1: Market Intelligence (6-18 months before procurement)

**Actions:**
- Monitor government master plans (แผนแม่บท), digital government plans, ministry strategies
- Track budget allocations via สำนักงบประมาณ annual reports
- Identify upcoming IT projects through DGA digital government plan
- Build relationships with consulting firms doing government master plans
- Attend government technology events (Digital Government Summit, etc.)

### Phase 2: Need Shaping (6-12 months before)

**Actions:**
- Offer **free knowledge-sharing sessions** (สัมมนาให้ความรู้) to build credibility
- Provide **study visit** opportunities to reference sites
- Propose **feasibility study** (ศึกษาความเป็นไปได้) — positions you as expert
- Help client understand solution landscape — educate before they write TOR
- Publish thought leadership: whitepapers, case studies from similar agencies

**Critical Rule**: Never write the TOR for the client. Instead, educate them so thoroughly
that the TOR naturally aligns with your solution's strengths.

### Phase 3: Pre-Bidding Preparation (1-3 months before)

**Actions:**
- Monitor e-GP system daily for procurement announcements
- Analyze TOR requirements against your solution capabilities
- Identify mandatory vs. desirable requirements — assess win probability
- Form consortium/JV if needed (local partner requirement is common)
- Prepare all required documents: company registration, financial statements, references

### Phase 4: Bidding & Proposal Submission

**Government Proposal Structure (Thai Standard)**
```
ส่วนที่ 1: ข้อเสนอด้านเทคนิค (Technical Proposal)
├── 1.1 ความเข้าใจขอบเขตงาน (Understanding of TOR)
├── 1.2 แนวทางและวิธีการดำเนินงาน (Methodology & Approach)
├── 1.3 แผนการดำเนินงาน (Project Plan & Timeline)
├── 1.4 ทีมงาน (Team Composition & CVs)
├── 1.5 ประสบการณ์ (Relevant Experience & References)
└── 1.6 เอกสารเพิ่มเติม (Additional Documents)

ส่วนที่ 2: ข้อเสนอด้านราคา (Price Proposal)
├── 2.1 ราคารวม (Total Price — sealed envelope)
├── 2.2 รายละเอียดค่าใช้จ่าย (Cost Breakdown)
└── 2.3 เงื่อนไขการชำระเงิน (Payment Terms)
```

### Phase 5: Post-Award & Delivery

**Government Project Success Factors:**
- Strict adherence to TOR scope and timeline
- Regular progress reports to steering committee
- Documentation in Thai language — critical for inspection committee
- Change management within government bureaucracy
- Knowledge transfer and sustainability planning

## 4. Relationship Building in Public Sector

### Do's
- Build long-term relationships (ความสัมพันธ์ระยะยาว) — government officials move between agencies
- Offer genuine value through knowledge sharing, not just selling
- Respect protocol and hierarchy (ลำดับชั้น) in all interactions
- Be patient — government decisions take time due to process requirements
- Provide excellent post-sale service — reputation is everything in government circles

### Don'ts
- Never attempt to influence procurement outcomes improperly
- Never bypass procurement procedures — compliance is non-negotiable
- Don't promise what you can't deliver — government contracts have legal penalties
- Don't underestimate the importance of proper Thai documentation
- Never make officials feel pressured or lose face (เสียหน้า)

## 5. Pricing Strategy for Government

### Price Scoring Models
Most government IT procurements use one of these scoring methods:

**Method 1: Price-Performance (ราคาประเมินต่ำสุด)**
- Technical score must pass minimum threshold (usually 80%)
- Lowest price among qualified bidders wins
- Strategy: Optimize technical score while being price-competitive

**Method 2: Weighted Score (คะแนนรวม)**
- Technical: 60-80% weight | Price: 20-40% weight
- Strategy: Maximize technical differentiation, competitive pricing

**Pricing Intelligence:**
- Government price benchmarks are often based on previous similar projects
- สำนักงานการตรวจเงินแผ่นดิน (Auditor General) reviews pricing reasonableness
- Include all costs transparently — hidden costs damage trust
- Consider multi-year maintenance pricing carefully — this is recurring revenue

## 6. SOE (State-Owned Enterprise) Strategy

SOEs (รัฐวิสาหกิจ) like PTT, EGAT, TOT, CAT, Thai Airways follow slightly different rules:

**Key Differences from Regular Government:**
- More autonomous procurement authority
- May use private sector-like evaluation methods
- Often have their own IT subsidiaries (e.g., PTT Digital)
- Budget comes from own revenue, not government allocation
- Decision-making can be faster but still bureaucratic
- Board approval required for large investments

**SOE Engagement Strategy:**
- Treat more like large enterprise but with government compliance overlay
- Build relationships at both management and board levels
- Understand each SOE's digital transformation roadmap
- Partner with their IT subsidiaries rather than competing with them

## 7. Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Impact | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Late engagement | TOR favors competitor | Start 12-18 months early |
| Price-only competition | Margin erosion | Shape TOR for value-based evaluation |
| Document non-compliance | Automatic disqualification | Checklist every TOR requirement |
| Over-promising in proposal | Delivery failure, blacklisting | Be honest about capabilities |
| Ignoring local partner need | Can't bid | Identify JV partners early |
| Poor Thai documentation | Low technical scores | Invest in professional Thai writing |
| Not understanding fiscal year | Miss budget window | Align sales cycle to Oct-Sep fiscal year |
