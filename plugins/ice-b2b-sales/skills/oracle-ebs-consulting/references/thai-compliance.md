## PART 10: Thai Regulatory & Localization

### Thai Financial Reporting

**Thai Accounting Standards**:
- **TFRS Alignment**: Thai Financial Reporting Standards based on IFRS
- **Thai GAAP**: Thai Generally Accepted Accounting Principles
- **Dual Reporting**: Thai GAAP for statutory, IFRS for group consolidation
- **Chart of Accounts**: Design to support both Thai GAAP and IFRS reporting
- **Financial Statements**: Balance sheet, income statement, cash flow, notes to accounts

**Statutory Reporting**:
- **Revenue Department**: Corporate income tax filing (Por Ngor Dor 50)
- **SET (Stock Exchange of Thailand)**: Quarterly, annual financial statements
- **DBD (Department of Business Development)**: Annual registration, financial statements
- **MOC (Ministry of Commerce)**: Business registration, annual filing

### Thai Tax Compliance

**Value Added Tax (VAT)**:
- **VAT Rate**: 7% (standard rate), 0% (export), exempt (specific goods/services)
- **VAT Configuration**: Tax codes, tax recovery, tax posting, tax reconciliation
- **E-Tax Invoice**: Electronic tax invoice submission to Revenue Department
- **VAT Returns**: Por Por 30 (monthly), Por Por 36 (annual), e-Filing
- **Input VAT vs Output VAT**: Reconciliation, VAT refund claims

**Withholding Tax (WHT)**:
- **WHT Types**: Income from services (3%), rent (5%), transport (1%), advertising (2%)
- **WHT Calculation**: Automatic calculation based on supplier tax profile
- **Por Ngor Dor Certificates**: WHT certificate generation (Por Ngor Dor 1, 3, 53)
- **WHT Filing**: Por Ngor Dor 1 Kor (monthly filing), annual reconciliation

**Corporate Income Tax**:
- **Tax Rate**: 20% (standard rate), BOI entities may have 0% or reduced rate
- **Transfer Pricing**: Related party transactions, TP documentation, advance pricing agreement
- **Tax Planning**: Tax incentives, BOI privileges, investment promotion
- **Tax Audit**: Revenue Department audit, transfer pricing audit, documentation requirements

### Thai Payroll & HR Compliance

**Social Security**:
- **Employee Contribution**: 5% of salary (max THB 750/month)
- **Employer Contribution**: 5% of salary (max THB 750/month)
- **Wage Ceiling**: THB 15,000/month for contribution calculation
- **Social Security Fund**: Electronic filing, payment via bank, contribution reconciliation

**Provident Fund**:
- **Employee Contribution**: Voluntary, typically 2-15% of salary
- **Employer Contribution**: Matching or higher, vesting schedule, portability
- **Fund Selection**: Employee choice, fund performance, fund management
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Provident Fund Act, annual submission to MOL

**Personal Income Tax**:
- **Progressive Tax Rates**: 0% (0-150K), 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, 30%, 35%
- **Allowances**: Personal allowance (60K), spouse (60K), children (30K each)
- **Deductions**: Provident fund, life insurance, mortgage interest, social security
- **Withholding**: Monthly withholding, annual reconciliation, Por Ngor Dor 91

**Labor Law Compliance**:
- **Minimum Wage**: Provincial minimum wage, Bangkok vs. provinces
- **Working Hours**: 8 hours/day, 48 hours/week, overtime premium (1.5x, 3x for holidays)
- **Leave Entitlements**: Annual leave (6 days minimum), sick leave, maternity leave (90 days)
- **Severance Pay**: Based on years of service, termination without cause, statutory formula
- **Workmen's Compensation**: Employer insurance, accident claims, MOL filing

### Thai Banking & Payments

**Bank Integration**:
- **Thai Bank Formats**: Kasikorn, SCB, Bangkok Bank, Krung Thai, payment formats
- **PromptPay**: QR code payments, phone number/national ID linkage, instant transfer
- **Bank Statement Import**: Thai bank statement formats (CSV, MT940, proprietary)
- **Payment Methods**: Check, bank transfer, PromptPay, credit card, petty cash

**Foreign Exchange**:
- **Bank of Thailand**: Exchange rate reference, FX controls, reporting requirements
- **Forward Contracts**: Hedge FX risk, forward rate, settlement at maturity
- **FX Gain/Loss**: Realized gain/loss on settlement, unrealized gain/loss on revaluation
