# Competitor Objection Bank — Enterprise Software (Thai Market)

## How to use this skill

1. Identify the **competitor** named in the deal (or implied in the objection)
2. Find the **objection pattern** in the matching section
3. Use the **anchor response** — but ALWAYS adapt to the customer's specific WHY (don't recite verbatim)
4. Apply the **trap-avoidance** rules
5. If the objection comes from procurement, **redirect to economic buyer** before responding
6. **Re-anchor on WHY** if the objection is price-focused (price obj = unfound value)

## A. SAP RISE / S/4HANA vs Oracle Fusion Cloud ERP

| # | Objection (we hear this) | Root cause | Anchor response | Trap to avoid |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | "SAP is the manufacturing standard — Oracle is finance-led" | Industry-bias from SAP install base | Fusion SCM Cloud has closed mfg gap; Modern Best Practice content includes process + discrete + project mfg; Cite TH/APAC mfg references | Don't disparage SAP mfg outright — concede their heritage, then differentiate on cloud UX + AI + lower TCO |
| 2 | "Our SAP team won't accept Oracle" | Sponsor / champion misalignment | Engage IT sponsor early; offer parallel architecture review; hire-from-Oracle services partner already in TH | Don't attack their team — frame as "augment, not replace" |
| 3 | "SAP RISE bundles infra + apps in one contract" | Single-throat-to-choke value | Oracle has Cloud@Customer + Universal Credits + Customer 2 Cloud — same single-vendor story; cite global SLA bundling | Don't deny RISE's commercial elegance — acknowledge then re-frame |
| 4 | "Migration risk from ECC6 to Fusion is too high vs S/4HANA Brownfield" | Risk-aversion | Brownfield carries hidden technical debt (ABAP, custom code retained); Fusion Greenfield = clean slate; offer joint risk workshop with TH-ref SI | Don't claim no-risk — every migration has risk; differentiate on which risk is more controllable |
| 5 | "We've invested in SAC for analytics" | Sunk cost + tooling lock-in | Fusion + FAW gives prebuilt KPIs; OAC works with non-Oracle data; show co-existence path; SAC may stay for SAP-specific | Don't tell them to throw out SAC — propose phased decommission tied to value |

## B. SAP B1 / GROW vs NetSuite (TH SMB / mid-market)

| # | Objection | Root cause | Anchor response | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | "B1 partners are everywhere in TH — better support" | TH ecosystem density | Acknowledge — then position Oracle's NetSuite Solution Provider channel growing; cite specific TH partners (e.g. NTT Data, Bluebik, MFEC); offer success commitment | Don't claim NetSuite has bigger partner base in TH SMB — it doesn't (yet) |
| 7 | "B1 is cheaper than NetSuite at Year 1" | Year-1 lens, hides TCO | Build 3-year TCO including: B1 add-ons (manufacturing, retail, e-commerce), partner add-on maintenance, infra; NetSuite TCO often LOWER over 3y | Don't focus on license alone — compare full stack, including SaaS upgrades automatic in NetSuite |
| 8 | "NetSuite caps at 100 employees / it's too small" | Confusion (B1 caps at ~100; NetSuite scales to $1B+) | Show NetSuite scale references — Oracle, Sephora, Goldman Sachs (subsidiaries), TH SET-listed groups using NetSuite OneWorld | Don't laugh at this — many TH buyers genuinely confuse B1 and NetSuite scale claims |
| 9 | "GROW is the future, B1 is for now, you're somewhere in between" | SAP roadmap narrative | NetSuite IS the cloud-native ERP — already there; SAP is fragmenting between B1 / GROW / RISE; offer NetSuite-to-Fusion roadmap as customer scales | Don't bash SAP's product fragmentation publicly — cite competitor analyst reports (Gartner / Forrester) |

## C. Microsoft Dynamics 365 (F&O / BC) vs Oracle / NetSuite / SAP

| # | Objection | Root cause | Anchor response | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10 | "We're a Microsoft shop — D365 integrates with M365 / Teams / Power Platform natively" | Ecosystem stickiness | Concede strong M365 integration; cite Oracle/NetSuite/SAP integrations to M365 (Outlook calendar, Excel edit, Teams app); differentiate on functional depth | Don't dismiss MS ecosystem — it's real; counter on functional depth + analytics + multi-GAAP |
| 11 | "Power BI / Power Platform / Copilot is included" | Cost-bundle perception | Comparable: Oracle FAW + Digital Assistant; NetSuite Analytics Warehouse; SAP SAC; usually customer overestimates Copilot utility today | Don't oversell our AI vs Copilot — cite specific use cases not slogans |
| 12 | "Total MS spend gets us a discount across D365 + Azure + M365" | EA cross-product bundling | Frame on workload-fit, not bundle math; if MS bundle is the only argument, customer may be buying suboptimal apps | Don't try to out-bundle Microsoft on Microsoft's home turf — fight on functional + TCO |
| 13 | "D365 Manufacturing is now strong" | True — MS has invested | Concede MS mfg has improved; differentiate on industry depth (process mfg / OPM, lease accounting, multi-GAAP, project mfg); references in TH FMCG / pharma | Don't claim D365 mfg is weak — it's gotten better; argue on specific module depth |

## D. Workday Financials / HCM vs Oracle / SAP

| # | Objection | Root cause | Anchor response | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | "We're already on Workday HCM — Workday Financials is the natural extension" | Vendor consolidation logic | Concede HCM Workday strength; counter Workday Financials on: multi-GAAP / multi-entity depth, EPM consolidation, procurement, project mfg, industry breadth | Don't attack Workday HCM — argue scope of Financials |

| 16 | "Workday Adaptive Planning is enough EPM" | Tooling minimalism | Adaptive does FP&A planning; Oracle EPM does FP&A + Consolidation + Tax + Reconciliation + Master Data — full EPM suite needed for SET-listed / multi-GAAP | Don't deny Adaptive's elegance for FP&A only — attack on coverage |

## F. Odoo / Thai-grown SMB packages (FlowAccount, PEAK, Express, AutoCount, Business Plus)

| # | Objection | Root cause | Anchor response | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 19 | "Odoo is open-source — no vendor lock-in" | Open-source ideology | Open-source != free at enterprise scale; Odoo Enterprise has commercial license + partner cost; counter on enterprise audit trail, IFRS depth, scale | Don't dismiss open-source — many Thai SMBs are pragmatic; argue on enterprise readiness |
| 20 | "FlowAccount / PEAK is enough — they handle Thai compliance natively" | TH vertical strength | Concede TH SMB simplicity; counter on: scale (>50 users), multi-currency, multi-entity, IFRS, audit defensibility for SET listing, integration to bigger systems | Don't disparage Thai-grown packages — many sub-$5M companies are well-served by them |
| 21 | "Why pay USD subscription when we can pay Baht to local vendor?" | FX + budget risk | Validate concern; counter on: USD subscription locks rate (vs annual Baht inflation in TH packages), enterprise scale, multi-country expansion future | Don't ignore — it's real; address FX hedge in proposal |

## G. Status Quo / "We don't need to change"

| # | Objection | Root cause | Anchor response | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 22 | "Our current system works fine" | No urgency / weak Why Change | Re-discover Why Change with quantified pain: cost of close days, audit findings, growth blockers, talent attrition, competitor moves; if no real pain, walk away | Don't manufacture pain — fake urgency loses the deal AND the relationship |
| 23 | "We just upgraded last year — too soon to change" | Sunk cost + recent investment | Acknowledge investment; frame on capability gap that triggered the discovery; offer phased / additive approach, not rip-and-replace | Don't try to invalidate their recent investment — disrespectful |
| 24 | "We have other priorities this year" | Real budget conflict | Surface what those priorities are; reframe ERP as enabler of those priorities (e.g. M&A, growth, new market, ESG, AI) | Don't push when budget is genuinely allocated elsewhere — build pipeline for next FY |

## H. Internal alignment / Champion challenges

| # | Objection | Root cause | Anchor response | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 25 | "I (champion) like it but board / CEO doesn't see urgency" | Weak Why Now / Why Invest at exec level | Build exec briefing on Why Change quantified at board level (cost, risk, opportunity); offer joint exec session with our exec | Don't expect champion to convince board alone — multi-thread up |
| 26 | "Our IT team is too busy to take this on" | Capacity constraint (real or excuse) | Offer fully-managed implementation (SI + AMS); de-risk customer team load; phase project so internal IT involvement is gated | Don't dismiss as excuse — it's often real; capacity is a legitimate blocker |
| 27 | "Procurement won't approve the price" | Procurement squeeze (often after business agreed) | Re-anchor on value w/ business sponsor; offer trade ladder (multi-year, scope, reference); escalate if procurement is operating outside business mandate | Don't engage procurement on price-only without re-anchoring with business |

## I. Risk / proof concerns

| # | Objection | Root cause | Anchor response | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 28 | "Where are your TH references in our industry?" | Reference-driven risk-aversion | Provide 3 named TH references in same industry/size; if none, offer regional (APAC) or peer-industry; offer joint-risk POC | Don't fake or stretch references — TH market is small, customers WILL check |
| 29 | "What if Oracle/SAP/MS gets acquired or changes pricing?" | Long-term commitment fear | Provide pricing protection clause (3-year cap), MSA contractual assurances; cite vendor's track record | Don't dismiss — vendor risk is a legitimate enterprise concern |
| 30 | "Show us a working POC before we commit" | Credibility check (often valid) | Define POC success criteria explicitly; gate POC scope tightly; ensure POC has paid-for-deliverables clause to avoid free-consulting | Don't agree to free unbounded POC — set scope, timeline, success criteria, exit conditions |

## Universal trap-avoidance rules

1. **Concede before you counter** — never deny the customer's framing; acknowledge, then differentiate
2. **Re-anchor on WHY** when objection is price — price objections are nearly always disguised value gaps
3. **Multi-thread up** when champion-level objection — exec to exec
4. **Don't disparage competitor by name** — let analysts (Gartner / Forrester / IDC) do the comparison
5. **Always have 3 references ready** — TH market is reference-driven; without proof, you lose
6. **Trade for concession** — see negotiation-agent's trade ladder
7. **Walk away from manufactured urgency** — fake Why Now hurts trust
