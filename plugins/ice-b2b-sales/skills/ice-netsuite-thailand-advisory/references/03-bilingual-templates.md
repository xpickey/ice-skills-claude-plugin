## Executive summary template (bilingual)

### English version

```
Executive Summary — <Customer> NetSuite Implementation

The <Customer> board faces three converging pressures: <pressure 1>,
<pressure 2>, and <pressure 3>. Without action, the cost of inaction over
the next 24 months is estimated at <amount>.

This proposal recommends a phased NetSuite OneWorld implementation,
delivered in <X> months across <Y> Thai subsidiaries, with Thai
localization covering VAT, WHT, e-Tax, and BOI segregation.

The investment is <amount>. The expected return — quantified in Section 6 —
is <amount> in hard savings over 36 months plus strategic value the board
will recognize as the foundation for <future state>.

We recommend Option B, the SuiteSuccess-aligned phased rollout, as the
right balance of speed, risk, and value for <Customer>'s context.
```


## Why Change / Now / Invest / Us / Stay template

### English

```
Why Change
  <Customer> operates today on <current system>, which has reached the limits
  of its capacity for <specific limit>. <Specific business impact>.

Why Now
  <Trigger event in last 12-24 months>. <Why waiting is more expensive than acting>.

Why Invest in NetSuite
  Against <alternative 1> and <alternative 2>, NetSuite offers <specific
  differentiator that matters for this customer>.

Why Us (iCE)
  <Specific iCE capability — Thai depth / public sector / industry / named team>.

Why Stay (post go-live)
  <Roadmap alignment / ecosystem / AMS quality / talent availability>.
```

## Style notes for Thai writing

- Use ผู้วิจัย / เรา / ทีมงาน explicitly. Do not over-hedge with passive constructions.
- Avoid "ในยุคปัจจุบัน," "ปฏิเสธไม่ได้ว่า," "อย่างพิถีพิถัน" — these are AI-pattern signatures.
- Mix sentence length deliberately. A short Thai sentence followed by a longer explanatory sentence reads better than uniform-length prose.
- Numbers: use Arabic numerals for figures and percentages. Use Thai numerals only when reproducing official document numbering (e.g., มาตรา ๑ — but Section 1 of a proposal uses 1).
- Currency: write THB X,XXX,XXX for international audience and X,XXX,XXX บาท for Thai-primary audience. Pick one and stay consistent.

## When NOT to translate

Some terms should stay in English even in Thai-primary documents because translation reduces clarity:

- NetSuite-specific module names (SuiteScript, SuiteCloud, SDF, OneWorld).
- Standard acronyms (UAT, SLA, ROI, TCO, AMS, BOI).
- Tax framework names where the Thai is awkward (Pillar Two — write "Pillar Two (กฎภาษีขั้นต่ำระดับโลก)" on first mention, then "Pillar Two" thereafter).
