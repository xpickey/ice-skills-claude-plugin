# Reference 03 — Vendor Themes

This reference defines how to layer vendor accent colors on top of your chosen industry theme. The industry theme (Reference 02) is the **base layer** — 60% of your deck's visual identity. The vendor accent is the **spice layer** — a controlled 10% overlay that signals alignment with the vendor's product without imitating the vendor's marketing deck.

---

## How Vendor Accents Work

When pitching a vendor's product (Oracle, SAP, Microsoft, Salesforce, NetSuite, Workday), your deck originates from YOUR industry theme, not the vendor's corporate palette. The vendor's recognizable accent color appears sparingly in:

- CTA buttons and high-impact callouts
- Highlight bars on key slides (e.g. "Why Oracle?" slide)
- Accent rule between sections
- One or two hero imagery overlays
- Numerical stat callouts
- Border accents on comparison tables

**The 60-30-10 rule remains in effect:** Your industry primary stays dominant (60%), your industry secondary supports (30%), and the vendor accent is the spice (10% maximum).

**Why this matters:** Vendors protect their brand guidelines strictly. An Enterprise Service Provider (your role) uses a vendor accent to signal "we are deeply aligned with this product" — not to pretend to be the vendor or to copy their pitch-deck template verbatim. Overusing the vendor color reads as either inexperienced design or a misunderstanding of your role.

---

## When to Use Which Vendor Accent

| Scenario | Vendor | Decision Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Pitching Oracle Cloud (Fusion, EBS, Enterprise) | **Oracle** | Use Oracle red for high-stakes moments (business case, ROI, timeline) |
| Pitching SAP S/4HANA, RISE, or BTP | **SAP** | Use SAP blue for transformation moments (supply chain, finance, operations) |
| Pitching Dynamics 365, Power Platform, Azure | **Microsoft** | Use Microsoft blue sparsely; multi-product deals may use light four-color accents |
| Pitching Salesforce CRM or platform | **Salesforce** | Use Salesforce blue for customer-facing and revenue metrics |
| Pitching NetSuite (ERP, financials, planning) | **NetSuite** | Use NetSuite navy for finance, consolidation, and planning narratives |
| Pitching Workday (HR, Finance, Planning) | **Workday** | Use Workday orange for transformation and future-of-work moments |
| Multi-product deal (e.g. NetSuite + Salesforce) | **Hybrid** | See Section 4 below; select one primary accent OR use neutral industry theme |

## 1. Oracle (Cloud / Fusion / EBS / NetSuite Sub-Brand)

**Recognizable Accent:** Controlled Oracle red. Oracle's brand identity is built on restraint — the red appears in specific, high-contrast moments.

**Permitted Hex Color:** `#C74634`

**Color Discipline:**
- Use **only** for CTA buttons, high-stakes stat callouts, "Why Oracle?" headers, and ROI/business case highlights.
- Do **not** background entire slides with Oracle red. Never.
- Do **not** use Oracle red for body text or large text blocks.
- If pairing with an industry theme, the industry primary remains dominant. Oracle red is the accent detail.
- Maximum 2-3 instances per deck slide group (e.g. the vendor-advantage section).

**Typography Pairing:**
- EN headers: Keep your industry theme's header font (e.g. **Inter Tight** if you chose Trust Navy).
- Do **not** switch to Oracle's corporate serif font (which is proprietary).
- A subtle uppercase small-cap label ("ORACLE COMMITMENT" or "ORACLE-POWERED OUTCOME") in 11pt is acceptable once per section.
- TH: Continue with your industry theme's Thai headers.

**Visual Motif Notes:**
- Oracle's identity centers on sharp, geometric precision. If your industry theme permits, use Oracle red at sharp 90° corners (no rounded edges on Oracle-red accent blocks).
- Oracle's SaaS marketing often uses minimal photography. Keep photography secondary; let diagrams and business flows speak.
- If showing an Oracle product screen (e.g. Fusion payroll module), keep it small and contextual — never full-slide.

**Compliance Note:**
- Do **not** use Oracle's logo (the red oval "O") without explicit written confirmation from the customer that they are an existing Oracle partner or licensed customer.
- If the customer is a greenfield (new to Oracle), position the deck as "Built on Oracle Cloud" — descriptive, not branded.
- Oracle red is fair game; the logo is not.

## 2. SAP (S/4HANA / RISE / BTP)

**Recognizable Accent:** SAP blue. SAP's identity is built on technical trustworthiness and enterprise stability.

**Permitted Hex Color:** `#0070F2`

**Color Discipline:**
- Use **only** for transformation milestones, process-redesign moments, and "Why SAP?" positioning.
- Do **not** use SAP blue as the background for slide decks. It overwhelms the message.
- Pair with plenty of white or your industry secondary color. SAP blue is high-contrast; it must have breathing room.
- One accent bar per logical section (e.g. Finance Transformation, Supply Chain Redesign).
- Maximum 2 stat callouts or CTA buttons in SAP blue per deck section.

**Typography Pairing:**
- EN headers: Use your industry theme font. If your industry allows geometric sans-serifs (**Manrope**, **Inter**, **Prompt**), those pair naturally with SAP's modern product design.
- Do **not** use SAP's corporate font (UI Sans Web), which is proprietary.
- A subsection title like "SAP-Driven Process" in 12pt, regular weight, is fine.
- TH: If your customer is Thai, SAP decks in Thai often use **IBM Plex Sans Thai** for the technical credibility signal.

**Visual Motif Notes:**
- SAP's enterprise identity relies on process clarity — flowcharts, ERP module diagrams, and integration maps.
- Use SAP blue to highlight a critical process box or the "As-Is → To-Be" transformation arrow.
- If illustrating SAP S/4HANA modules (Finance, Procure-to-Pay, Order-to-Cash), keep each module icon in simple geometric style; do not copy SAP's design system icons exactly.
- SAP audiences (CFOs, COOs, Supply Chain leads) are detail-oriented and process-obsessed. Invest in clear architecture diagrams over glossy photography.

**Compliance Note:**
- Do **not** use SAP's logo (the blue oval with white letters) without explicit customer approval.
- SAP blue is a signature color and is acceptable; the logo requires partnership confirmation.
- If the customer is a new prospect, frame the deck as "SAP S/4HANA-native" or "Built for SAP" — technically accurate, not branded.

## 3. Microsoft (Dynamics 365 / Power Platform / Azure)

**Recognizable Accent:** Microsoft blue, used sparingly. Microsoft's identity is deliberately multi-colored, so a single blue accent can feel restrictive. Deploy it carefully.

**Permitted Hex Color:** `#0078D4`

**Color Discipline:**
- Use Microsoft blue **sparsely** — only for CTAs and high-impact stats.
- Do **not** use it as a dominant section background. Microsoft's own marketing uses the four-color motif; you are not Microsoft, so avoid that.
- If the deal involves multiple Microsoft products (Dynamics + Power Platform + Azure), you have two options:
  - (a) Stick to Microsoft blue alone and lean on your industry theme for visual variety.
  - (b) Escalate to Section 4 (Multi-Vendor Decks) and use your industry theme only, with a single subtle "Microsoft Ecosystem" label.
- One accent line or button per major section.

**Typography Pairing:**
- EN headers: Microsoft's corporate font is **Segoe UI** (proprietary). Use your industry theme's sans-serif instead (**Inter**, **Manrope**, **Poppins**, depending on industry).
- A label like "Microsoft-Powered Cloud Platform" in 11pt is fine; full headlines in Microsoft's font are not.
- TH: Microsoft Thai decks often use **IBM Plex Sans Thai** for consistency with Azure documentation.

**Visual Motif Notes:**
- Microsoft's brand language emphasizes interconnection, cloud, and "platform ecosystem." If your slide is about integrating Dynamics with Power Apps and Azure, use a simple connection diagram with Microsoft blue accent dots at the nodes.
- Do **not** copy Microsoft's fluent design language (rounded corners, soft shadows, pastel accents). Your industry theme's visual rules take precedence.
- If embedding a Power BI dashboard or screenshot, keep it small and contextual. Never full-slide.
- Microsoft audiences (IT directors, CIOs, enterprise architects) respond to clear architecture and integration stories. Photography is secondary.

**Compliance Note:**
- Do **not** use Microsoft's logo (the four-square emblem) without explicit customer confirmation.
- Microsoft blue is acceptable as an accent color; the logo requires partnership or distribution agreement proof.
- If the customer is not yet a Microsoft partner, position the deck as "Dynamics 365-native" or "Built on Microsoft Azure" — factual without over-claiming.

## 4. Salesforce (CRM / Platform / Sales Cloud)

**Recognizable Accent:** Salesforce blue, used with confidence. Salesforce's identity is customer-centric and energetic.

**Permitted Hex Color:** `#00A1E0`

**Color Discipline:**
- Use Salesforce blue for customer outcomes, revenue metrics, and "Why Salesforce?" positioning.
- Salesforce blue is slightly lighter than Microsoft blue and pairs well with white space; use it more freely than Oracle red.
- One accent bar or button per major message block is appropriate.
- CTA buttons, stat callouts, and key process-step highlights are natural homes for this color.
- Maximum 2-3 instances per deck section.

**Typography Pairing:**
- EN headers: Salesforce uses **Salesforce Sans** (proprietary). Use your industry theme's header font instead.
- A subsection title like "Salesforce Success Story" in 12pt regular is acceptable.
- Do **not** switch your headline or body font family to mimic Salesforce's identity.
- TH: Salesforce Thai decks typically use **IBM Plex Sans Thai** or **Sarabun** for accessibility.

**Visual Motif Notes:**
- Salesforce's brand centers on customer relationships and revenue. Use Salesforce blue for slides covering:
  - Sales pipeline visibility
  - Customer sentiment / NPS
  - Revenue forecasting
  - Customer success metrics
- Include one or two authentic customer moments or use-case callouts; Salesforce audiences expect social proof.
- If showing a Salesforce dashboard (Sales Cloud, Service Cloud), keep it small and annotated. Never use a full-slide screenshot as your entire message.
- Salesforce audiences (Sales VPs, customer success leaders, CMOs) are metric-driven and narrative-hungry. Pair your blue accent with clear storytelling.

**Compliance Note:**
- Do **not** use Salesforce's logo (the blue cloud with white text) without explicit customer permission.
- Salesforce blue is a signature color and is acceptable for accents; the logo requires partnership confirmation.
- Frame new deals as "Salesforce-enabled" or "Salesforce-native implementations" — accurate terminology without logo usage.

## 5. NetSuite (ERP / Financials / Planning)

**Recognizable Accent:** NetSuite navy. NetSuite is technically part of Oracle but maintains a distinct visual identity — darker, more conservative, finance-focused.

**Permitted Hex Color:** `#1A2647`

**Color Discipline:**
- NetSuite navy is very dark and reads almost black. Use it **carefully** and only in high-contrast scenarios.
- Ideal for: Finance transformation slides, consolidation messages, planning & visibility statements.
- Do **not** use NetSuite navy as a full slide background unless your industry theme background is very light (off-white or cream).
- One accent bar or button per financial process block.
- Pair with plenty of white. NetSuite navy needs breathing room.
- Avoid using NetSuite navy and Oracle red together in the same deck — they compete and feel chaotic.

**Typography Pairing:**
- EN headers: Use your industry theme's header font. Finance-focused themes often use serif headers (**Lora**, **Source Serif 4**) or measured sans-serifs (**Inter**, **Source Sans 3**).
- A subsection label like "NetSuite-Driven Close" in 11pt is appropriate.
- Do **not** adopt NetSuite's corporate font. NetSuite decks are often conservative; let your industry theme guide the tone.
- TH: Finance and consolidation audiences in Thailand appreciate **TH Sarabun PSK** or **IBM Plex Sans Thai** for credibility.

**Visual Motif Notes:**
- NetSuite's identity is built on financial precision, consolidation, and visibility across the enterprise.
- Use NetSuite navy to highlight:
  - Consolidation workflows
  - Multi-subsidiary reporting
  - Planning & forecasting moments
  - Close process improvements
- Include reference diagrams (subledger architecture, intercompany reconciliation) with NetSuite navy accent on critical nodes.
- NetSuite audiences (CFOs, controllers, accounting directors) are process auditors. Invest in detailed, well-labeled diagrams over trendy imagery.

**Compliance Note:**
- Do **not** use NetSuite's logo (the navy square with orange/white lettering) without explicit customer approval.
- NetSuite navy is appropriate as an accent color; the logo requires partnership confirmation.
- NetSuite is Oracle-owned but often positioned as a distinct cloud product. Use "NetSuite-powered" or "NetSuite-native" positioning for new prospects.

## 6. Workday (HR / Finance / Planning)

**Recognizable Accent:** Workday orange. Workday's brand is forward-looking, human-centric, and energetic.

**Permitted Hex Color:** `#F38B00`

**Color Discipline:**
- Workday orange is warm and approachable. It works well for transformation moments, future-of-work narratives, and employee experience highlights.
- Use Workday orange for: Organizational change, talent-driven outcomes, real-time insight, and innovation messaging.
- One accent bar, button, or stat callout per major narrative block.
- Pair with white or a light secondary color. Workday orange is vibrant and needs calm surroundings.
- Do **not** use Workday orange as a full-slide background unless your industry theme explicitly permits bright accents (e.g. Vibrant Coral or Signal Magenta).
- Maximum 3 instances per deck section.

**Typography Pairing:**
- EN headers: Use your industry theme's header font. Workday's own headers use sans-serifs with generous tracking; paired with **Inter**, **Manrope**, or **Prompt**, Workday orange feels natural.
- A subsection label like "Workday Future-of-Work Vision" in 12pt regular is appropriate.
- Do **not** switch to Workday's corporate font family. Maintain your industry theme's typography.
- TH: Workday Thai decks often use **Prompt** or **IBM Plex Sans Thai** for a modern, human tone.

**Visual Motif Notes:**
- Workday's brand centers on human experience, real-time insight, and organizational agility.
- Use Workday orange for slides covering:
  - Talent acquisition and development
  - Employee experience transformations
  - Real-time workforce analytics
  - Organizational change moments
- Include authentic people moments (diverse teams, candid work scenes) — Workday audiences expect humanity in the story.
- If showing a Workday interface (HCM dashboard, compensation planning, recruiting module), keep it contextual and small. Use it to prove a point, not as the main visual.
- Workday audiences (CHROs, CFOs, COOs, talent leaders) are change-oriented and outcomes-focused. Pair your orange accent with clear transformation narratives.

**Compliance Note:**
- Do **not** use Workday's logo (the orange square with white "wd") without explicit customer permission.
- Workday orange is a signature color and is acceptable for accents; the logo requires partnership confirmation.
- Frame new deals as "Workday-native" or "Workday-powered transformations" — accurate without branded logo usage.

## Multi-Vendor Decks (Hybrid Scenarios)

**When a deal involves two or more vendors (e.g. NetSuite + Salesforce, or Dynamics + Power Platform):**

### Option A: Choose One Primary Vendor Accent

- Identify the "hero" vendor (the one driving the largest transformation or ROI).
- Use that vendor's accent color throughout the deck.
- Reference the secondary vendor verbally and in text, not through color.
- Example: NetSuite + Salesforce deal where financials/planning is the primary transformation. Use NetSuite navy as the accent; mention Salesforce integration in text and diagrams.

**Pros:** Clean visual story, unambiguous hierarchy.
**Cons:** Secondary vendor feels less prominent.

### Option B: Use Neutral Industry Theme Only

- Deploy your industry theme (Reference 02) with **no** vendor accents.
- Let your industry primary color dominate.
- Dedicate one slide to "The Integrated Platform" showing both vendors' logos and how they connect.
- Reference each vendor by name and functionality, not by color.

**Pros:** Equally weighted; no vendor feels secondary.
**Cons:** Less visual signaling of vendor alignment; can feel generic.

### Option C: Accent Bars for Each Vendor (Advanced)

- Use a thin horizontal accent bar (1-2pt height) for each vendor's section.
- Vendor A slides get Vendor A's accent bar; Vendor B slides get Vendor B's accent bar.
- Ensure the bars are narrow and consistent in placement (e.g. top of each section header).
- This works only if the two vendors' accent colors are sufficiently distinct (e.g. NetSuite navy + Workday orange, or Oracle red + SAP blue).
- Do **not** attempt this if the colors are similar (Microsoft blue + Salesforce blue) — it looks indecisive.

**Pros:** Clear, visual navigation; honors both vendors equally.
**Cons:** Requires precise design discipline; can feel busy if poorly executed.

**Recommendation:** For most multi-vendor deals, **Option A** (one primary accent) is clearest. Option B works if neither vendor is clearly dominant. Option C is for experienced design teams with strong visual controls.

## What NOT to Do

### Logo Overuse
- **FORBIDDEN:** Using the vendor's logo as a slide background or repeating watermark.
- **FORBIDDEN:** Implying you are the vendor or that the vendor endorses this deck (unless you have written partnership approval).
- **ALLOWED:** One vendor logo on a title slide or partnership page (only with customer permission).

### Template Cloning
- **FORBIDDEN:** Copying the vendor's official pitch-deck template or layout grid.
- **FORBIDDEN:** Using the vendor's exact section structure (e.g. copying SAP's "Why SAP?" slide verbatim).
- **ALLOWED:** Adopting the vendor's logical flow (e.g. "Business Drivers → Why Product X → Implementation → Value") and making it your own.

### Font Copying
- **FORBIDDEN:** Switching to the vendor's corporate font family mid-deck (e.g. switching from your industry theme's **Sarabun** to SAP's proprietary serif).
- **FORBIDDEN:** Using the vendor's brand guidance for typography when it conflicts with your industry theme.
- **ALLOWED:** Using your industry theme's fonts consistently and adding a subtle vendor-specific label (e.g. "Oracle-Powered") in a small caps footnote.

### Color Dominance
- **FORBIDDEN:** Making the vendor's accent color your primary color (60% of slides).
- **FORBIDDEN:** Backgrounds, headlines, or body text in the vendor's color.
- **ALLOWED:** Accent bars, CTA buttons, stat callouts, and one or two hero overlays in the vendor color (10% maximum).

### Screenshot Overuse
- **FORBIDDEN:** Full-slide vendor product screenshots as your main visual content.
- **FORBIDDEN:** Unattributed or watermarked screenshots without context.
- **ALLOWED:** Small, annotated product interface callouts (2-3 per deck) that illustrate a specific point.
