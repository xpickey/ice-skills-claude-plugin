# Reference: Presentation & Proposal Structures

> Use this reference in **Authoring Mode**. Turns discovery + qualification + narrative into customer-facing artifacts.

---

## 1. The Apple-style B2B pitch template (short form)

Use when the artifact is a one-page pitch, a cold-email opener, or the first three slides of a deck.

```
Everything we do at [COMPANY], we believe that [BELIEF about industry / customer].

We believe the way [STATUS QUO] happens today is broken because [PROBLEM].

The way we challenge this is by [DIFFERENTIATING APPROACH #1, #2, #3].

We happen to [PRODUCT / SERVICE].

If you share our belief that [RESTATE BELIEF], let's talk about what is possible.
```

### Before / after example — Thai digital transformation firm

**Before (WHAT-first, weak):**
> "We are a leading Digital Transformation provider in Thailand. 15 years of experience, 200+ clients. Expertise across Oracle, SAP, Microsoft, Salesforce. Starting price THB X M. Interested to talk?"

**After (Apple-style, strong):**
> **[BELIEF]** "We believe Thai businesses are not inferior to global firms — we are simply constrained by outdated tools and advisors accustomed to yesterday's methods.
>
> **[CHALLENGE]** We believe that a transformation which takes 3 years and burns hundreds of millions is a dead model.
>
> **[HOW]** The way we challenge this is by:
> 1. Starting from business outcome, not from modules.
> 2. Using Fortune-500 Modern Best Practice as the starting point, not redesigning from scratch.
> 3. Measuring success by decision speed, not by go-live date.
>
> **[WHAT]** We happen to build this on Oracle Fusion Cloud and SAP Rise.
>
> **[INVITATION]** If you believe, as we do, that Thai enterprise is ready to compete globally — let's discuss what the next 12 months should look like."

## 2. The WHY-Driven 10-Section Proposal

Use when writing a formal proposal, board paper, or executive summary document. Each section has one job.

```
Section 1 — Executive Summary          → Shared belief (we believe; you believe)
Section 2 — Why Change                 → Current state, cost of status quo
Section 3 — Why Now                    → Window of opportunity, compound cost
Section 4 — Why Invest                 → 3-Lens business case (Financial / Strategic / Personal)
Section 5 — Why Us                     → 3 Dimensions (Outcomes / Beliefs / Risk mitigation)
Section 6 — Our Approach (HOW)         → Methodology that *proves* the WHY
Section 7 — What We Deliver (WHAT)     → Scope, deliverables, timeline
Section 8 — Why Stay                   → Long-term partnership, expansion path
Section 9 — Commercial                 → Investment, not cost
Section 10 — Call to Action            → Next step in the shared journey
```

### Section-by-section guidance

- **S1 Executive Summary** — open with a *shared belief* paragraph, not a company overview. Three sentences: the belief, the consequence of inaction, the invitation.
- **S2 Why Change** — one page. Named current-state pain, elevated to strategic/emotional.
- **S3 Why Now** — one page. Three converging forces (external + internal + compound cost).
- **S4 Why Invest** — 2–3 pages. All three lenses. One headline financial number, one strategic argument, one personal/organizational anchor.
- **S5 Why Us** — 2–3 pages. One reference customer per dimension (Outcomes / Beliefs / Risk).
- **S6 Our Approach** — methodology written as *proof* of the WHY. Each phase should include a "this is how we prove we believe what we said".
- **S7 What We Deliver** — this is the *last* place to list scope, not the first.
- **S8 Why Stay** — a 3-year roadmap + who stays on the account past go-live.
- **S9 Commercial** — title every pricing section "Investment", not "Cost".
- **S10 Call to Action** — one concrete next step (date, name, action), not a vague "let's stay in touch".

### Traditional vs. WHY-driven proposal — why the order matters

Traditional proposals open with a company overview, capabilities, then solution. They lose the executive before page 3 because the reader never knows *why they should care*. The WHY-driven order puts the reader's belief first, then earns the right to describe the solution.

## 3. 60-minute executive presentation flow

Each section maps to a MEDDPICC element, so the room is *qualifying itself while being persuaded*.

| Time | Section | Content | MEDDPICC hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0–3 min | Hook | Shared belief opener; one question the room should answer. | Identify Pain |
| 3–10 min | Why Change | Current state, named pain, emotional resonance. | Identify Pain, Metrics |
| 10–15 min | Why Now | Three converging forces (external + internal + compound cost). | Metrics (cost of inaction) |
| 15–25 min | Why Invest | 3-Lens business case; the headline financial number lives here. | Metrics, Economic Buyer |
| 25–40 min | Why Us | 3 dimensions (Outcomes / Beliefs / Risk), with one reference per dimension. | Competition, Champion |
| 40–50 min | How We Deliver | Methodology mapped to the Decision Criteria. | Decision Criteria, Decision Process |
| 50–55 min | Commercial & Paper | Investment framing; surface Paper Process requirements now, not at close. | Paper Process |
| 55–60 min | Call to Action | Concrete commitment — date, owner, next artifact. | Close the meeting |

### Presenter notes

- Do not put your company logo on every slide — it trains attention to the wrong thing.
- Anchor on one customer story per WHY, not three.
- Let the customer *speak their own WHY* in the Why Invest section. It lands twice as hard as yours.

## 4. The Executive Bridge Slide (one page, high leverage)

Put one slide in the deck — and paste it into the proposal executive summary — that combines MEDDPICC facts with the Why-Stack. It is the slide the EB quietly takes away and uses in their own internal conversation.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│              Our Shared Path Forward                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                     │
│   Why Change  → [current state pain]                │
│   Why Now     → [urgency in Q2]                     │
│   Why Invest  → [X THB in → Y THB return, Z months] │
│   Why Us      → [shared belief + track record]      │
│   Why Stay    → [3-year partnership vision]         │
│                                                     │
│   Success Metrics:   [M1, M2, M3]                   │
│   Decision by:       [Date]                         │
│   Sponsor (EB):      [Name, Title]                  │
│   Champion:          [Name, Title]                  │
│                                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Rules:
- One page, never two.
- No company logo — this is about *the customer's* decision.
- Names and dates, not placeholders. If you do not yet have them, the deal is not ready for this slide.

## 5. Seven tips for communicating WHY in B2B

1. **Open with "We believe…", not "We are…".** "We believe Thai business can lead Asia" outranks "We are Thailand's leading IT consultancy" — every time.
2. **Use a person's name, not an organization's.** "CFO Khun Somchai of ABC told us…" beats "ABC was successful".
3. **Tell stories that prove the WHY, not the capability.** "We walked away from a THB 50M project that didn't match our WHY" is stronger than "We deliver on time 95%."
4. **Make the customer *discover* the shared WHY, not be *told*.** Lead with questions; let them speak the belief first.
5. **Send WHY before the slides.** Three lines of shared belief in the email changes who walks in as believers vs. skeptics.
6. **Train the delivery team to speak WHY as well as the sales team.** Apple's Genius Bar staff sound like Apple. Your consultants should sound like your CEO.
7. **Measure presentation success by what the customer *remembers*.** If they recall specs and pricing — the presentation failed. If they recall the belief — you won.

## 6. Format-specific guidance

### Email (first outreach, 150 words max)
- Subject line is a WHY statement in six words or fewer.
- First sentence: shared belief.
- Second paragraph: two specific reasons now is the moment (Why Now).
- Third: one reference (Why Us).
- Close: a concrete next step (date, 20-minute call, named artifact).

### Demo script
- Open with Why Change and Why Now (do *not* open with a product tour).
- Every feature shown must answer a Why Invest or Why Us question — not the other way round.
- Close by asking the customer to articulate *their* shared belief back to you.

### QBR / EBR deck (post-sale)
- Open with "The belief we agreed on at kickoff". Revisit it.
- Show what was delivered as *evidence of WHY*, not only as scope completed.
- Close with Why Stay — the next phase, the next 12 months of shared journey.

### Board paper / investment memo
- Lead with Why Invest (the board's language).
- Why Change and Why Now fold into "context".
- Why Us becomes the "partner selection" section.
- Why Stay becomes the "long-term positioning" section.
