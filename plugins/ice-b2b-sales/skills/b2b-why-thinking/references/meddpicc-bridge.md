# Reference: WHY-to-MEDDPICC Bridge (Qualification Layer)

> Use this reference in **Qualification Mode** and **Deal Health Mode**. Consolidated from "WHY-to-MEDDPICC-Bridge-Framework.md".

---

## 1. The problem this bridge solves

- **Problem A — "Qualified but losing":** MEDDPICC says all 8 boxes are ticked, yet deals are price-squeezed or lost. Cause: no belief alignment.
- **Problem B — "Loved but stuck":** customer believes in us, but Paper Process, Decision Criteria, or Champion is weak. Cause: no qualification muscle.

**Fix:** put a WHY question behind every MEDDPICC letter, and add a 5-WHY narrative layer on top. Hard qualification + belief discovery = one scorecard.

---

## 2. MEDDPICC primer

| Letter | Element | Core question |
|---|---|---|
| M | Metrics | How will success be measured, and by whom? |
| E | Economic Buyer | Who signs the budget? |
| D | Decision Criteria | What formal criteria will rank the options? |
| D | Decision Process | What steps, stages, committees — in what order? |
| P | Paper Process | Legal, procurement, risk, compliance, audit gates. |
| I | Identify Pain | What hurts today — functionally, strategically, personally? |
| C | Champion | Who inside the customer sells *for* us? |
| C | Competition | Which alternatives are in the room (including "do nothing")? |

---

## 3. The WHY Bridge Map — a WHY question behind every letter

| Letter | WHY question to ask | Insight produced |
|---|---|---|
| **M — Metrics** | "Why does *this* number matter to *you* (not only to the CFO)?" | Separates vanity metrics from purpose-linked metrics. |
| **E — Economic Buyer** | "What personal legacy does the EB want to build from this initiative?" | Unlocks the emotional trigger that releases the budget. |
| **D — Decision Criteria** | "Which criteria are non-negotiable, and why?" | Separates belief-driven criteria from feature checklists. |
| **D — Decision Process** | "Why is the process designed this way? What past event shaped it?" | Reveals the organization's historical WHY around risk and governance. |
| **P — Paper Process** | "Which compliance / paper requirement would kill the initiative if missed?" | Surfaces the compliance/risk WHY of legal and procurement. |
| **I — Identify Pain** | "How does this pain erode what the organization believes about itself?" | Lifts pain from functional → strategic / emotional. |
| **C — Champion** | "Why is this person willing to take political risk for us?" | Reveals the Champion's personal WHY — the basis of loyalty. |
| **C — Competition** | "What does the customer *believe* about each competitor that we can't easily change?" | Names the belief gap — where we win or lose on WHY. |

### Core insight

> MEDDPICC tells you **the facts**. WHY tells you **the reasons behind the facts**.

Example:
| MEDDPICC alone | MEDDPICC + WHY |
|---|---|
| "EB = CFO." | "EB = CFO, under CEO pressure to prove finance is strategic, not back office." |
| "Metric = -50% close cycle." | "Metric = -50% close cycle because CFO promised the board — legacy on the line." |
| "Champion = Finance Director." | "Champion is risking career because she believes without this she is replaced in 18 months." |

## 4. The 5-WHY Narrative Stack (sits on top of MEDDPICC)

Ordered to match executive decision logic:

```
Why Change? → Why Now? → Why Invest? → Why Us? → Why Stay?
```

- **Why Change?** — status quo is broken.
- **Why Now?** — cost of waiting is higher than the cost of acting.
- **Why Invest?** — returns beat alternatives (financial + strategic + personal).
- **Why Us?** — we are the least risky, best-fit partner.
- **Why Stay?** — the 3-year partnership thesis.

For the full treatment (discovery questions, frameworks, pitch patterns per WHY), see `why-stack-narrative.md`.

## 5. The Integrated 65-Point Scorecard

Score every cell 1–5. Total out of 65. Use this in qualification and weekly deal review.

|---|---|---|---|
| M — Metrics | Clear and measurable | Linked to strategic purpose | |

| D — Decision Criteria | Defined | Reflects belief, not only feature | |
| D — Decision Process | Mapped | We understand the organizational WHY behind it | |
| P — Paper Process | Understood | We understand compliance/risk WHY | |
| I — Identify Pain | Identified | Elevated to emotional / strategic | |
| C — Champion | Active | Champion's personal WHY is tied to our success | |
| C — Competition | Mapped | We know the belief gap vs. each alternative | |
| Why Change | — | Customer believes change is required | |
| Why Now | — | Customer believes now is the moment | |
| Why Invest | — | Customer believes the investment pays back | |

### Interpretation

| Score | State | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 55–65 | 🟢 Strong | Drive to close; forecast confidence high. |
| 40–54 | 🟡 Workable | Strengthen the 2–3 weakest cells before closing. |
| 25–39 | 🟠 At Risk | Return to discovery; do not forecast high. |
| < 25 | 🔴 Dead | Disqualify or reduce investment — redirect resources. |

## 6. Four Deal Archetypes (pattern recognition)

Plot the deal on two axes: MEDDPICC strength × WHY strength.

| Archetype | Signal | Risk | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🟢 **Aligned Believer** (High MEDDPICC + High WHY) | All boxes ticked, shared belief clear. | Over-promising, complacency. | Close fast, protect expectations, lock renewal path. |
| 🟡 **Technical Fit** (High MEDDPICC + Low WHY) | Checklist passes, no emotion. | Will become a price war. | Build shared belief urgently. Introduce an executive sponsor who echoes WHY. Use *Why Us* 3-Dimensions. |
| 🟡 **Spiritual Connection** (Low MEDDPICC + High WHY) | Customer loves us, no path to PO. | Will stall for quarters. | Force commitment: if the belief is real, demand budget allocation, Champion escalation, Paper Process kickoff. |
| 🔴 **Lost Cause** (Low MEDDPICC + Low WHY) | Neither qualified nor believed. | Resource drain. | Disqualify, or nurture at minimum cost. |

## 7. Red-Flag → Weak-WHY diagnosis table

When any of these show up in a deal, skip the wishful thinking and look at the matching WHY.

| Red flag in the room | Likely weak WHY / MEDDPICC |
|---|---|
| "Let's revisit next quarter." | Why Now is weak. |
| "The price is over budget." | Why Invest is weak — customer sees cost, not investment. |
| "We're still evaluating vendor X." | Why Us is weak — belief gap vs. competitor. |
| "IT needs to evaluate first." | Decision Process unclear — Champion hasn't mapped it. |
| "The EB is unavailable." | Economic Buyer not engaged. |
| "The Champion is out for weeks." | Champion isn't strong enough. |
| "Customer is asking to reduce scope." | Why Change is weak — pain has not been elevated. |

## 8. Deal Health Pulse (5 minutes per deal)

Run in weekly pipeline review. Structured to stay under 5 minutes even for large pipelines.

**Step 1 — Quick MEDDPICC (2 min)**
For each of the 8 letters: do we have *evidence* (not belief, not hope) that it is true?

**Step 2 — Why-Stack Pulse (2 min)**
Ask the seller five yes/no questions:
1. Does the customer believe they have to change? (Why Change)
2. Does the customer believe now is the time? (Why Now)
3. Does the customer believe the investment pays back? (Why Invest)
4. Does the customer believe we are the answer? (Why Us)
5. Does the customer see three years of us? (Why Stay)

**Step 3 — Diagnosis + single next action (1 min)**
Call out the weakest cell. Name the action for the week (e.g., "Get CFO's personal legacy answer from Champion by Thursday"). No action = no movement.

---

## 9. Forecast discipline rules

- Only *Aligned Believer* deals with 55+ scorecard should sit in "Commit".
- *Technical Fit* deals belong in "Most Likely" at best, with the caveat that margin is at risk.
- *Spiritual Connection* deals belong in "Upside" — they feel close but will not land on the forecasted date unless Paper Process is opened.
- *Lost Cause* deals should be pulled from the forecast entirely.

## 10. Worked micro-example (before / after)

**Before (MEDDPICC-only):**
- Metric: -50% close cycle. EB: CFO. Criteria: feature + price + timeline. Champion: Finance Director. Competition: SAP, Workday.
- Result: shortlist, 15% price below SAP still not enough, deal at risk.

**After (MEDDPICC + WHY bridge):**
- EB's personal WHY: new CFO wants to prove in 18 months he is a strategic leader, not an accountant.
- Champion's personal WHY: wants to move from manual work to strategic analyst role.
- Organizational WHY: group IPO in 24 months.
- Why Now: IPO window + CFO window + current license renewal = three forcing functions.
- Why Us: reference Thai manufacturer group IPO'd in 22 months; advisory staffed by a former CFO, not only technical consultants; 20% fee-at-risk clause the competitor refuses.
- Result: won at +8% price premium over SAP, with add-on EPM opportunity in the next fiscal year.
