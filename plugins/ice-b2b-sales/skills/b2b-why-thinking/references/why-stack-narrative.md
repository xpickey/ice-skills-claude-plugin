# Reference: The Why-Stack Narrative Layer

> Use this reference in **Narrative Mode** and **Authoring Mode**. Full treatment of the 5-WHY executive narrative.

---

## 0. The five WHYs in executive decision order

```
Why Change? → Why Now? → Why Invest? → Why Us? → Why Stay?
```

Most deals fail at Why Now (status quo wins), then at Why Invest (seen as cost, not investment), then at Why Us (no belief gap vs. competitor). Always stress-test all five before shipping.

---

## 1. Why CHANGE — the status quo is broken

**Intent:** prove that continuing as-is carries an unacceptable cost.

### Discovery questions
- What is broken today that can no longer be tolerated?
- How much does "as-is" cost per month — in money, time, risk, morale?
- What recent event exposed the crack (audit finding, lost deal, system outage, board pushback)?
- Who is personally frustrated today and why?

### Framework: Pain Elevation Ladder
Move the pain from functional → strategic → emotional.
- **Functional:** "The close takes 15 days."
- **Strategic:** "Finance can't give the board timely data."
- **Emotional:** "The CFO is being called a back-office function, not a strategic leader."

### Red flags Why Change is weak
- Customer asks to reduce scope — the pain isn't strong enough.
- Discussion stays inside IT — pain hasn't reached the business line.
- No named individual is angry.

---

## 2. Why NOW — beat the "do-nothing competitor"

**Intent:** defeat status-quo inertia. In B2B enterprise, 60–70% of losses are to "do nothing", not to a named competitor. Why Now is usually the weakest WHY.

### Discovery questions

**A. External triggers**
- Any regulation forcing action (PDPA, IFRS9, ESG, e-Tax, e-GP)?
- Competitor move that demands a response?
- End-customer behavior shift already underway?
- Budget / funding window about to close?

**B. Internal triggers**
- Leadership change wanting to make a mark?
- Legacy system reaching end-of-support?
- Recent incident that moved the topic from "someday" to "now"?
- M&A, restructuring, or IPO timeline?

**C. Compound cost**
- What does "one more month of current state" cost?
- What becomes irreversible if we delay six months (lost talent, lost deal, lost window)?
- Will the team that is ready today still be here next year?

### The "Why Not Next Year" test
> If the customer postpones by 12 months, what *cannot be recovered*?

If the answer is weak, the deal will slip 100%.

### Framework: Burning Platform Matrix

```
                  External Urgency (outside pressure)
                         LOW              HIGH
                    ┌─────────────┬─────────────┐
       HIGH   Ready │ Nice-to-have│  🔥 BUY NOW │
   Internal  (can   │             │             │
    Readiness act)  ├─────────────┼─────────────┤
       LOW    Not   │ Dead Deal   │ Parked Deal │
              ready │             │             │
                    └─────────────┴─────────────┘
```

Goal: move the deal into the "Buy Now" cell by raising *both* external urgency and internal readiness.

### Executive pitch pattern (Why Now)
> Three forces converge this quarter:
> 1. *[External trigger]* — e.g. IPO 24 months out requires audit-ready systems.
> 2. *[Internal trigger]* — e.g. new CFO needs to prove impact within 18 months.
> 3. *[Compound cost]* — e.g. current license renews in Q3; renewing it wastes budget twice.
>
> Delaying 12 months is not "saving" — it is paying more in forms you can't see on the P&L.

### Red flags Why Now is weak
- "Let's revisit next quarter" heard more than once.
- No time-bound external event cited.
- Customer wants to "wait for the new fiscal year".

## 3. Why INVEST — returns beat alternatives

**Intent:** reframe from "cost" to "investment" across three lenses.

### The 3-Lens Business Case

**Lens 1 — Financial**
- ROI, NPV, IRR over a stated horizon.
- Payback period.
- TCO vs. alternatives.
- Cost of inaction over 12 / 24 / 36 months.

**Lens 2 — Strategic**
- Competitive position, market share protection.
- Capability built (data, IP, reusable assets).
- Risk mitigated (regulatory, cyber, continuity).
- Optionality unlocked (M&A, IPO, new geography).

**Lens 3 — Personal / Organizational**
- Legacy of the EB.
- Career upside for the Champion.
- Team transformation and retention.
- Culture / identity reinforcement.

### Discovery questions
- **Financial:** "What ROI number will make the board approve without debate?"
- **Financial:** "Which other initiatives compete for this budget — what tips it our way?"
- **Strategic:** "How does this support the 3-year strategic plan?"
- **Strategic:** "If a competitor does this 6 months ahead of us, what happens?"
- **Personal:** "If this succeeds, what do you want to be remembered for?"
- **Personal:** "What is the biggest career risk if this fails?"

### Executive pitch pattern (Why Invest)
> The investment of [X] Million Baht is not about cost — it is about [shared belief].
>
> - **Financially:** it returns [Y]% over 3 years and breaks even at [Z] months.
> - **Strategically:** it protects [K] Million in margin that would erode if competitors act first.
> - **Personally:** it positions [EB's team] as the group that *transformed* the function.
>
> The question is not "can we afford this?" — it is "can we afford *not* to?"

### Red flags Why Invest is weak
- "Budget is tight" repeated multiple times.
- Only functional ROI, no strategic narrative — the board will not sponsor.
- EB has not mentioned the initiative in an internal speech.

## 4. Why US — unfair advantage the competitor can't copy

**Intent:** build a belief gap that is not a feature gap (features can be copied in six months).

### The 3 Dimensions of Why-Us

1. **Proven Outcomes (track record)** — we have done *this specific outcome* at a comparable customer.
2. **Shared Beliefs (WHY fit)** — our WHY overlaps with the customer's WHY.
3. **Risk Mitigation (protection)** — we take commercial or methodological risk the competitor refuses.

The strongest position is the intersection of all three: *proven + believed + de-risked*.

### Discovery questions
- **Outcome:** "Which one reference is closest to this industry, size, and outcome?"
- **Outcome:** "How many similar outcomes in the last three years?"
- **Belief:** "Where does our WHY and yours overlap?"
- **Belief:** "What do we refuse to do that competitors won't refuse?"
- **Risk:** "Which risk worries you most about choosing any partner?"
- **Risk:** "What commercial protection would we offer that others won't?"

### Executive pitch pattern (Why Us)
> Several partners can deliver this solution. We differ in three ways:
>
> 1. **Proven outcomes:** we did [specific outcome] at [comparable company] in [timeframe].
> 2. **Shared beliefs:** we believe the same thing you do — that [belief]. That shapes the way we staff, the way we design, the way we escalate.
> 3. **Risk mitigation competitors refuse:** we will accept [fee-at-risk / outcome clause / SLA]; they will not.
>
> We are not selling a solution. We are selling *proof that you chose right*.

### Common mistakes in Why Us
- Listing credentials (Gartner Leader, ISO 27001, etc.) — competitors list them too.
- Saying "we are the best" — nobody believes it.
- Differentiating on features a competitor can add in 6 months.
- Ending with a logo wall instead of a belief statement.

## 5. Why STAY — the multi-year partnership thesis

**Intent:** reframe the transaction as a journey. Converts a single purchase into a renewal and expansion relationship.

### Discovery questions
- Where do they want to be in 3 years as a business? As a function?
- Which capability gaps does *this* initiative unlock for the *next* initiative?
- What is the logical sequence — ERP → EPM → Planning → AI close? NPL → collection → IFRS9? GFMIS integration → e-Tax → cross-agency reporting?
- Who inside our firm will own the relationship beyond go-live?

### Executive pitch pattern (Why Stay)
> You are not buying [solution]. You are buying a three-year journey to [destination]. Phase 1 lands [outcome]; Phase 2 unlocks [outcome]; by Year 3 you have [capability]. Our role shifts from implementer to partner — measured on *your* outcomes, not our deliverables.

### Red flags Why Stay is weak
- No named account executive / partner past go-live.
- No roadmap beyond the initial phase.
- Contract structure encourages churn (hard cutoff, no glide path).

---

## 6. Assembling the Why-Stack — checklist before you ship

- [ ] All five WHYs have a *headline belief* (one sentence).
- [ ] Each WHY has *three supporting points* — specific, with evidence anchors (`[to confirm]` where unknown).
- [ ] Each WHY has a *pitch-pattern paragraph* in the executive's language.
- [ ] Red flags for each WHY have been tested and mitigated.
- [ ] There is a *one-page Executive Bridge Slide* summarizing all five plus metrics, decision date, sponsor, champion.
- [ ] Thai + English phrasings are prepared if the customer is in the Thai market.

---

## 7. One-sentence tests (when you need a gut check)

- **Why Change:** "If we do nothing, what breaks first — and who gets blamed?"
- **Why Now:** "What becomes irreversible if we wait 12 months?"
- **Why Invest:** "Does the board approve this in 10 minutes or does it need 60?"
- **Why Us:** "Remove our name — could a competitor paste their name in and keep this WHY?"
- **Why Stay:** "In three years, what is the customer *still* buying from us?"
