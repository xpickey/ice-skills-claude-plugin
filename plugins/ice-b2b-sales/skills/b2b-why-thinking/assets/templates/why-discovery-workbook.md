# WHY Discovery Workbook

> Fill out before (or during) a WHY discovery workshop — for your own company, a practice, or a specific offer. Take 45–60 minutes; answer in prose, not bullets.

## Part 1 — Origin

1. How did this organization / practice come to be? Tell it as a story (not a timeline).

2. Who is the founder (or current leader)? What do they believe?

3. When did the team first think: "yes, this is what we should be doing"? Describe the moment.

---

## Part 2 — Belief

4. What do we believe about our industry that competitors do not?

5. Which status quo do we think is wrong — and what evidence do we see?

6. If we had to stand *against* one thing, what would it be?

---

## Part 3 — Contribution

7. How are customers better off because of us — beyond product features?

8. If we closed tomorrow, who would be genuinely hurt, and why?

---

## Part 4 — Impact

9. What impact do we want to have created 10 years from now?

10. What should our legacy be?

---

## Drafts

### Candidate WHY — Standard form
> "To __________________________________ so that __________________________________."

### Candidate WHY — Apple-style form
> "Everything we do, we believe in __________________________________. The way we challenge the status quo is by __________________________________. We just happen to __________________________________."

---

## 7-Point Clarity Test (check ≥ 6 of 7)

- [ ] Simple — a 10-year-old can grasp it in one sentence.
- [ ] Specific — a competitor can't swap names and keep it.
- [ ] Believable — current customers say "yes, that's why I picked you".
- [ ] Actionable — can decide the last 3 big calls we made.
- [ ] Emotional — makes the reader feel, not only understand.
- [ ] Positive — a "do", not a "don't".
- [ ] Enduring — still valid in 10 years.

## Red flags (should be zero)

- [ ] No "best / leading / premier / #1" language.
- [ ] No financial or KPI numbers.
- [ ] No product or service name.
- [ ] Under two sentences.
- [ ] Team can recall it after three readings.
