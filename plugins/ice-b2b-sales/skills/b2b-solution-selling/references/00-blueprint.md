# Solution Selling Skill — Research Blueprint
**For: Enterprise Software Solution Provider (Thailand + APAC)**
**Products: Oracle Cloud ERP/EPM/SCM, SAP, NetSuite, MS Dynamics, Custom/Industry, Financial & Digital Transformation**
**Date: 22 April 2026**
**Purpose: Input for /skill-creator to build a custom "Solution Selling" skill**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

A modern Solution Selling skill for an enterprise software provider in Thailand cannot be a single methodology — it must be a **hybrid framework** that fuses five proven schools of thought and adapts them to Thai/APAC buying culture and the full sell-through-deliver lifecycle.

The recommended hybrid:

| Layer | Methodology | Primary Use |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Conversation engine** | SPIN Selling (Rackham) | Ask the right questions in the right order |
| **Pain & vision engine** | Solution Selling (Bosworth/Eades) | Diagnose pain, co-create vision, map buyer roles |
| **Differentiation engine** | Challenger Sale (Dixon/Adamson) | Teach, Tailor, Take Control with commercial insight |
| **Qualification & forecast discipline** | MEDDPICC (Dunkel/Napoli/Whyte) | Score deals, hold pipeline accuracy |
| **Strategic & advisory layer** | McKinsey / BCG / Big Four frameworks | MECE, hypothesis-driven discovery, SCQA, Trusted Advisor, business-case quantification |

The seller's responsibility runs **prospect → qualify → diagnose → propose → negotiate → close → handover → wrap-up**, with explicit handover discipline so delivery teams don't inherit broken commitments.

## 2. Mandatory Components of the Skill

These nine components are non-negotiable. Each one must be present in the final skill (as either an instruction, a template, or a sub-skill).

### Component 1 — Pre-Call Discovery & Hypothesis Builder
**Purpose:** Force sellers to enter every meeting with a falsifiable hypothesis (McKinsey/BCG style), not an open-ended fishing trip.

Mandatory inputs the seller must capture before each call:
- Industry, sub-segment, company size, ownership structure (family, SET-listed, MNC subsidiary, government/SOE)
- Top 3 likely business pains (hypothesis)
- Likely Economic Buyer + Champion candidates
- Compelling event hypothesis (regulatory deadline, system EOL, M&A, audit finding)
- Competitive landscape (incumbent + 2 alternatives)
- Two reframe insights to teach (Challenger)

### Component 2 — SPIN-Driven Question Generator
**Purpose:** Replace product-pitch openers with disciplined Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-payoff sequencing.

Mandatory artefacts:
- Question library by buyer role (CFO, CIO, COO, Controller, IT Director, Procurement, Business Unit Head)
- Question library by product domain (ERP, EPM, SCM, HCM, CRM, Industry vertical)
- Time allocation rule: ≥60% Implication + Need-payoff in complex sales (Rackham research)
- Anti-pattern checklist: too many Situation questions, premature Feature dump, unearned Benefit statement

### Component 3 — Pain Sheet & Pain Chain (Solution Selling)
**Purpose:** Convert vague complaints into Admitted Pain → Critical Pain with quantified business impact.

Mandatory structure:
- Pain (admitted by buyer in their words)
- Reason (root cause)
- Impact (quantified — baht, days, FTE, % margin, audit risk)
- Pain owner (which role feels it)
- Pain Chain (how this pain cascades up the org — User → Manager → Director → C-suite)
- Capability needed to relieve the pain (NOT product feature)

### Component 4 — 9-Box Vision Processing Model
**Purpose:** Co-create the buyer's vision of the solution so they own it (instead of being sold to).

Mandatory matrix:
|  | Reasons | Impact | Capabilities |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Open** | Open question to surface a reason | Open question to surface an impact | Open question on capability needed |
| **Control** | Confirm or correct reasoning | Confirm or correct impact | Confirm or correct capability |
| **Confirm** | Lock the reason in writing | Lock the quantified impact | Lock the capability requirement |

The seller never says "Oracle does X." The buyer says "I need a system that does X." That is the Solution Selling discipline.

### Component 5 — MEDDPICC Deal Qualification Scorecard
**Purpose:** Force red/yellow/green discipline on every deal in pipeline. Drive forecast accuracy.

Mandatory eight elements (each scored 0–3):
- **M** — Metrics (quantified business outcome agreed by EB)
- **E** — Economic Buyer (named, met, engaged within last 21 days)
- **D** — Decision Criteria (technical + business + relationship — documented)
- **D** — Decision Process (steps + dates + gates)
- **P** — Paper Process (legal, procurement, security, audit — mapped)
- **I** — Identify Pain + Compelling Event (deadline-driven)
- **C** — Champion (proven by an action they took for you in last 14 days)
- **C** — Competition (named alternatives + status quo)

Rule: **No deal moves to commit forecast unless 7 of 8 elements are green.**

### Component 6 — Challenger Commercial Teaching Pitch
**Purpose:** Differentiate from "feature parity" competitors by reframing the buyer's worldview.

Mandatory 6-step structure:
1. **Warmer** — Establish credibility ("We've worked with 3 Thai food-processing groups...")
2. **Reframe** — Challenge their current approach ("The lift-and-shift you're planning is the most expensive path...")
3. **Rational Drowning** — Quantify the cost of inaction in their numbers
4. **Emotional Impact** — Tell a peer story that makes the pain feel real
5. **New Way** — Present the alternative criteria for success
6. **Your Solution** — Show how your offering uniquely enables the new way

Rule: Calibrate tone for Thai culture — challenge through questions, not assertions; lead with humility ("Our peers have learned…"), not judgment.

### Component 7 — Stakeholder Map + Mobilizer Targeting
**Purpose:** Avoid single-threaded selling; build coalition across the buying centre.

Mandatory artefacts:
- Power × Interest grid (Promoters / Subjects / Allies / Minimal effort)
- Mobilizer classification (Go-Getter / Teacher / Skeptic = good; Friend / Guide / Climber / Blocker = manage or neutralize)
- Champion proof points (what have they done for you in the last 14 days?)
- Detractor mitigation strategy (especially the legacy ERP admin whose job is threatened)
- RACI for the buying decision (who is Responsible / Accountable / Consulted / Informed)

### Component 8 — Quantified Business Case (Big Four style)
**Purpose:** Convert solution into board-grade ROI / NPV / Payback that the CFO can defend internally.

Mandatory model:
- Baseline current-state metrics (with source)
- Target future-state metrics (with assumptions)
- Year-by-year benefit cashflow (3 or 5 year)
- Total solution cost (license + implementation + change mgmt + run-rate)
- ROI %, NPV @ company cost of capital, IRR, Payback period
- Sensitivity analysis (best / base / worst — which 3 assumptions move the answer most)
- THB-denominated, with FX risk note for USD-priced licenses
- SCQA executive summary (Situation, Complication, Question, Answer — 1 page, signed off by Champion)

### Component 9 — Sales-to-Delivery Handover & Project Closure Pack
**Purpose:** Stop the "throw it over the wall" failure mode that destroys customer trust in week one of implementation.

Mandatory handover artefacts (produced before contract signature, NOT after):
1. **Win Brief** (1–2 pages — context, sponsor, success criteria, top 3 risks)
2. **SOW Alignment Document** (line-by-line walk of SOW vs. customer's pain)
3. **Customer Success Criteria Sheet** (measurable business outcomes with baseline + 30/60/90/180/365-day targets)
4. **Stakeholder Map** (with detractor strategy)
5. **Commercial Summary** (price, milestones, T&Cs, SLA, renewal date)
6. **Open Commitments Register** (every verbal / side-letter promise — flagged In-SOW vs. Out-of-Scope)
7. **Risk Register** (with mitigation owner)
8. **Handover Meetings**: Internal (Sales → Delivery) AND Joint Kick-off (Sales + Delivery + Customer)
9. **Account Continuity Model**: Executive Sponsor or Hands-Off — explicitly chosen and documented
10. **Closure / Wrap-up Pack**: Value Realization Review, Reference / Case Study, NPS, Expansion plan, Renewal strategy locked 6–9 months before expiry

KPIs for "good handover": <5% scope-creep, ≤2 escalations in 12 months, NPS ≥7 at 90 days, business case attainment on track at 90 days, customer signals renewal intent by month 9.

## 3. Mandatory Character Traits / Seller DNA

The skill must coach the seller AND assess the seller. These are the non-negotiable traits.

### A. Hard Skills (Knowledge — testable)
1. **Business Acumen** — read a P&L / Balance Sheet / Cash Flow; identify CFO red flags (DSO, DIO, DPO, free cash flow, covenant ratios)
2. **Financial Literacy** — ROI, NPV, IRR, Payback, TCO, Cost of Capital, sensitivity analysis
3. **Industry Knowledge** — at least 2 verticals deep (e.g., Manufacturing + Financial Services + Government)
4. **Product Depth** — own the 5 core modules of Oracle / SAP / NetSuite / Dynamics; know the integration patterns
5. **Competitive Intelligence** — when does each platform win and lose, and why
6. **Contract & Commercial Fluency** — licensing models, SLA terms, liability caps, payment milestones, change control
7. **Project Economics** — implementation duration estimation, internal customer-resource cost, FX risk, regulatory/localization scope
8. **Thai Procurement Law** — Procurement Act B.E. 2560, e-GP / e-bidding / selection / specific method, TOR drafting, price ceiling, สตง. audit sensitivity, anti-corruption (PACC, NACC, FCPA, UKBA)

### B. Soft Skills (Behavioural — observable)
1. **Active Listening** — listens for unspoken fear; uses 10-second silence after questions
2. **Executive Presence** — speaks in SCQA structure, commands without dominating, dresses appropriately
3. **Empathy** — recognizes the GL manager's job-loss fear; the controller's perfectionism; the IT team's "yet-another-system" fatigue
4. **Intellectual Curiosity** — asks "why" five times; reads Gartner / Forrester quarterly
5. **Courage to Challenge** — pushes back on bad assumptions, but in Thai-appropriate language ("With respect, may I suggest…")
6. **Situational Awareness** — reads the room, names the silence, calls out political currents diplomatically
7. **Storytelling** — sells via peer-customer narratives, not feature lists
8. **Negotiation & Conflict Resolution** — separates people from problems; creates win-win; escalates only at sponsor level

### C. Mindset / Behavioural DNA (Identity-level — coachable but slow to change)
1. **Coachability & Growth Mindset** — debriefs every call, owns mistakes immediately
2. **Resilience** — survives 12–24-month enterprise cycles, lost deals, executive turnover, budget freezes
3. **Accountability & Ownership** — owns forecast accuracy, owns customer outcomes after the close
4. **Integrity** — admits what they don't know, never oversells, protects customer-confidential information
5. **Customer-Obsession over Quota-Obsession** — willing to delay a deal that isn't right for the customer

### D. Cross-Cultural / Thailand-Specific DNA
1. **Patience & Long-Term Relationship Orientation** — accepts 9-month cycles where Western seller expected 6
2. **Kreng-Jai Fluency** — never publicly contradicts, always preserves face
3. **Comfort with Ambiguity** — Thai contracts and decision rights are often less explicit; the seller must navigate without forcing premature clarity
4. **Trilingual Communication** — Thai / English / business jargon; produces dual-language proposals; brings a Thai-speaking partner to government and family-business meetings
5. **Consensus-Decision Tolerance** — prepares stakeholder-specific materials; expects 10+ touchpoints; doesn't try to artificially compress
6. **Anti-Corruption Discipline** — refuses gifts >THB 500 to government; no private entertainment with public officials; documented

### E. Trusted-Advisor vs Vendor (the senior-seller divide)
| Dimension | Vendor (junior) | Advisor (senior) |
|---|---|---|
| Discovery | "What are your requirements?" | "What's keeping your CFO awake?" |
| Proposal | "Here's our system; here's the cost." | "Here are 3 outcome scenarios; which is most valuable?" |
| Objection | "But our software does X…" | "I hear the concern. Let's design an approach that addresses it." |
| Stakeholders | Talks to IT only | Maps & engages CFO, CIO, Controller, Audit, Treasury |
| Post-sale | Hands off to support | Owns QBRs and value realization |
| Pricing | List price + discount | Outcome-based ("share of savings") |

### F. Common Derailers (must be screened OUT)
- **Happy Ears** — believes "sounds good" = "will buy"
- **Alligator Arms** — won't ask for the order
- **Feature-Dumping** — recites 47 features instead of solving 1 pain
- **Single-Threaded Selling** — entire deal depends on one CIO who will leave
- **Avoiding the Economic Buyer** — spends 12 weeks with the GL manager
- **Sandbagging Forecast** — destroys credibility within 2 quarters

## 6. Success Criteria for the Skill (validation in Step 5 of the workflow)

When built, the skill should be able to:

1. Generate a SPIN question set for any role × any product in <2 minutes
2. Score any deal on MEDDPICC and produce the 3 weakest elements with remediation actions
3. Build a Thai-context-aware Challenger reframe that doesn't sound culturally tone-deaf
4. Output a CFO-grade business case (THB, NPV, sensitivity) signed-off-able by a Big Four reviewer
5. Produce a handover pack that delivery accepts without a re-discovery cycle
6. Coach the seller on weakest DNA traits with specific behaviours to practise
7. Adapt language and process for Government (e-GP / TOR / สตง.) vs Large Enterprise vs SME

Quality gates before deployment:
- Tested on 3 sample deals (1 government, 1 large enterprise, 1 SME) by experienced sellers
- Reviewed by a Thai-native senior account director for cultural calibration
- Cross-checked against the existing `b2b-enterprise-sale-strategy` skill to avoid contradiction
