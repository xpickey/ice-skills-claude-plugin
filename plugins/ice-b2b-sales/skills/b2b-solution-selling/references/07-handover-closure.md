# Reference 07 — Sales-to-Delivery Handover & Project Closure Pack

**Use when:** Contract is about to be signed (NOT after — see rule below). Internal kickoff is being scheduled. Customer kickoff workshop being prepared. Sales is being asked to "stay involved" after signature. A signed deal is bleeding margin because expectations diverged from SOW. Project is closing and you want to mine it for renewal, expansion, and reference value.

**Cardinal rule:** *All handover artefacts must exist BEFORE the contract is signed.* A handover after signature is a disaster recovery, not a handover. The 10 artefacts below should be drafted during the negotiation phase and finalised at signature.

---

## A. Why most enterprise software handovers fail

Industry data (Gartner, BCG): **70% of enterprise software project overruns** trace to sales-to-delivery misalignment, not technical issues. The four killers:

1. **Throw-over-the-wall**: seller closes, disappears, delivery inherits a black box
2. **Verbal commitments not in SOW**: "I told them we'd integrate with Workday" — but it is not scoped
3. **Stakeholder map lost**: delivery starts with the working-level team, never meets the EB until escalation
4. **Success criteria undefined**: customer success means whatever the customer wants it to mean by month 6

The discipline below prevents all four.

## B. The 10 mandatory handover artefacts

| # | Artefact | Owner | Definition of Done | When |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Win Brief** | Account Director | 1-pager: why customer bought, what they expect, EB's KPIs, key Champion contacts, why they chose us over the competition | At verbal-yes |
| 2 | **SOW Alignment Memo** | AD + Delivery Lead | Maps every customer expectation to a specific SOW line item; flags any mismatch in writing | Before signature |
| 3 | **Customer Success Criteria** | EB-signed document | 3–5 numerical outcomes (e.g., "close in 7 days by Q4 FY27", "system available 99.5%", "50 power users certified") | Before signature |
| 4 | **Stakeholder Map** | AD | Power × Interest grid + Mobilizer types + Champion proof-points + Blocker neutralisation status | Before signature |
| 5 | **Open Commitments Log** | AD | Every verbal promise made during the sale, with status: in-SOW / out-of-scope-but-promised / acknowledged-not-promised | Before signature |
| 6 | **Risk Register** | AD + Delivery Lead | Top 10 risks (commercial, technical, change-management, political), with mitigation and residual owner | Before signature |
| 7 | **Delivery Roadmap** | Delivery Lead | Phases, milestones, gate criteria, customer dependencies, RAID log structure | Before kickoff |
| 8 | **Change-Management Plan** | Delivery Lead + customer Champion | Comms plan, training plan, super-user network, go-live readiness checklist | First 30 days |
| 9 | **Governance Charter** | EB + Steering Committee | Executive sponsor on each side, steering committee cadence, escalation path, decision rights | Before kickoff |
| 10 | **Renewal & Expansion Brief** | AD | Year-2 expansion hypothesis, additional modules, adjacent business units, success-story potential | At signature, refreshed quarterly |

## C. The Win Brief template

A 1-page document that travels with the deal. Delivery reads this before kickoff.

```
=== WIN BRIEF ===
Customer:                [name + entity]
Deal:                    [product, scope, TCV, contract length]
Signed date / Go-live:   [dates]
Account Director:        [name]
Delivery Lead:           [name]
Executive Sponsor (us):  [name]

=== WHY THEY BOUGHT ===
Critical Pain:           [Pain Sheet top entry, in their words]
Compelling Event:        [the deadline / forcing function]
Quantified value:        [THB / days / % they expect]
Their EB's headline win: [what the EB will tell their boss in 12 months]

=== WHO BOUGHT ===
Economic Buyer:          [name, role, KPI]
Champion:                [name, role, what they did for us]
Other key stakeholders:  [name, role, posture]
Risk: known Blocker?     [name, why, neutralisation plan]

=== WHY US (vs competition) ===
Final round:             [our offer vs competitor offer]
Decisive factor:         [the one thing that won]
Our differentiator they will reference at first complaint: ...

=== COMMITMENTS WE MADE (open log) ===
Commitment | In SOW? | Status | Owner
1. ...
2. ...

=== EARLY-WARNING TRIPWIRES ===
Watch for: [first 3 things that, if they happen, mean the deal is in trouble]
1. ...
2. ...
3. ...
```

## D. Customer Success Criteria — the contract-within-the-contract

The SOW says what we will *do*. The Success Criteria say what good *looks like*. They are not the same.

**Bad Success Criteria** (ambiguous, will be argued at month 6):
- "Improve finance efficiency"
- "Successful go-live"
- "Stakeholder satisfaction"

**Good Success Criteria** (numerical, measurable, EB-signed):
- "Month-end close ≤ 7 business days by Q4 FY27 (currently 18)"
- "System availability ≥ 99.5% in any rolling 30-day window post-go-live"
- "≥ 50 finance users certified to power-user level by go-live + 30 days"
- "Audit findings on segregation-of-duties closed by next external audit"
- "User adoption (weekly active power users) ≥ 80% by go-live + 90 days"

**Discipline:** EB signs the Success Criteria document. It is referenced in every steering committee. Variance from criteria triggers escalation — not a passive-aggressive email.

## E. Internal kickoff (sales → delivery, before customer kickoff)

A 2-hour working session, mandatory, attended by:

- Account Director (sales)
- Delivery Lead / Engagement Manager
- Solution Architect
- Product specialist(s)
- Pre-sales engineer who built the demo
- Executive sponsor (us)

**Agenda:**

1. **Win Brief read-aloud** (15 min) — every word matters
2. **Open Commitments review** (30 min) — what did we promise that is not in SOW; how do we close the gap (re-scope, write-off, CR)
3. **Stakeholder Map walk-through** (30 min) — who delivery will meet first, who needs Champion-mediated introduction
4. **Risk Register review** (20 min) — top 5 risks, who owns mitigation
5. **Roles, RACI, decision rights** (15 min) — who escalates to whom on each side
6. **First 30 / 60 / 90 days plan** (10 min) — what delivery commits to do, what AD commits to do (yes, AD has obligations post-signature)

## F. Customer kickoff (the public face)

A half-day workshop with the customer's working team + EB attendance.

**Agenda:**

1. **EB-to-EB opening** (15 min) — our exec sponsor and theirs jointly state the strategic intent
2. **Success Criteria read-back** (20 min) — read the EB-signed Success Criteria; everyone in the room agrees
3. **Roadmap & milestones** (45 min) — gate-by-gate, with explicit customer dependencies
4. **Governance charter** (30 min) — steering committee cadence, escalation path, decision rights
5. **Risk register review** (30 min) — joint ownership of mitigations
6. **Change-management plan** (45 min) — comms, training, super-user network
7. **Open issues parking lot** (15 min) — anything not addressed today, with owner and date

**Output:** EB signs the workshop output document. Everyone in the room has a copy. This becomes the reference for every steering committee.

## G. AD's role post-signature (the part most sellers skip)

Sales does not "hand over and walk away". The Account Director's responsibilities post-signature:

| Cadence | Activity | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly | EB touchpoint (joint with Delivery Lead) | Renewal radar; expansion radar; political weather report |
| Quarterly | Executive Business Review | Show progress against Success Criteria; plant seeds for year 2 |
| At every gate (kickoff, design freeze, UAT, go-live) | Attend in person | Signals continuity; AD knows when reality diverges from sales narrative |
| Continuous | Update Win Brief and Renewal & Expansion Brief | Refreshed quarterly; ready for renewal conversation 9 months before contract end |

A seller who disappears post-signature has no business asking for the renewal.

## H. Project closure pack (at go-live + 90 days)

The closure pack converts a delivered project into the next deal. Build it as a standard artefact:

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **Outcomes scorecard** | Success Criteria: target, achieved, variance, root-cause if missed |
| **Quantified value** | THB delivered vs THB promised; methodology shown |
| **Customer testimonial** | EB quote, 2 sentences, signed-off for use |
| **Reference call willingness** | EB / Champion willing to take 30-min calls — yes / no, frequency limit |
| **Lessons learned (private)** | What we should do differently next time (sales + delivery joint) |
| **Expansion brief** | Year-2 expansion hypothesis: modules, business units, geographies, integrations |
| **Renewal brief** | Renewal date, recommended terms, leverage points |
| **Case-study draft** | 2-page customer case study, ready for marketing approval |

This pack is reviewed in a "Win Replay" session by the broader sales team. Other AEs harvest the pattern for their own accounts.

## I. Renewal & expansion strategy

**Renewal rules of thumb:**
- Start renewal conversations **9 months** before contract end (not 90 days)
- A customer who is hitting Success Criteria renews 4× more often than one who is not
- The biggest renewal risk: EB churn. If the EB who signed has left, treat the renewal like a new logo
- The biggest expansion lever: a peer EB who saw your customer's outcome and wants the same

**Expansion playbook:**
1. **Land** — first deployment, narrow scope, fast win
2. **Prove** — Success Criteria delivered, EB quotable
3. **Expand horizontally** — same customer, adjacent module / business unit / geography
4. **Expand vertically** — same customer, deeper functional capability
5. **Reference** — peer EB introduction; new logo via warm referral

The Renewal & Expansion Brief from the closure pack feeds steps 3–5 directly.

## J. Output-format guidance

When the user asks for a handover or closure pack, deliver:

1. **The Win Brief** filled in with deal specifics
2. **A SOW Alignment table** — every customer expectation mapped to SOW clause; gaps flagged with proposed close
3. **The Customer Success Criteria** drafted in numerical, EB-signable form
4. **A Stakeholder Map** carried over from `references/05-stakeholder-mobilizer.md`
5. **An Open Commitments Log** — every verbal promise, status, owner, close-out plan
6. **A Risk Register** — top 10 risks, with probability × impact and mitigation
7. **A 30/60/90 day plan** for both AD and Delivery Lead
8. **A Governance Charter** — exec sponsors, steering cadence, escalation path

For closure: the **Project Closure Pack** with the 8 sections above.

Then offer the next component: "Want me to draft the EBR deck for the customer's first quarterly review, or build the Year-2 expansion business case?"

## K. Common failure modes

- **Handover after signature.** Disaster recovery, not handover. Build artefacts in negotiation phase.
- **No EB-signed Success Criteria.** Six months later, "success" is whatever they want it to be — and they will move the goalposts.
- **AD disappears post-signature.** Renewal goes to procurement RFP and you are the incumbent fighting on price. AD must own EB relationship for life of contract.
- **Open Commitments not logged.** "But you told them you'd integrate with Workday" — and now delivery is doing it for free. Log every verbal commitment in writing at signature.
- **No Risk Register.** Risks become surprises become escalations become reference-poison.
- **Closure pack treated as marketing chore.** The closure pack is the next deal. Treat it as commercial-critical.
- **Renewal conversation 90 days before contract end.** Customer has already RFP'd alternatives. Start at month 9 of the year before, not month 11.
