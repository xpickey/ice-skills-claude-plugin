# Reference 09 — Seller DNA Rubric (Hard / Soft / Mindset / Thailand-specific)

**Use when:** Coaching a seller after a deliverable is produced. Diagnosing why a seller's deals are stuck. Hiring or promoting an enterprise account executive. Building a sales-enablement curriculum. Self-assessment by the seller.

This rubric is the diagnostic layer the skill uses *after* delivering any component. Every output produced by this skill exposes the seller's DNA — strengths to leverage, gaps to coach.

---

## A. The four DNA buckets

A senior enterprise software seller is not one trait — it is a stack of four. Weak in any one bucket caps the seller's ceiling.

1. **Hard skills** — domain, methodology, financial literacy
2. **Soft skills** — discovery, communication, executive presence
3. **Mindset & character** — courage, ownership, intellectual honesty
4. **Thailand / market-specific traits** — cultural fluency, regulatory awareness

## B. Bucket 1 — Hard skills

| Trait | Weak signal (1) | Senior signal (5) |
|---|---|---|
| **Domain depth (Oracle / SAP / NetSuite / etc.)** | Recites brochure features | Can debate architecture trade-offs with a CIO; knows three competitor weaknesses by heart |
| **Industry depth** | "ERP is ERP" | Names 3 critical processes specific to retail / manufacturing / banking; knows the regulator and the audit pattern |
| **Methodology fluency (SPIN / MEDDPICC / Challenger / Solution Selling)** | Picks one favourite, applies it to everything | Selects the right tool for the right moment; combines them naturally |
| **Financial literacy (NPV / IRR / payback / sensitivity / WACC)** | Can spell ROI; cannot calculate IRR | Builds defensible business case unaided; defends sensitivity assumptions to a CFO |
| **Procurement literacy (esp. Thai e-GP / Procurement Act 2560)** | Hands TOR to legal | Drafts TOR-defensible proposals; spots Procurement Act issues before legal does |
| **Negotiation tactics** | Discounts to close | Trades scope for price; uses risk-transfer instruments; protects margin and integrity |

**Diagnostic question:** Could this seller defend their proposal in a CFO challenge meeting *without backup*?

## C. Bucket 2 — Soft skills

| Trait | Weak signal (1) | Senior signal (5) |
|---|---|---|
| **Discovery — listening ratio** | Talks 70% of the call (feature pitch) | Talks 30%; the buyer talks 70% (Pain admitted); seller summarises and confirms |
| **Question quality** | Mostly Situation questions ("which version are you on?") | Heavy Implication and Need-payoff; buyer feels the pain getting bigger as they speak |
| **Executive presence** | Reads slides verbatim; nervous in C-suite | Drives the room; reframes the agenda; CFO leans in |
| **Storytelling** | Quotes statistics | Tells a peer-customer story that the EB remembers a week later |
| **Writing — concise, structured, persuasive** | 5-page email no one reads | 1-page SCQA the Champion forwards to the board |
| **Tailoring** | Same pitch to CFO, CIO, Procurement | Distinctly different pitch per persona; same insight, different language and frame |
| **Active listening + paraphrasing** | "OK got it, let me show you the solution" | "So if I have heard this right, the cost is about THB 13M and the audit risk is the bigger concern. Is that the order?" |

**Diagnostic question:** After this seller's call, does the buyer say "they really understood our business" — or "they did a great pitch"?

## D. Bucket 3 — Mindset & character

This is where Challenger research showed the biggest separation between top and bottom performers. Hard skills can be trained; mindset is harder.

| Trait | Weak signal (1) | Senior signal (5) |
|---|---|---|
| **Courage to challenge the buyer** | "Whatever you want, sir" | Pushes back constructively when buyer's plan will fail; cites peer evidence; offers alternative |
| **Intellectual honesty** | Sandbags forecasts; happy ears on bad signals | Tells manager "this deal is yellow even though the customer is friendly — here is why" |
| **Ownership of customer outcome** | "Sales is closing; delivery is delivery" | Stays engaged post-signature; attends every gate; treats customer success as personal KPI |
| **Comfort with ambiguity** | Needs a script | Operates from first principles when methodology runs out |
| **Resilience** | Loses one deal, slumps for a quarter | Loses; runs the post-mortem; identifies root cause; back at it next week |
| **Curiosity** | Reads no industry news outside required reading | Reads customer's annual report, regulator's announcements, peer's earnings call before every meeting |
| **Long-game patience (esp. Thailand / govt)** | Wants close-this-quarter; abandons after silence | Invests for 18 months; understands compounding bun-khun; closes 8-figure deals others give up on |

**Diagnostic question:** When this seller loses a deal, do they blame the buyer / product / pricing — or do they own a specific gap and change behaviour?

## E. Bucket 4 — Thailand / market-specific traits

This bucket is what separates a foreign-trained seller from a Thailand-winning seller. It is independent of the other three; weak here caps the ceiling in Thailand markets.

| Trait | Weak signal (1) | Senior signal (5) |
|---|---|---|
| **Cultural fluency (kreng-jai, face, hierarchy)** | "They never said no" — assumes silence = yes | Reads silence accurately; provides face-saving exits; never publicly contradicts a senior |
| **Bun-khun discipline** | Transactional only | Long-arc relationship investment; compounds favours over years |
| **Family-business map literacy** | Org chart only | Identifies family principal, family advisor, family lawyer — maps the shadow buying centre |
| **Government / SOE procurement literacy** | Treats government like enterprise | Understands pre-TOR / TOR / post-TOR phases; never lobbies the committee |
| **Procurement Act 2560 fluency** | Reads it once, never again | Can quote relevant sections; spots TOR defensibility risks; protects customer from สตง. exposure |
| **Anti-corruption discipline** | "It is just a small gift" | Knows the THB 3,000 threshold; reports all hospitality; never uses agents informally |
| **Thai language and protocol** | English-only; calls EB by first name | Conversational Thai (or partnered with someone who is); appropriate khun + nickname use; respects seniority titles |
| **Senior-to-senior pairing** | Sends junior to senior buyer | Pairs seniority on each side; brings exec sponsor to EB meetings |

**Diagnostic question:** Has this seller closed a Thai government / SOE / family-conglomerate deal in the last 2 years — and can they explain *why* it closed?

## F. The eight derailers (most common reasons enterprise sellers miss quota)

These are the behavioural anti-patterns that the skill should screen for in every output and coach against:

| # | Derailer | Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Happy ears** | Reports the deal as "going well" when no Champion action and no EB meeting | MEDDPICC scorecard refresh weekly; manager rejects forecast without 7/8 green |
| 2 | **Alligator arms** | Avoids the EB; comfortable only with Coach / friend | Manager mandates EB touch every 21 days; if not happening, deal yellow |
| 3 | **Feature dumping** | Pitch slide first; pain second (or never) | SPIN ratio enforced: 50%+ Implication and Need-payoff |
| 4 | **Single-threaded** | One contact; CRM has 5 names but only 1 met in 30 days | Multi-thread plan in every deal review; Champion enablement pack |
| 5 | **Sandbagging the forecast** | 5/8 green deals in commit; consistent miss | Manager rejects 5/8 commits; coaches on what action turns the next element green |
| 6 | **Discount reflex** | Drops price at first procurement push | Trade scope for price; risk-transfer instruments; bring product mgmt to negotiation |
| 7 | **Throw-over-the-wall** | Closes; disappears; delivery escalates within 60 days | All 10 handover artefacts before signature; AD attends every steering committee |
| 8 | **Blame externalising** | "Customer was not serious / pricing was unfair" after every loss | Mandatory loss post-mortem with named root cause and behaviour change |

## G. The coaching loop

After the skill produces any deliverable, the coach (or the seller in self-assessment) does this loop:

1. **Pick one bucket** that the deliverable most exposed (often Mindset for stuck deals, Hard Skills for fuzzy business cases)
2. **Score 1–5** on the 2–3 most relevant traits in that bucket
3. **Pick one specific behaviour** to change in the next 14 days (not a vague aspiration)
4. **Define the proof** — what would success look like in 14 days? (e.g., "Champion will introduce me to EB", "I will deliver a 1-page SCQA the Champion forwards unchanged")
5. **Review at the next 1:1** — did the proof happen? If not, why not?

A seller who runs this loop weekly grows a level per quarter. A seller who does not, plateaus.

## I. Output-format guidance

When the user asks the skill to coach a seller (or the user is self-coaching after a deliverable):

1. **Identify the bucket** the deliverable most exposed
2. **Highlight 2–3 traits** with specific evidence from the deliverable ("your business case had no sensitivity table → financial literacy gap")
3. **Suggest one behaviour change for the next 14 days** with a measurable proof
4. **Reference the most relevant deal action** from the related component (e.g., "before the next CFO meeting, build the sensitivity table per `06-business-case-bigfour.md`")
5. **Call out any derailer** the deliverable revealed
6. **In Thailand context, always include a Bucket 4 trait** in the coaching focus

## J. Common failure modes (in coaching)

- **Coaching only on hard skills.** Mindset is harder to change but moves the needle more.
- **Generic feedback.** "Improve your discovery" is useless. "Your last call was 65% Situation questions; cut to 20% by preparing 4 Implication questions" is coaching.
- **No proof point.** "Be more curious" cannot be measured. "Read the customer's last 4 quarterly reports before next week's meeting" can.
- **Coaching the symptom, not the bucket.** A discount-reflex seller does not need negotiation training — they need courage to walk away (Mindset). Diagnose the bucket.
- **Skipping Bucket 4 for Thai sellers / deals.** A Bucket 1–3 senior who does not understand kreng-jai will lose Thai deals at the same rate as a junior.
