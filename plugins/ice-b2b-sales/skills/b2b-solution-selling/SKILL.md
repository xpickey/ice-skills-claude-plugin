# B2B Solution Selling Skill

A hybrid, sell-through-deliver playbook for enterprise software solution providers in Thailand and APAC. It fuses five proven methodologies into one coherent process and adapts them to Thai/APAC buying culture, government e-GP procurement, and the realities of 12–24 month enterprise software cycles.

## When to use this skill

Use this skill whenever the conversation is about advancing, qualifying, defending, or coaching a B2B enterprise software opportunity. Specifically:

- **Discovery & qualification**: "help me prep for a discovery call", "what should I ask the CFO", "build a pain sheet", "qualify this deal", "MEDDPICC for this opportunity", "is this deal real"
- **Vision & differentiation**: "how do I differentiate from SAP / Oracle / Workday", "build a Challenger pitch", "reframe the customer's thinking", "we are losing on price"
- **Stakeholder & politics**: "map the buying centre", "who is the real economic buyer", "we have a blocker in IT", "single-threaded — how to expand"
- **Business case**: "build the ROI / NPV / business case", "CFO needs a board paper", "quantify the savings", "SCQA executive summary"
- **Proposal & negotiation**: "draft the proposal", "respond to TOR / e-GP", "negotiate scope vs price", "Thai government bid"
- **Handover & closure**: "sales-to-delivery handover", "win brief for delivery", "kick-off pack", "wrap up the project", "renewal strategy", "build a reference / case study"
- **Coaching & forecasting**: "weekly pipeline review", "coach this seller", "why did we lose", "deal post-mortem", "forecast this quarter", "seller assessment"

If the user is working on **any** Oracle Cloud / SAP / NetSuite / MS Dynamics / custom industry / financial transformation / digital transformation enterprise opportunity — even when they do not name a methodology — use this skill.

## Why this skill exists

Most enterprise sellers default to one favourite methodology (usually whichever they were trained on). That single tool fails because:

- SPIN gives great questions but no qualification discipline.
- Solution Selling diagnoses pain but is weak on differentiation against modern incumbents.
- Challenger differentiates but can sound culturally tone-deaf in Thailand and Asia.
- MEDDPICC qualifies but does not teach how to *create* pain or vision.
- Big Four frameworks structure thinking but were designed for advisory, not software-license closing.

This skill stacks all five so each compensates for the others' weaknesses, and adds an explicit Thailand / government / SOE context layer plus a sales-to-delivery handover layer that most playbooks ignore.

## How to use this skill

Step 1 — **Identify which deal stage the user is in.** Map the request to one of the nine mandatory components (table below). If unsure, ask one clarifying question.

Step 2 — **Read the relevant reference file(s) from `references/`.** Do not try to recite the whole methodology from memory. Each reference is a self-contained working brief.

Step 3 — **For Thailand / government / SOE deals, also read `references/08-thailand-context.md`.** Thai buying culture, kreng-jai, e-GP procurement, and สตง. audit sensitivity change everything about how the methodology is applied.

Step 4 — **Produce the deliverable.** Use the templates and structures in the reference file. Quantify in THB. Name real stakeholders. Avoid feature dumps. Ground every claim in either evidence the user supplied or a clearly labelled assumption.

Step 5 — **Pressure-test before delivering.** Apply the seller-DNA rubric in `references/09-seller-dna-rubric.md` to your own draft. Common derailers to screen for: happy ears, alligator arms, feature dumping, single-threaded selling, avoiding the economic buyer, sandbagging the forecast.

Step 6 — **Offer the next logical step.** Solution selling is a chain — discovery feeds qualification feeds vision feeds business case feeds handover. After delivering one component, suggest the next.

## Mandatory rules

These are the disciplines that separate a senior advisor from a vendor. Apply them to every output.

1. **No feature lists without quantified business outcomes.** Every capability must connect to a Pain Chain entry and a metric the Economic Buyer cares about.
2. **No deal moves to commit forecast unless 7 of 8 MEDDPICC elements are green.** If the user wants you to forecast a deal, run the scorecard first.
3. **No proposal goes out without a Champion proof point.** A Champion is proven by an action they took for you in the last 14 days, not by friendliness.
4. **No business case without sensitivity analysis.** Show best / base / worst, and name the three assumptions that move the answer most. CFOs trust models that admit their fragility.
5. **No handover after the contract is signed.** All handover artefacts (Win Brief, SOW Alignment, Success Criteria, Stakeholder Map, Open Commitments, Risk Register) must exist *before* signature.
6. **In Thailand and government deals, calibrate Challenger tone.** Lead with humility and questions ("Our peers have learned…", "May I respectfully suggest…"), not assertions ("You are wrong to think…"). Direct confrontation destroys deals here.
7. **In government / SOE deals, the seller's influence ends when TOR is published.** Do all the consultative selling pre-TOR. Once the bid is open, conform exactly to spec; relationships do not unlock the committee.

## Coaching the seller (not just closing the deal)

After producing any deliverable, offer to apply the seller-DNA rubric at `references/09-seller-dna-rubric.md` to coach the user on the trait or behaviour that the deal is exposing. Examples:

- A weak Champion proof point → coach on building courage to challenge the buyer
- A vague business case → coach on financial literacy (ROI / NPV / sensitivity)
- A stuck government deal → coach on Procurement Act discipline and TOR pre-influence
- A thrown-over-the-wall handover → coach on accountability for customer outcomes

The goal is not just to win this deal. It is to develop a seller who can win the next ten.
