# Color Pairing & Gradient Guide (for slide design)

Practical rules for choosing colors and gradients in a .pptx deck.

## Picking a 2-color gradient
- Same-temperature pairs (cool+cool, warm+warm) read calm and professional. Cross-temperature (warm+cool) reads energetic/creative.
- Keep hue distance moderate. Very distant hues (e.g., green→magenta) can look muddy in the middle — check the mid stop.
- For text over a gradient, ensure contrast at the WORST stop (usually the lighter end for white text). Aim for WCAG AA (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large).

## Where to use gradients
- Title slide / section dividers: full-bleed gradient with large white or near-black title.
- Body slides: avoid full gradients behind text; use a thin top/side accent bar or a low-opacity wash.
- Data slides: keep background neutral; use one gradient color as the chart accent only.

## Building a deck palette from a gradient
1. Take the gradient start and end as your two brand accents.
2. Derive a neutral dark (near-black) and a light (near-white/off-white) for text and backgrounds.
3. Use one accent for emphasis (key numbers, action titles), the other sparingly for secondary highlights.
