## 1. Executive Summary

Design Thinking & Ideation is **not a "nice-to-have" creative method**. For an enterprise software solution provider, it is the strategic bridge that converts undefined customer pain into a qualified, differentiated, and defensible deal. It transforms the sales motion from "feature-push / price war" into "outcome co-creation / trusted advisor," which directly improves win rate, average contract value (ACV), and customer NPS.

Leading vendors already institutionalize this: **SAP AppHaus, Oracle Innovation Labs, Microsoft Envisioning Centers, IBM Garage, Deloitte Greenhouse, PwC BXT Studios**. A custom skill in this domain must replicate that rigor — adapted for your Thailand enterprise context and Big Four methodology.

## 2. Core Concept & Foundation

### 2.1 Definition
Design Thinking is a **human-centered, iterative, hypothesis-driven problem-solving methodology** that blends empathy (user needs), technology feasibility, and business viability. Ideation is its divergent-thinking phase where solution options are generated before convergent evaluation.

### 2.2 Canonical Frameworks
The skill should reference and remix these established models rather than invent new ones:

| Framework | Origin | Strength | When to use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **5-Stage Model** (Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test) | Stanford d.school | Teachable, linear, proven | Workshop default |
| **Double Diamond** (Discover → Define → Develop → Deliver) | UK Design Council | Visualizes divergence/convergence | Executive briefings |
| **IDEO HCD** (Hear → Create → Deliver) | IDEO.org | Field-research heavy | Voice-of-customer work |
| **IBM Enterprise Design Thinking** (Hills → Playbacks → Sponsor Users) | IBM | Scaled for enterprise teams | Multi-stakeholder programs |
| **SAP AppHaus Method** | SAP | Sales + implementation combined | ERP pre-sales workshops |
| **Google Design Sprint** (5-day) | GV | Time-boxed decision forcing | Deal acceleration |
| **PwC BXT (Business × eXperience × Technology)** | PwC | Big Four sales model | Proposal workshops |

### 2.3 Why it matters for Enterprise Software (specific, not generic)
- Enterprise deals involve **6–18 month cycles** and **5–12 stakeholders**; pain is rarely stated correctly in the RFP.
- Buyers increasingly require a **"Day-in-the-Life" demo**, not a feature matrix.
- **55–70% of enterprise software failures** trace to requirements misalignment, not technology — Design Thinking compresses that risk.
- It **justifies premium pricing**: the methodology itself becomes a billable asset (Innovation Workshops, Envisioning Sessions).
- In Thailand's relationship-based market, facilitated workshops accelerate **trust-building** with C-suite faster than demos.

## 3. Mandatory Components of the Skill

These are the non-negotiable building blocks the skill must deliver.

### 3.1 Empathize — Discovery & Listening
- Stakeholder persona mapping (CFO, CIO, COO, LOB leaders, IT, procurement, end-users)
- Empathy map canvas (Say / Think / Do / Feel)
- Customer journey map (AS-IS)
- Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD) interview script
- Voice-of-Customer techniques: contextual inquiry, shadowing, diary studies
- Pain / Gain / Job capture tied to Value Proposition Canvas
- Industry & regulatory context scan (especially Thailand PDPA, BoT, SEC, e-GP, GFMIS)

### 3.2 Define — Problem Framing
- Point-of-View (POV) statement: *"[User] needs [need] because [insight]"*
- How Might We (HMW) question generator
- Problem Tree / 5 Whys / Fishbone for root-cause
- MECE problem decomposition (McKinsey style)
- Opportunity sizing ($ impact, time-to-value)
- Success criteria & KPI definition
- Hypothesis statement (to be validated in ideation/testing)

### 3.3 Ideate — Divergent Solution Generation
- Brainstorming protocols: **Crazy 8s, 6-3-5 Brainwriting, SCAMPER, Mind Mapping, Worst Possible Idea, Analogous Inspiration**
- Silent ideation (to counter dominance bias in hierarchical Thai/APAC settings)
- Forced-connection cards (e.g., "apply Netflix logic to finance close")
- Reverse brainstorming (how would we *worsen* this?)
- Idea clustering & theme synthesis
- Convergence tools: **Dot voting, Impact-Effort Matrix (2×2), MoSCoW, RICE, Now/Next/Later**
- Solution hypothesis register

### 3.4 Prototype — Rapid Low-Fidelity Mockups
- Storyboards (6-panel scenario)
- Wireframes / click-through sketches
- Demo scripts tied to personas
- "Envisioning" slide prototypes (Oracle/SAP/MSFT style)
- Value Case prototype: ROI model, TCO comparison, Payback calc
- Proof-of-Concept (PoC) scope definition
- Architecture whiteboard (conceptual)

### 3.5 Test — Validation & Iteration
- Customer validation session script
- Usability testing (5-user rule)
- Business-value validation (the "so what" test)
- A/B framing of solution options for stakeholder choice
- Iteration decision gate (pivot / persevere / kill)
- Readout to sponsor

# PART B — Companion Skill: "Strategic Thinking"

## 11. Executive Summary — Why Strategic Thinking is the Missing Half

Design Thinking answers *"What should we build, and for whom?"* It is human-centered, bottom-up, and divergent-to-convergent.

**Strategic Thinking answers a different question: *"Where should we play, and how will we win?"*** It is market-centered, top-down, and options-generative with explicit trade-offs.

For an Enterprise Software Solution Provider operating in Thailand, **both are mandatory**:

| Without Strategic Thinking… | Without Design Thinking… |
|---|---|
| Beautifully designed solutions nobody wants | Strategically correct bets that fail on adoption |
| Chase the wrong accounts at the wrong time | Win deals that don't fit your long-term portfolio |
| Undercut on price with no defensible moat | Price-sensitive commoditized positioning |
| Lose to vendors with sharper positioning | Stakeholders disengage from feature-led pitches |

Top-tier firms institutionalize Strategic Thinking through **McKinsey Three Horizons, BCG Strategy Practice, Bain Net Promoter / Founder's Mentality, Deloitte Monitor, PwC Strategy&, Porter's Monitor Group legacy, and Playing to Win (Lafley & Martin)**. The custom skill must replicate that rigor, adapted for Thai enterprise software economics.

---

## 12. Core Concept & Canonical Frameworks

Strategic Thinking is the **disciplined mental activity** that answers five linked questions:

1. **Where to play?** — markets, segments, geographies, verticals, channels
2. **How to win?** — positioning, differentiation, moat, pricing
3. **What to prioritize?** — portfolio, resource allocation, capital
4. **How to adapt?** — scenarios, options, flexibility
5. **When to act?** — timing, sequencing, pace

The skill must be fluent in — and remix — the following canonical frameworks. It should **choose the right tool for the question**, not apply all of them to every problem.

| Framework | Best used for | Origin |
|---|---|---|
| Porter's 5 Forces | Industry attractiveness | Porter (HBS) |
| PESTEL | Macro environment scan | Aguilar |
| SWOT / TOWS | Quick internal+external read | Humphrey / Weihrich |
| VRIO | Resource-based capability audit | Barney |
| Value Chain | Internal activity diagnosis | Porter |
| Strategic Group Map | Competitive landscape mapping | Porter |
| **Playing to Win Choice Cascade** | Integrated strategy formulation | Lafley & Martin |
| Ansoff Matrix | Growth vector selection | Ansoff |
| BCG Growth-Share | Portfolio allocation | BCG |
| GE-McKinsey 9-box | Multi-factor portfolio | McKinsey |
| **Three Horizons** | Innovation portfolio balancing | McKinsey / Baghai |
| Blue Ocean / ERRC | Uncontested space creation | Kim & Mauborgne |
| **7 Powers** | Moat / durable advantage diagnosis | Hamilton Helmer |
| **Wardley Mapping** | Tech/platform evolution strategy | Simon Wardley |
| Business Model Canvas | Business model design | Osterwalder |
| Scenario Planning (2×2) | Uncertainty navigation | Shell / GBN |
| Cynefin | Problem-space domain diagnosis | Snowden |
| OODA Loop | Competitive response cycle | Boyd |
| Strategy Map + Balanced Scorecard | Strategy-to-execution bridge | Kaplan & Norton |
| Hedgehog Concept | Focus discipline | Jim Collins |
| Core Competency | Long-term capability lens | Hamel & Prahalad |
| Hoshin Kanri / OKRs | Cascaded execution | Japanese / Intel-Google |
| Pre-mortem | Risk surfacing | Gary Klein |
| Real Options | Investment flexibility | Myers / Copeland |

**Wardley Mapping deserves special emphasis** — it is the most IT-native strategy tool, and directly maps to enterprise software choices (commodity vs custom, cloud migration timing, platform bets, build vs buy vs partner).

## 13. Mandatory Components of the Strategic Thinking Skill

These are non-negotiable building blocks, grouped by strategic lens.

### 13.1 External Lens — Know the Field
- Industry structure analysis (Porter 5 Forces, with Thai market adjustments for oligopoly, government influence, local champions)
- Macro scan (PESTEL) — with Thailand-specific drivers: BOI incentives, EEC corridor, Thailand 4.0, Digital Government, PDPA
- Market sizing: TAM / SAM / SOM with conservative-base-stretch scenarios
- Competitor profiling + moat diagnosis (Strategic Group Map + 7 Powers)
- Customer segmentation & ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) definition
- Technology S-curves & disruption scan (Cloud, GenAI, Agentic AI, Low-code, Data Platforms, Edge)
- Partner/ecosystem landscape (Oracle, SAP, MSFT, AWS, Azure, GCP, SI partners)

### 13.2 Internal Lens — Know Thyself
- VRIO audit on people, IP, relationships, data, technology, process
- Capability maturity mapping (current vs needed)
- Value chain diagnostic (where we create vs destroy value)
- Financial performance read (revenue mix, margin stack, CAC/LTV, pipeline coverage)
- Culture & organization readiness
- Portfolio inventory of solutions, accounts, partnerships, IP

### 13.3 Strategic Choices — Where to Play / How to Win
- **Playing to Win Choice Cascade** as default synthesizer:
  - Winning Aspiration → Where to Play → How to Win → Capabilities → Management Systems
- Ansoff growth vector choice (market penetration, dev, product dev, diversification)
- Portfolio allocation (Three Horizons, BCG, 9-box)
- Blue Ocean ERRC grid (Eliminate / Reduce / Raise / Create)
- Partnership & alliance strategy (reseller, OEM, co-sell, JV)
- Build vs Buy vs Partner decision framing
- Pricing strategy options (value-based, per-user, per-module, outcome-based, subscription)
- Go-to-Market model (direct, channel, hybrid, platform)

### 13.4 Dynamic / Futures Thinking
- Scenario planning (2×2 matrix with four futures, wind-tunnel tests for each option)
- Wardley Mapping (evolution axis: genesis → custom → product → commodity)
- Real options framing (delay / abandon / scale / switch)
- Cynefin domain diagnosis (simple / complicated / complex / chaotic / disorder)
- Red team / war gaming of competitive responses
- Pre-mortem on chosen strategy

### 13.5 Strategy-to-Execution Bridge
- Strategy map (cause-effect chain across Kaplan-Norton perspectives)
- Balanced Scorecard (Financial / Customer / Process / Learning)
- OKR cascade — Horizon 1 quarterly, Horizon 2 annual, Horizon 3 multi-year
- Strategic initiatives portfolio (Now / Next / Later)
- Capital & capacity allocation plan
- Risk register with mitigation + owner + trigger
- Review cadence (strategy-in-action)

### 13.6 Decision Rigor
- Hypothesis tree / issue tree (MECE decomposition)
- Pyramid Principle for communication
- 2×2 trade-off visuals for any multi-option decision
- Cost-benefit / NPV / Real options math
- Assumption register with confidence levels
- Disconfirmation tests (what would change our mind)

## 16. How Strategic Thinking + Design Thinking Pair Together

The two skills are **complementary, not substitutes**. Either alone is incomplete.

| Dimension | Strategic Thinking | Design Thinking |
|---|---|---|
| Primary question | Where to play / How to win | What to build / For whom |
| Direction | Top-down (market → firm → offer) | Bottom-up (user → solution → scale) |
| Time horizon | 1–5 years, Horizon-tagged | Sprint to one year |
| Unit of analysis | Market, firm, portfolio, account | User, journey, feature, moment |
| Core skill | Pattern recognition, synthesis, trade-off | Empathy, divergent ideation, prototyping |
| Key output | Choice cascade, strategy map, scenario set | Prototype, workshop kit, value case |
| Risk if missing | Build the wrong thing | Build for the wrong audience |

### 16.1 Integrated Flow for Enterprise Software Sales

1. **ST** — Define Where to Play → Horizon 1/2/3 account targets and vertical priorities.
2. **ST** — Define How to Win → positioning, moat, pricing, partner model.
3. **DT** — Discovery with qualified target accounts (empathy, journey, pain).
4. **DT** — Ideate solution with HMW, Crazy 8s, convergence.
5. **ST** — Strategic-fit gate → does this opportunity align with Where/How? Kill, adjust, or accelerate.
6. **DT** — Prototype + validate with sponsor users.
7. **ST** — Scale decision → invest in offering, productize, or one-off.
8. **DT** — Deliver the workshop readout; **ST** — deliver the portfolio update.

### 16.2 Handoff Contract Between the Skills
- DT → ST: customer pain insights, unmet jobs, willingness-to-pay signals, adoption blockers.
- ST → DT: target accounts list, ICP definition, positioning guardrails, pricing envelope, non-goals.

## 20. FINAL Strategic Thinking Requirement Specification

The Strategic Thinking skill inherits the locked context from the Design Thinking Best-in-Question session (Pichai, 22-Apr-2026) and adds ST-specific requirements.

### 20.1 Inherited from Design Thinking session
- Full-lifecycle coverage (Prospect → Delivery → Internal Innovation)

- Prompt-driven output format (pptx / docx / xlsx / pdf / canvas)
- Success criteria: executive-grade quality, deal progression impact, Socratic inquiry

### 20.2 Strategic Thinking–specific additions
1. **Minimum three strategic options** before any recommendation — single-answer output is rejected.
2. **Explicit trade-offs** — every option must state what is being traded away ("to win here, we accept losing there").
3. **Horizon tagging** — every initiative labeled Horizon 1 / 2 / 3 with time range.
4. **Hypothesis tree before conclusions** — MECE decomposition of the strategic question with disconfirmation tests.
5. **Scenario treatment for volatile assumptions** — minimum two scenarios (base, stress) when market/tech uncertainty is material.
6. **Execution bridge mandatory** — OKR cascade + roadmap + capital allocation + risk register for every strategy.
7. **Pairing protocol with Design Thinking skill** — explicit handoff inputs and outputs defined.
8. **Wardley Mapping support** — because IT strategy without Wardley is incomplete.

### 20.3 Interaction Modes (the skill must support all three)
- **Advisory mode** — responds to a strategy question with an options-and-trade-offs answer.
- **Socratic mode** — asks probing questions (*"Where will you NOT play? Who are you happy to lose to? What would have to be true for this to work?"*) before answering.
- **Workshop-facilitation mode** — produces a full session kit (agenda, frameworks, canvases, facilitator guide, readout template) for a strategy offsite.
