# Workshop Facilitation Guide

Building and running a runnable workshop kit for enterprise software envisioning, discovery, ideation, and strategy sessions. Adapt the pattern to the time box, audience, and discipline (DT, ST, or both).

## Master Workshop Anatomy

Every workshop — whether 90 minutes or 3 days — uses the same five-part spine:

1. **Open** — set context, build rapport, agree on outcomes
2. **Diverge** — generate breadth (insights, options, ideas)
3. **Converge** — narrow to priorities with explicit criteria
4. **Commit** — name owners, dates, and next actions
5. **Close** — reinforce decisions, thank contributors, set follow-up

Time-box each part. A common split is 10 / 35 / 35 / 15 / 5 percent.

## Pre-Workshop Preparation

### Strategic fit check (ST)
Before any customer workshop, confirm:
- Account is on the target list
- ICP fit is at least partial
- Positioning and pricing envelope are known
- Non-goals are understood
- A senior sponsor owns the outcome

If any of these are missing, delay the workshop or reduce its ambition.

### Stakeholder intelligence
Collect and synthesize:
- Org chart for attendees
- Prior conversations (CRM notes, email threads, previous proposals)
- Publicly stated strategy (annual report, investor day, CEO interviews)
- Recent wins / losses / organizational moments (M&A, new exec, regulatory action)

### Agenda design
Write the agenda backwards from the desired output:
- What artifact must exist at the end? (workshop report, signed commitments, prototype storyboard, proposal brief)
- Which activities produce each section of that artifact?
- Which attendees are required for each activity?
- What is the fallback if a key person cannot attend?

### Pre-reads
For senior audiences, send a 1-page pre-read 3–5 days before:
- Context paragraph
- Workshop objective
- Agenda at a glance
- What we are asking of them (decision, input, endorsement)
- Pre-work, if any (max 20 minutes)

### Logistics
- Room setup — U-shape for discussion, cabaret for small groups, theater for large plenary
- Wall space for sticky notes / posters (rule: 2m of wall per 4 attendees)
- Materials kit — sticky notes, Sharpies, dot stickers, timer, flipcharts
- Digital alternative — Miro / Mural / FigJam template pre-built
- Catering — respect dietary and cultural norms; in Thailand, tea and light snacks during breaks

## Facilitation Principles

### Rule 1 — The facilitator owns the process, not the content
The client owns the content. If the facilitator starts giving answers, the workshop has failed. Facilitator contributions belong in debrief sessions, not the plenary.

### Rule 2 — Make the thinking visible
Everything said gets written on a sticky, flipchart, or shared digital canvas. "If it is not on the wall, it did not happen."

### Rule 3 — Time-box ruthlessly
Every activity has a timer. When time is up, stop. Over-running one activity starves a later one.

### Rule 4 — Equalize voice
Default to silent + written ideation for the first pass of any activity. Talkers dominate; silent ideation reveals the quiet ones. Especially critical in Thai hierarchical contexts.

### Rule 5 — Disagreement is data
Surface disagreement; do not suppress it. When the room is too polite, ask *"what would have to be true for this to not work?"*

### Rule 6 — Park, do not dismiss
When an off-topic or too-big question comes up, park it on a "parking lot" flipchart. Acknowledge, preserve, and return to it in the close.

### Rule 7 — Name the discipline
Tell the room *"we are now in divergent mode — no critique"* or *"we are now in convergent mode — we need criteria and trade-offs"*. Mode clarity prevents premature judgment.

### Rule 8 — Energy management
Watch the room. Break before minds check out. Use physical movement (stand-ups, wall walks, gallery reviews) every 45–60 minutes.

## Standard Activity Library

### Opening activities (10 min each)
- **Hopes & Fears** — two columns, sticky notes, read aloud
- **Success criteria** — "how will we know this was worth it?"
- **Context graffiti** — one sticky per person on "what is happening in our market right now"

### Empathy & discovery (DT)
- **Empathy interviews** — 45-min semi-structured, 2-person teams (interviewer + note-taker)
- **Stakeholder map** — circles of influence, proximity = power
- **Journey map (AS-IS)** — stages × actions × thoughts × feelings × pain/gain
- **Jobs-to-be-done** — functional / emotional / social jobs per persona
- **Day in the life** — 1-page narrative of a typical user day

### Problem framing (DT)
- **POV statements** — "[user] needs [need] because [insight]"
- **How Might We** — reframe POVs as questions; generate 10–15 HMWs
- **HMW prioritization** — impact × feasibility 2x2

### Ideation (DT)
- **Crazy 8s** — 8 ideas, 8 minutes, one per folded square
- **Worst Idea / Best Idea** — provoke by inverting
- **SCAMPER** — substitute, combine, adapt, modify, put to other use, eliminate, reverse
- **Analogous inspiration** — "how does [other industry] solve this?"
- **Tech-forward** — "if AI could do anything here, what would it do?"

### Convergence (DT + ST)
- **Dot voting** — 3 dots per person, reveal clustering, then discuss
- **Impact-Effort matrix** — quick wins vs. big bets vs. fill-ins vs. money pits
- **MoSCoW** — Must / Should / Could / Won't
- **Storyboard** — 6–8 frames showing user experience

### Strategic framing (ST)
- **Choice Cascade** — aspiration, where to play, how to win, capabilities, management systems (Playing to Win)
- **Five Forces scan** — rapid assessment of industry structure
- **PESTEL** — macro-environment canvas
- **Three Horizons** — H1 defend, H2 build, H3 explore
- **Wardley Map** — value chain on evolution axis
- **Scenario 2x2** — two most uncertain and most important axes

### Value case (ST + DT)
- **Value tree** — revenue lever × cost lever × risk lever × option value
- **Cost-of-inaction** — what breaks in 12 / 24 / 36 months
- **Why Invest / Why Now / Why Us** — three-column one-pager

### Closing activities (5–15 min each)
- **Commitments board** — owner × action × date
- **Next conversation** — date + attendees + agenda for the follow-up session
- **Plus / Delta** — what worked, what to change (for continuous improvement)
- **Gratitude round** — each person names one thing they value from the session

## Time-Box Templates

### 90-minute discovery (C-suite touchpoint)
- Open (10) — hopes/fears, agenda
- Context share (15) — exec pain and priorities
- Stakeholder map + AS-IS journey (25) — live co-creation
- HMW generation (15) — top 5 questions to pursue
- Prioritization + next steps (20) — dot vote + commit
- Close (5)

### Half-day (4 hours) envisioning
- Open (20)
- Empathy + journey AS-IS (60)
- POV + HMW (45)
- Ideation (Crazy 8s + SCAMPER) (45)
- Impact-effort + top 3 concepts (30)
- Why-Invest/Now/Us draft (30)
- Close + commitments (10)

### 1-day ideation
- Open (30)
- Discovery block (90) — empathy + journey + stakeholder map
- Problem framing (60) — POV + HMW + prioritize
- Break for lunch (60)
- Ideation block (90) — Crazy 8s + analogous + tech-forward
- Convergence (60) — impact-effort + top 3 storyboards
- Value case draft (30)
- Close (20)

### 3-day design sprint (Google-style adapted)
- Day 1 — Map (AS-IS) + Target (TO-BE ambition)
- Day 2 — Sketch + Decide (solution concepts)
- Day 3 — Prototype + Validate (stakeholder users)

### Strategy offsite (1-day ST intensive)
- Open (30) — aspiration restatement
- External (90) — PESTEL + 5 Forces + competitor scan
- Internal (60) — VRIO + capability map
- Portfolio (90) — 3 Horizons + build-buy-partner
- Trade-offs (60) — options × criteria
- Choice (60) — Choice Cascade commit
- Close (30)

## Remote / Hybrid Considerations

- Miro / Mural / FigJam templates pre-built for every activity
- Camera-on expectation for small groups, camera-optional for plenary
- One facilitator runs the room, a second runs the board
- Shorter blocks (45 min max) than in-person
- Explicit hand-raise protocol to avoid cross-talk
- Break every 50 minutes

## Thai-Specific Adjustments (Quick Reference)

- Open with relationship time (15 min minimum for senior stakeholders)
- Silent + written ideation for 30%+ of ideation time
- Small-group breakouts before plenary discussion (safer for junior voices)
- "Take-away for consultation" time — do not force final decisions
- Thai language for empathy / emotional content, English for analytical / framework content
- Honor seniority in seating and speaking order

See `thailand-localization.md` for the full Thai facilitation playbook.

## Post-Workshop Deliverables

### Immediate (within 24 hours)
- Photo / screenshot archive of all boards and artifacts
- Action log with owners and dates
- Thank-you note to attendees

### Within 3–5 business days
- Workshop report — narrative + synthesized artifacts
- Updated journey map, personas, POVs, HMWs as relevant
- Why-Invest / Why-Now / Why-Us draft
- Proposed next meeting agenda

### Within 2 weeks
- Proposal or solution envisioning document
- Updated account strategy
- Pipeline update in CRM

## Facilitator Self-Check (before running)

- [ ] Agenda is backwards-designed from the target artifact
- [ ] Every activity has a time-box
- [ ] Room / digital canvas is set up
- [ ] Stakeholder intelligence is synthesized
- [ ] Sponsor has approved scope and attendees
- [ ] Strategic fit check is complete (for customer workshops)
- [ ] Language mix is decided
- [ ] Post-workshop deliverables are planned
- [ ] A second person owns notes / photos
