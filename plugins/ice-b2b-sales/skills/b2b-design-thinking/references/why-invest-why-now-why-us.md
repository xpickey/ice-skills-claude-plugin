# Why Invest / Why Now / Why Us

The three-question narrative that wins enterprise software deals. Activate this whenever the prompt signals sales pitching, proposal creation, exec readout, business case, or pitching to the customer.

## When to Activate

**Mandatory** when the prompt contains or implies:
- "pitch", "proposal", "proposed", "pitch deck"
- "present to customer", "exec readout", "C-suite presentation"
- "why should they buy", "justify investment", "business case"
- "sell this", "close this deal"

**Optional** but recommended for:
- Solution envisioning that will feed a proposal
- Account strategy that requires executive sponsorship
- Internal investment justification

**Not needed** for pure working-level artifacts (empathy maps, journey maps, ideation boards, internal strategy hypothesis trees).

## Structure — Three Questions, One Narrative

The three questions must be answered in this order and connected as a story:

### 1. Why Invest — The cost of inaction

The client does not decide "should we do this project?" They decide "should we allocate capital to this vs everything else?" Give them the reason.

Components:
- **Cost of inaction** — what breaks if they do nothing in 12 / 24 / 36 months
- **Opportunity size** — revenue uplift, cost takeout, risk avoidance, strategic option value (use concrete numbers, even if ranges)
- **Strategic alignment** — how this advances the client's stated strategy (quote their 2027 plan, investor day, CEO letter)

Template:
> *"If [client] does nothing, [specific consequence] within [timeframe]. The investment unlocks [concrete value]: [revenue lever], [cost lever], [risk lever]. This directly supports [client's stated strategic priority]."*

### 2. Why Now — The timing pressure

Capital is scarce. There must be a reason this year, not next.

Timing drivers, ranked by typical potency:
1. **Regulatory deadline** — PDPA enforcement, IFRS adoption, Cloud Standard 2567, ECC sunset 2027, new tax / procurement rules
2. **Contract expiry / license renewal** — forcing a decision window
3. **Competitive move** — competitor just implemented, now at parity disadvantage
4. **Technology S-curve** — AI / cloud / low-code inflection; first movers capture disproportionate value
5. **Cost curve change** — SaaS pricing shift, exchange rate, infrastructure cost change
6. **Organizational moment** — new CEO / CFO / CIO, M&A integration, IPO prep
7. **Macro pressure** — inflation, supply chain, labor shortage

Template:
> *"The window is [timeframe]. [Trigger event] means delaying creates [specific penalty]. Moving now captures [specific advantage] that becomes unavailable later."*

Thai-specific Why-Now drivers: fiscal year cycles, BOI window, EEC incentives, Thailand 4.0 milestones, upcoming government administration changes.

### 3. Why Us — The defensible differentiation

Why pick our firm / our solution over competitors (or DIY)?

Components (choose 3–5 most relevant):
- **Local track record** — Thai / APAC references, size-comparable clients, vertical depth
- **Vendor certifications and partnership tier** — Oracle Cloud Elite, SAP Platinum, MSFT Solutions Partner, etc.
- **Bilingual and cultural fit** — Thai-speaking team, PDPA / Cloud Standard 2567 fluency
- **Methodology** — proven frameworks, design thinking, Big Four rigor, change management
- **Accelerators / IP** — pre-built templates, industry solutions, AI accelerators
- **People** — named senior consultants the client has met or can meet
- **Commercial construct** — fixed-price commitments, outcome-based pricing, risk-share

Template:
> *"[Our firm] delivers this because [3–5 concrete differentiators]. Competitor X offers [X] but lacks [gap]. We commit to [specific outcome / SLA / guarantee]."*

## Connecting the Three Into a Narrative

The three answers must form one story, not three separate bullets:

> *"You have until [date] to [regulatory / competitive / contractual pressure]. Doing nothing costs [quantified risk]. The solution unlocks [specific value]. We are uniquely positioned to deliver because [key differentiator], with [reference] to prove it, and we commit to [outcome / SLA]."*

## Output Formats

### One-slide version (C-suite default)
Single slide, three columns:

| Why Invest | Why Now | Why Us |
|---|---|---|
| Cost of inaction | Timing trigger | Differentiation |
| Opportunity size | Window closes | Track record |
| Strategic fit | Penalty for delay | Commitment |

### Narrative-paragraph version (proposal body)
One paragraph per question, 3–5 sentences each, connected by transition phrases.

### Executive-memo version (one-pager)
Situation → Complication → Question → Answer → Three grouped reasons → Next action.

### Talk-track version (sales conversation script)
- Opening (30 sec): *"The reason we are recommending this now is…"*
- Why Invest (60 sec): cost of inaction + opportunity + strategic fit
- Why Now (60 sec): trigger + window + penalty
- Why Us (60 sec): 3 differentiators + proof + commitment
- Close (30 sec): ask for the next step

## Bilingual Considerations

When delivering to a Thai audience, tailor the emphasis:

- **Why Invest** — emphasize risk reduction and audit defensibility alongside financial uplift. Thai CFOs weight downside avoidance heavily.
- **Why Now** — tie to concrete Thai triggers (fiscal year, regulatory, BOI, political change). Vague "market is moving" does not land.
- **Why Us** — emphasize local presence, Thai references, past performance with comparable organizations, and named senior Thai leadership.

For bilingual parallel output, keep the structure identical in both languages; do not translate idioms literally.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Feature list disguised as value** — "Cloud, AI, mobile, analytics" is not a Why-Invest; it is a what
- **Generic Why-Now** — "digital transformation is here" is not a trigger; "your ECC support ends December 2027" is
- **Weak Why-Us** — "experienced team, strong partnerships" is boilerplate; name specific people, specific references, specific commitments
- **Three separate pitches** — the three questions must braid into one story
- **No next action** — always close with the specific request: meeting, PoC, pilot scope, decision date

## Self-Check Before Sending

- [ ] Is the cost of inaction quantified or at least concrete?
- [ ] Is the timing trigger specific and dated?
- [ ] Is each Why-Us differentiator concrete (not generic)?
- [ ] Does the narrative read as one story?
- [ ] Is there a named next action?
- [ ] Is the language appropriate for the audience seniority?
- [ ] For Thai audiences — are local triggers and references used?
