# Strategy Map Template

Kaplan & Norton's one-page view linking strategic objectives across four perspectives: Financial, Customer, Internal Process, Learning & Growth. A coherence tool — ensures every operational metric connects to financial outcomes.

## The Four Perspectives (top to bottom)

### 1. Financial (outcomes)
What does success look like in dollars / baht?
- Revenue growth
- Margin expansion
- Cash / working capital
- Return on capital

### 2. Customer (drivers of financial)
What must we deliver to customers to achieve the financial outcomes?
- Value proposition
- Customer satisfaction / NPS
- Retention / churn
- Share of wallet

### 3. Internal Process (how we deliver customer value)
What must we be excellent at internally?
- Innovation pipeline
- Operational efficiency
- Quality / reliability
- Regulatory / compliance excellence

### 4. Learning & Growth (the foundation)
What capabilities and culture underpin everything?
- Human capital (skills, people)
- Information capital (data, systems)
- Organization capital (culture, leadership, alignment)

## The Causal Chain

Each perspective drives the one above it:

```
       FINANCIAL (outcomes)
              ↑
         CUSTOMER (drivers)
              ↑
      INTERNAL PROCESS (enablers)
              ↑
      LEARNING & GROWTH (foundation)
```

Every strategic objective must be causally linked upward. If an L&G objective does not trace to an Internal Process that drives a Customer outcome that delivers a Financial result, it is noise.

## Example — Thai Oracle Practice Strategy Map

### Financial
- Grow practice revenue 25% YoY
- Maintain gross margin ≥ 35%
- Generate positive cash within Q1 of each fiscal year

### Customer
- 5+ reference-able Thai mid-market Fusion Cloud go-lives per year
- NPS ≥ 50 from client executive sponsors
- 80%+ customer retention into Phase 2 / extension work

### Internal Process
- Deliver projects on fixed-price with ≤10% variance
- Publish 3 industry accelerators per year (BOI-defensible templates)
- Achieve Oracle Cloud Elite tier by year-end

### Learning & Growth
- Hire / certify 5 senior Oracle Fusion consultants
- Build a Thai Oracle Academy for junior talent pipeline
- Embed design thinking / Big Four rigor as practice norms

## Workshop Run (half-day)

1. Confirm strategy (aspiration, Where / How) — 30 min
2. Draft Financial objectives — 30 min
3. Draft Customer objectives that drive Financial — 45 min
4. Draft Internal Process objectives that enable Customer — 45 min
5. Draft Learning & Growth objectives that underpin Internal Process — 45 min
6. Trace causal links; kill orphan objectives — 45 min
7. Assign owners and metrics — 30 min

## Pairing with Balanced Scorecard

The strategy map shows the causality; the balanced scorecard provides the KPIs. One objective → 1–3 KPIs with targets, initiatives, and owners.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Everything in one perspective** — usually Financial-heavy; push for breadth
- **No causal links** — objectives sit in silos, not a system
- **Too many objectives** — 5–7 per perspective max; beyond that, focus is lost
- **Vague L&G** — "build capabilities" without specifying which ones

## Quality Bar

- Four perspectives populated with 3–7 objectives each
- Causal arrows traced from L&G to Financial
- No orphan objectives
- Each objective has an owner and KPI
- Strategy map visible on one page
