# POV & HMW Template

Point-of-View (POV) statements convert empathy insights into sharp problem statements. How-Might-We (HMW) questions convert those problems into ideation prompts. Use at the Define phase of Design Thinking, before any ideation.

## Why This Matters

Good ideation depends on good framing. Ideation against vague problems produces vague ideas. POV and HMW are the bridge from insight to opportunity.

## POV Statement Formula

```
[USER] needs [NEED] because [INSIGHT]
```

**User** — a specific persona, not "the customer" or "the business"
**Need** — a verb phrase describing what they need to do
**Insight** — a non-obvious truth from empathy work that explains the why

### Examples

Bad: *"Customers need a faster invoice process because they want efficiency."*
- Generic user, generic need, no insight

Good: *"The AP manager at a Thai mid-market manufacturer needs to close the monthly books in 5 days (not 12) because the CEO's board meeting falls on day 7 and late numbers mean bonus clawback for the whole finance team."*
- Specific user, measurable need, sharp insight with consequence

### POV Quality Checks
- [ ] User is a specific persona
- [ ] Need is actionable (can be addressed)
- [ ] Insight is non-obvious
- [ ] A stranger reading the POV would understand the urgency

## HMW Question Formula

```
How might we [VERB] [USER] [DESIRED OUTCOME] [CONTEXT/CONSTRAINT]?
```

**Verb** — enable, help, empower, reduce, eliminate, transform, unify
**User** — same specific persona as POV
**Outcome** — the value they want
**Context** — constraint or opportunity that shapes the solution space

### Goldilocks Sizing

- **Too broad**: *"How might we improve the finance function?"* — boundless
- **Too narrow**: *"How might we redesign the invoice approval button?"* — solution-masquerading-as-problem
- **Just right**: *"How might we enable the AP manager to close by day 5 without adding headcount?"* — actionable, bounded, preserves solution space

### Examples from POV to HMW

POV: *"The Thai government CFO needs to defend every budget line to OAG auditors because one finding can delay the next fiscal year's allocation."*

HMWs:
- How might we give the CFO real-time audit-ready evidence for every transaction?
- How might we pre-empt the auditor's questions at the point of data entry?
- How might we turn audit defense from a scramble into a routine?

## Generating HMWs from One POV

For each POV, generate 5–10 HMWs by varying the approach:
- **Amp up the good** — HMW increase [positive thing]?
- **Remove the bad** — HMW eliminate [negative thing]?
- **Explore the opposite** — HMW make [current weakness] a strength?
- **Question the assumption** — HMW work without [current constraint]?
- **Analogy** — HMW apply [other industry pattern] here?
- **Break into parts** — HMW solve just [one dimension] first?

## Prioritizing HMWs

Run an impact × feasibility dot vote:
- Each participant gets 3 dots for high-impact HMWs and 3 for high-feasibility
- HMWs with votes on both axes become ideation candidates
- Aim for 3–5 HMWs to ideate against in the next phase

## Template for Workshop Output

```
INSIGHT 1: [from empathy work]
  POV: [persona] needs [need] because [insight]
  HMW 1: How might we...
  HMW 2: How might we...
  HMW 3: How might we...

INSIGHT 2: [from empathy work]
  POV: ...
  HMW 1-3: ...

INSIGHT 3: [from empathy work]
  POV: ...
  HMW 1-3: ...

SHORTLIST (top 5 HMWs for ideation):
  1.
  2.
  3.
  4.
  5.
```

## Quality Bar

- Every POV traces back to an empathy data point (interview quote, journey pain, observation)
- Every HMW preserves solution space (does not presuppose the answer)
- Shortlisted HMWs pass the Goldilocks test
- HMWs are concrete enough to generate distinct ideation directions
