# Persona Canvas Template

A structured profile of an enterprise buyer persona or end user. Richer than an empathy map; focuses on role context, decision criteria, and buying behavior for enterprise software.

## When to Use
- Sales account planning
- ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) definition
- Content / enablement targeting
- Discovery workshops where the team needs shared understanding of who we serve

## Canvas Structure

### 1. Identity
- Name / archetype (e.g., "Chief Transformation Officer at mid-market Thai manufacturer")
- Title, function, reporting line
- Seniority (C / VP / Director / Manager / IC)
- Tenure in role / in company
- Background (career path, functional origin)

### 2. Context
- Industry, sub-sector
- Company size (revenue, employees)
- Geography
- Current systems / vendors in use
- Recent organizational moments (M&A, new exec, regulatory pressure)

### 3. Goals & Metrics
- Top 3 business objectives for this year
- How they are measured (KPI, bonus structure)
- What a promotion / win looks like
- What a career-ending failure looks like

### 4. Pains & Frustrations
- Current unresolved problems
- Tried-and-failed solutions
- Organizational blockers (budget, politics, capacity)

### 5. Jobs to Be Done
- Functional jobs (tasks to complete)
- Emotional jobs (feelings they seek)
- Social jobs (how they want to be perceived)

### 6. Decision-Making Behavior
- Role in the buying committee (Economic Buyer / Technical Buyer / User Champion / Coach / Blocker)
- Trust signals they value (references, certifications, analyst reports, peer recommendations)
- Risk tolerance
- Preferred channels (email, LinkedIn, phone, in-person, events)
- Preferred content formats (slides, case studies, demos, whitepapers, peer panels)

### 7. Objections Likely to Raise
- Cost / budget
- Timing / sequencing
- Risk / disruption
- Fit / relevance
- Vendor lock-in
- Change capacity

### 8. What Would Make Them Say Yes
- Specific proof points needed
- Commercial constructs that reduce their risk
- References they would trust
- Executive endorsement that would unblock

### 9. Why This Persona Matters to Us
- Role in the deal cycle
- Typical ACV / LTV impact
- Where we tend to win / lose with them

## Example — Thai Public Sector CIO (DGA-facing agency)

- Identity: DGA-facing government CIO, 20+ years in public sector IT
- Context: 5,000-person agency, GFMIS user, e-GP submitter, PDPA scope
- Goals: Thailand 4.0 compliance, OAG audit readiness, staff productivity
- Pains: legacy system stability, vendor dependency, cybersecurity exposure
- JTBD: functional — modernize without risk; emotional — be seen as prudent modernizer; social — respected by peer agencies
- Decision behavior: Technical Buyer who must convince Economic Buyer (Permanent Secretary) and the procurement committee
- Objections: cost (capex vs. opex), Cloud Standard 2567 fit, PDPA exposure, local support
- Yes triggers: Thai references from comparable agencies, DGA-endorsed vendor status, fixed-price commitment, Thai language support
- Why matters: gatekeeper for entire agency digital spend

## Quality Bar

- Grounded in real interviews / CRM data / market research (not invented)
- Specific to the account or vertical, not generic
- Objections are realistic (not strawmen)
- "Why this persona matters" ties to commercial reality
