# PESTEL Template

Scanning the macro-environment: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal factors. Use to surface external forces before deeper strategic analysis.

## The Six Lenses

### P — Political
- Government policies, stability, transitions
- Trade policy, tariffs
- Public sector priorities (Thailand 4.0, Digital Government)
- Political risk (elections, protests)

### E — Economic
- GDP growth, inflation, interest rates
- Exchange rates (THB / USD / EUR / JPY / CNY)
- Labor market conditions
- Capital availability, credit cycle
- Industry-specific indicators (manufacturing PMI, consumer spending)

### S — Social
- Demographics (aging, urbanization)
- Cultural shifts, values, attitudes
- Education and skills landscape
- Consumer behavior changes
- Workforce expectations (remote work, ESG)

### T — Technological
- Emerging technologies (AI, cloud, low-code, IoT)
- Technology adoption rates
- Infrastructure (5G, data centers, broadband)
- R&D intensity in relevant sectors
- Cybersecurity landscape

### E — Environmental
- Climate / sustainability pressures
- ESG regulation and reporting
- Resource availability (energy, water, rare earths)
- Circular economy expectations

### L — Legal
- Regulatory frameworks (PDPA, Cybersecurity Act, Cloud Standard 2567)
- Employment law
- Tax policy (e-Tax, transfer pricing)
- Industry-specific regulation (BoT, SEC, OIC for FinServ)
- IP, contract, and competition law

## Example — Thai Enterprise Software Market 2026–2028

| Lens | Current state | Direction | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Political | Post-election gov focus on digital infra, BOI incentives for manufacturing | ↑ favorable | Accelerate government and BOI-aligned deals |
| Economic | Modest GDP growth 2.5–3%, THB volatile, SME capex cautious | → mixed | Fixed-price offers hedge against SME conservatism |
| Social | Skills shortage in Oracle / SAP consultants; aging senior talent | ↑ concerning | Invest in local training / academy partnerships |
| Technological | GenAI mainstreaming; low-code gaining at SME; cloud mandates | ↑ strong | Lead with AI + low-code extension narrative |
| Environmental | ESG disclosure mandate coming; Scope 3 reporting pressure | ↑ rising | Bundle ESG reporting module; partner with sustainability firm |
| Legal | PDPA enforcement intensifying; Cloud Standard 2567 mandatory for CII | ↑ binding | Make compliance the price of entry; turn into Why-Us |

## Turning PESTEL Into Strategy

Each factor should answer: **so what for us?**

Good PESTEL output names:
- 2–3 tailwinds to accelerate into
- 1–2 headwinds to mitigate
- 1–2 wild cards to monitor
- At least one Why-Now trigger for sales conversations

## Common Pitfalls

- **Laundry list** — dozens of bullets, no prioritization
- **No direction arrow** — static snapshot, not a trend view
- **No "so what"** — data without implication
- **Too global** — always tie to the specific market / segment

## Thailand-Specific Additions

Add these to the default PESTEL when scanning Thai market:
- Fiscal year cycle (Oct–Sep for government)
- BOI incentive windows
- EEC / special economic zone policies
- Monarchy / political transition signals (sensitive — handle factually)
- Labor Ministry directives on foreign workers
- Ministry of Digital Economy and Society (MDES) priorities

## Quality Bar

- Each lens has at least 2 substantive entries
- Direction arrow on every entry
- "So what" implication articulated
- Tailwinds / headwinds / wild cards clearly named
- Links to Why-Now triggers in the sales narrative
