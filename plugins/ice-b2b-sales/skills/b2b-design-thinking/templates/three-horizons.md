# Three Horizons Template

McKinsey's portfolio framework for balancing today's performance with tomorrow's options. Every enterprise software offering, account, and capital allocation decision can be horizon-tagged.

## The Three Horizons

### H1 — Defend and Extend the Core
- Today's revenue and profit engine
- Mature, proven, cash-generating
- Time horizon: 0–12 months
- Objective: operate efficiently, defend share, optimize margin

### H2 — Build Emerging Businesses
- Growing businesses not yet at scale
- Proof points exist; scaling is the challenge
- Time horizon: 12–36 months
- Objective: invest capital and talent to scale

### H3 — Create Viable Options
- New experiments, research bets, early-stage
- High uncertainty, low current revenue
- Time horizon: 24–60+ months
- Objective: learn, optionality, portfolio protection

## Horizon Tags by Enterprise Software Examples

| Example | H1 (defend) | H2 (build) | H3 (explore) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Oracle practice | EBS support & upgrade | Fusion Cloud implementation | AI agents on Fusion |
| SAP practice | ECC maintenance | RISE with SAP migration | Joule + agentic workflows |
| Thai public sector | e-GP implementations | GFMIS enhancements | Generative citizen services |
| Sales motion | Renewals / expansions | New logo land in ICP | New market (Indochina) |

## Capital Allocation Rule of Thumb

- H1: 60–70% of resources (people, money, attention)
- H2: 20–25%
- H3: 5–15%

Tune to the maturity of the portfolio. A young firm tilts to H2/H3; a mature firm tilts to H1.

## Workshop Canvas

For each initiative:
- Revenue today
- Revenue in 3 years (best / likely / worst)
- Investment required
- Risk profile
- Horizon tag
- Resource allocation decision

## Horizon Diagnostic Questions

Ask the leadership team:
1. Where is most of our attention going today? (Often the answer is "all H1")
2. Are our H2 investments on track to replace H1 revenue?
3. Are our H3 bets producing learning, even when they fail commercially?
4. Have we killed any H3 bets that were not working? (If not, you are hoarding, not exploring)

## Horizons Over Time

Horizons are not static. A successful H3 bet becomes H2, then H1. A declining H1 becomes a burning platform — invest in H2 replacement, not H1 life-support.

Diagram:
```
Value
  |   H1 (declining)          H2 (growing)          H3 (emerging)
  |   ────────────────        ──────                ────
  |       \                   /                     /
  |        \                 /                     /
  |         \               /                     /
  |          \             /                     /
  |           \           /                     /
  |            ──────────                       ─────
  |_________________________________________________
                        Time
```

Rule: H1 cash funds H2 investment; H2 success displaces H1. Without H2/H3 pipeline, H1 decline = firm decline.

## Workshop Run (half-day)

1. List all current initiatives / accounts / offerings (30 min)
2. Tag each as H1 / H2 / H3 (30 min)
3. Score revenue, investment, risk for each (60 min)
4. Aggregate portfolio view — current allocation (30 min)
5. Compare to target allocation — where are the gaps? (30 min)
6. Decide moves — invest / shift / kill (60 min)

## Common Pitfalls

- **Everything is H1** — the portfolio has no future
- **H3 ideas never killed** — hoarding without learning
- **H2 underfunded** — the "bridge to tomorrow" is not being built
- **Mis-tagging** — calling a standard product rollout "H3 innovation" to justify resources

## Thailand Context

- Thai enterprises often over-index on H1 (risk aversion, hierarchy)
- H2 investments often blocked by board caution
- H3 benefits from partnerships (universities, accelerators, BOI-backed initiatives)
- Use BOI / EEC / Thailand 4.0 narratives to unlock H2/H3 capital

## Quality Bar

- Every current initiative horizon-tagged
- Current vs. target allocation quantified
- At least one "kill" or "shift" decision named
- H2/H3 pipeline named with milestones
- Capital allocation change explicit, not aspirational
