# Impact-Effort Matrix Template

A 2x2 convergence tool for prioritizing ideas, features, or opportunities by business impact versus implementation effort. Fast, visual, and defensible.

## When to Use
- After an ideation session with 15+ raw ideas
- Prioritizing a backlog with leadership
- Making a build / buy / defer / kill decision
- Setting a roadmap across horizons

## Canvas

```
              HIGH IMPACT
                  │
    BIG BETS      │    QUICK WINS
    (invest)      │    (do now)
                  │
 LOW EFFORT ──────┼────── HIGH EFFORT
                  │
    FILL-INS      │    MONEY PITS
    (when idle)   │    (avoid)
                  │
              LOW IMPACT
```

## Quadrant Definitions

### Quick Wins (high impact / low effort)
**Do now.** These pay for the whole session.
- Rules-based automation already available in the current platform
- Low-code apps on Power Platform / BTP / VBCS / APEX
- Process changes not requiring new systems
- Quick integration wins with pre-built connectors

### Big Bets (high impact / high effort)
**Invest deliberately.** Build a phased plan.
- Core ERP migration (S/4HANA, Fusion Cloud)
- New customer experience platform
- AI / ML investments requiring data foundation
- Major process redesign

### Fill-Ins (low impact / low effort)
**Do when you have idle capacity.** Do not prioritize explicitly.
- Minor UX tweaks
- Small report additions
- One-off integrations

### Money Pits (low impact / high effort)
**Avoid.** Actively kill if they are already in flight.
- Vanity projects
- Legacy maintenance that could be retired
- Over-customization without clear value

## Scoring Technique

For each idea, assign:

**Impact** (1–5):
- Revenue uplift potential
- Cost takeout potential
- Risk reduction
- Strategic option value
- User satisfaction impact

**Effort** (1–5):
- Build / integration complexity
- Data readiness
- Change management load
- Capital required
- Skill gaps

Plot each idea as a dot at (effort, impact). Label the dot.

## Workshop Run

1. Ideas on sticky notes or a digital board (Miro / Mural / FigJam)
2. Print the 2x2 on a large poster or digital canvas
3. Each person places their ideas on the matrix individually
4. Group discusses outliers — "why did one person call this a Big Bet and another a Money Pit?"
5. Reach consensus on final placement (or acknowledge disagreement)
6. Circle the top 5–7 Quick Wins and top 2–3 Big Bets
7. Add owners and dates

## Pitfalls

- **Everything in Quick Wins** — the team is underestimating effort; push back
- **Everything in Big Bets** — the team is avoiding commitment; demand specificity
- **Nothing killed** — a strong matrix names at least 1–2 ideas to kill explicitly
- **No size mention** — ideas vary 100x in size; add a size indicator (small / medium / large dot)

## Variation — Value-Effort Matrix (Strategic)

For strategy portfolio decisions, replace "impact" with "strategic fit" and "effort" with "capital required":

```
         HIGH FIT
           │
 FLAGSHIP  │  CORE
 (defend,  │  (optimize,
 invest)   │  grow)
           │
 ──────────┼──────────
           │
 EXPLORE   │  LEGACY
 (test,    │  (harvest,
 pilot)    │  divest)
           │
         LOW FIT
```

## Thai-Specific Adjustments

- In workshops, Thai participants may cluster ideas politely in the middle (to avoid putting anyone's idea in Money Pits). Facilitate explicit courage: *"which idea should we NOT pursue?"*
- Respect hierarchy — the senior person's placement should not silently dominate; use individual placement first, then aggregate.

## Quality Bar

- At least 10 ideas plotted
- Each idea is named, not numbered
- Quick Wins and Big Bets have owners
- At least one idea placed in Money Pits with an explicit "kill" decision
- Follow-up plan exists (who does what by when)
