# Crazy 8s Template

A rapid divergent-ideation technique: 8 ideas in 8 minutes, one per folded square of paper. Forces breadth over polish. Originated from Google Design Sprint.

## When to Use
- Early ideation against a prioritized HMW
- Breaking the team out of the first-idea-they-had rut
- Equalizing voice (silent, individual, then share)
- Generating raw material for convergence

## Setup
- One sheet of A4 / Letter paper per person
- Fold in half three times → 8 squares per sheet
- Pen / sharpie (avoid pencils — forces commitment)
- Timer
- One HMW question displayed prominently

## Run
1. State the HMW aloud. Everyone re-states it in their own words to confirm shared understanding.
2. 60 seconds per square × 8 squares = 8 minutes silent drawing
3. One idea per square. Sketch + 3–5 words.
4. No talking during the 8 minutes. Phones away.
5. When time is up, everyone stops.

## Share & Cluster
- Each person puts their sheet on the wall
- 2-minute silent gallery walk — everyone reads everyone's
- Each person marks their top 3 ideas across all sheets with dots
- Cluster similar ideas
- Brief discussion: *"what theme did we see?"*

## Rules for the Facilitator
- Enforce silence during the 8 minutes
- Enforce the time box — most ideas come from the last 2 squares under pressure
- Do not allow "I'm not artistic" as an out — stick figures count
- Remind everyone: quantity over quality at this stage

## Variations

### Crazy 4s (when time is tight)
4 squares, 4 minutes. Works for a quick energizer or when the HMW is narrow.

### Worst Possible Idea
Flip the prompt: *"how might we make this worse?"* Generates more energy, reveals assumptions, and the inverted ideas often have real nuggets.

### Analogy Start
Before Crazy 8s, name an analogous industry (e.g., "how does Netflix handle discovery?"). Participants draft ideas inspired by that analogy, then translate.

### Remote Crazy 8s
On Miro / Mural / FigJam: pre-built 8-square template per participant; 8-minute timer on shared canvas; dot-voting built into the board.

## Quality Bar

- Every participant generates 8 ideas (not 3, not 12)
- Ideas are distinct — if 5 squares are variants of one idea, push for more range
- Gallery walk generates at least one surprise idea that no one came in with
- Clustering reveals 3–5 conceptual directions, not 20 disconnected dots

## Common Pitfalls

- **Talking through ideas** — defeats the silence rule; push back firmly
- **Polishing one square** — remind people: sketch, move on
- **Self-editing** — quiet internal critic = fewer ideas; push for quantity
- **Skipping the gallery walk** — without sharing, individual ideas do not compound into team momentum

## What Comes Next

After Crazy 8s, pick the top 2–3 ideas and move to:
- **Solution sketch** — a more detailed 3-panel storyboard
- **Impact-effort matrix** — plot the top 8–10 ideas from all participants
- **Concept poster** — full-page elaboration of the most-promising idea

Crazy 8s is a divergent tool, not a decision tool. Pair it with a convergence technique in the same session.
