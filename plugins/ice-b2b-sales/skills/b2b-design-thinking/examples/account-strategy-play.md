# Example — Account Strategy Play (ST-primary)

A worked example where the request is **account strategy / market entry**, not workshop facilitation. Discipline: **ST-primary with DT supporting**.

## The Situation

**Internal trigger:** Our firm is reviewing its Thai mid-market Oracle Fusion practice and has noticed that 40% of deal pursuits in H1 FY27 stalled at the proposal stage. Leadership asks: "Rebuild our account strategy for Thai mid-market Oracle Fusion — where should we play, how will we win, what should we stop doing?"

**Ask:** Produce an account strategy memo + Choice Cascade for presentation to the partnership.

## Step 1 — Name the Discipline

This is an ST-primary request (market, portfolio, positioning). DT comes in later for customer validation of top segment bets.

We will use:
- `templates/choice-cascade.md` as the structural spine
- `templates/pestel.md`, `templates/five-forces.md` for external scan
- `templates/three-horizons.md` for portfolio view
- `templates/scenario-2x2.md` to stress-test
- DT empathy interviews with 3 recent won + 3 recent lost clients for customer-side signal

## Step 2 — External Scan

### PESTEL — Thai mid-market 2026–2028
- **Political:** BOI incentive continuity favorable; new gov digital-first orientation
- **Economic:** SME capex cautious; moderate GDP growth; baht volatility
- **Social:** Oracle / SAP talent shortage; shift to Thai management in MNC subs
- **Technological:** GenAI mainstreaming, low-code gaining at SME, cloud mandate rising
- **Environmental:** ESG disclosure pressure → ERP-driven reporting opportunity
- **Legal:** PDPA enforcement intensifying; Cloud Standard 2567 for regulated sectors

### Five Forces — Thai Mid-Market Fusion Implementation
- **Rivalry:** moderate-high (Oracle-specialized SIs number ~6 in Bangkok)
- **New entrants:** low-moderate threat (Oracle partner tiers hard-won; hyperscaler bundling emerging)
- **Buyer power:** moderate-high (SMEs highly price-sensitive, willing to delay)
- **Supplier power:** high (Oracle drives license; certified talent scarce)
- **Substitutes:** moderate-rising (NetSuite, Odoo, Dynamics 365 at lower price)

### Implications
- Differentiate on delivery rigor + Thai localization + regulatory defensibility
- Avoid price competition at the bottom of the market (handed over to NetSuite / Odoo specialists)
- Protect talent moat

## Step 3 — Internal (VRIO + Portfolio)

### VRIO on our capabilities
| Capability | Valuable | Rare | Inimitable | Organized | Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Oracle Fusion hands-on depth | ✓ | ✓ | partial | ✓ | Temporary advantage |
| Thai IFRS / BOI accelerator | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | partial | Sustainable (if we organize) |
| Fixed-price estimation discipline | ✓ | ✓ | partial | ✓ | Temporary advantage |
| Change management at mid-market | ✓ | partial | partial | ✓ | Parity |
| Senior Thai leadership | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ | Sustainable |

Key insight: Thai accelerator + senior Thai leadership are our genuine sustainable differentiators. Technical depth is temporary (copyable).

### Three Horizons view of practice
- **H1 (defend):** Fusion ERP implementations in existing ICP — protect
- **H2 (build):** BOI-defensible accelerators productized; AI / agent extensions on top of Fusion
- **H3 (explore):** Vertical solutions (F&B, healthcare); AI-native offerings

Current resource allocation: 80% H1 / 15% H2 / 5% H3 — too H1-heavy.
Target: 60% H1 / 30% H2 / 10% H3.

## Step 4 — DT Supporting — Customer Signal

Interview 3 recent wins + 3 recent losses (30 min each):

### Wins — what made us win
- "Thai leadership spoke our language"
- "Fixed-price and you held the line"
- "You knew BOI and IFRS cold"
- "Senior partner came to the steering meetings personally"

### Losses — what cost us
- "Price" (2 of 3 losses) — 10–20% above competitor
- "Felt like you didn't know our industry" (1 of 3 losses — food manufacturing)
- "Proposal was generic" — customer couldn't see themselves in it
- "Response time slow" (2 of 3 losses)

### DT → ST handoff
- Customer values Thai leadership + regulatory fluency + partner-level attention (confirms ST thesis)
- Losing on price = wrong-ICP accounts (below our sweet spot); these should be handed off, not chased
- Losing on "didn't know our industry" = we lack vertical accelerators in some segments
- Slow response = operating model problem, not strategy problem

## Step 5 — Choice Cascade

### Aspiration
*"To be the #1 Thai mid-market Oracle Fusion Cloud partner by FY29, measured by 35% share of Thai mid-market Fusion go-lives and ฿500M practice revenue."*

### Where to Play
**We will play in:**
- Thai mid-market (฿1B–฿10B revenue)
- Manufacturing, distribution, F&B, and BOI-certified companies
- Greater Bangkok + Eastern Seaboard / EEC geographies
- Direct sales with Oracle co-sell

**We will NOT play in:**
- SME below ฿500M revenue (handed to NetSuite / Odoo specialists)
- Thai public sector (dedicated government practice)
- Financial services (dedicated BFSI practice)
- Price-led RFPs where we cannot hold the line

### How to Win
- Differentiation: fixed-price, BOI-defensible, Thai senior partner-attached delivery
- Trade-off: we give up volume in bottom-of-market; we give up technology variety (Oracle-only)
- Moat (7 Powers):
  - Switching costs (become the Oracle partner of record)
  - Cornered resource (Thai senior Oracle leadership)
  - Process power (fixed-price methodology)

### Capabilities
Core:
1. Oracle Fusion Cloud deep configuration
2. Thai IFRS / BOI accelerator and audit fluency
3. Fixed-price methodology with variance discipline
4. Senior partner-attached delivery
5. AI / extension accelerators (BTP equivalent — OIC / VBCS / APEX)

Table stakes:
- Change management
- Integration capability
- Program management

Gaps:
- F&B vertical accelerator (build in FY27 H2)
- Healthcare vertical accelerator (monitor demand; build FY28 if signal strong)
- Faster RFP response (operating model, not capability per se)

### Management Systems
- Monthly deal review with strategic-fit gate (are we in ICP? if not, why pursuing?)
- Quarterly portfolio review with Three Horizons view
- Partner compensation tied to Oracle Cloud Elite metrics + Thai reference wins
- Hiring bar: Oracle certified + Thai accounting / BOI fluency for senior roles
- Response-time SLA: 10 business days for RFPs in ICP; auto-decline out-of-ICP RFPs

## Step 6 — Scenario Stress-Test

Using `templates/scenario-2x2.md` with axes:
- X: Cloud adoption pace
- Y: Regulatory stringency

In all 4 scenarios, our "Thai senior + regulatory fluency" strategy holds. Under "Digital Leap" (low reg / fast cloud), we face more price pressure — mitigated by moving faster on AI / extension (H2) moves.

## Step 7 — Commitments / Capital Allocation

- Shift 10% of H1 capacity to H2 (accelerator productization + AI extension)
- Launch F&B accelerator in FY27 H2 (targeted hire: 2 F&B Fusion consultants)
- Decline out-of-ICP deals explicitly (set expectation with sales team)
- RFP response SLA instituted in FY27 Q1
- Quarterly strategic-fit review agenda added to leadership ops cadence

## Step 9 — Orchestration Delegations

- Vendor pursuit detail → `b2b-enterprise-sale-strategy` (public sector strategy; large enterprise sub-strategy)
- Pipeline hygiene → `sales-pipeline-report`, `sales:pipeline-review`

## Key Lessons

- **ST-primary requests need DT supporting for customer signal** — without it, strategy is detached from the market.
- **Playing to Win Choice Cascade is the cleanest spine** — every question cascades.
- **Explicit "Where we will NOT play" is where the value is** — most firms' Where-to-Play is too broad.
- **VRIO + Three Horizons together** reveal capability gaps and portfolio imbalance simultaneously.
- **Scenario stress-test** protects against single-future optimization.
- **Strategic-fit gate in monthly deal reviews** is the ongoing mechanism that keeps the strategy live.
