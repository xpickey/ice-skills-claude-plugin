# 3-Day Design Sprint Kit

A compressed, Google-style design sprint adapted for enterprise software envisioning. Takes a team from problem statement to validated prototype in 3 days. Discipline: **DT-primary with ST anchoring**.

## Objective

Leave the sprint with:
- Shared definition of the target problem
- A concrete prototype (storyboard, wireframe, or live demo)
- Validation data from 3–5 target users
- Decision: invest further, pivot, or kill

## Ideal Audience

**Core team (present all 3 days):** 6–8 people — 1 executive sponsor (as "Decider"), 1 facilitator, 2 subject experts, 2 technical / design contributors, 1 voice-of-user.

**Guests (present day 1 morning):** additional stakeholders to contribute context, then leave.

**Test users (day 3 afternoon):** 3–5 people who represent the target persona — ideally real customer users.

## Pre-Sprint (1 week)

- Select problem that is real, focused, and ripe
- Confirm Decider role and availability
- Recruit 3–5 test users for day 3
- Book dedicated space (no interruptions)
- Pre-read + pre-work for core team

## Day 1 — Map & Target

### Morning — Map
- **Open (30 min):** goals, agenda, Decider role
- **Expert interviews (90 min):** 15 min each from 4–6 subject experts on the problem
- **How-Might-We harvest (30 min):** during interviews, everyone writes HMWs on stickies
- **Map the problem (60 min):** simple flow from user → end state, with the problem area highlighted

### Afternoon — Target
- **HMW affinity (45 min):** cluster and select top themes
- **Target the problem (45 min):** Decider picks the specific user, specific moment, specific goal
- **Ask the experts (60 min):** lightning talks on analogous solutions, tech possibilities, what's already been tried

End-of-day deliverable: a problem target statement and a long-list of solution directions.

## Day 2 — Sketch & Decide

### Morning — Sketch
- **Lightning demos (45 min):** 3-minute demos of inspiring solutions from any industry
- **Notes + ideas (20 min):** silent capture
- **Crazy 8s (8 min):** 8 ideas in 8 minutes
- **Solution sketch (90 min):** each person draws a detailed 3-panel storyboard of their best idea

### Afternoon — Decide
- **Art museum (15 min):** sketches posted silently
- **Heat map (10 min):** everyone marks interesting parts with dots
- **Speed critique (3 min per sketch):** facilitator narrates each sketch
- **Straw poll (5 min):** each person picks their one favorite
- **Supervote (5 min):** the Decider makes the call — 1 or 2 concepts to prototype
- **Storyboard (120 min):** detailed 10–15 panel board of the chosen concept

End-of-day deliverable: a detailed storyboard that can be built in a day.

## Day 3 — Prototype & Validate

### Morning — Prototype
- Split into roles:
  - Makers (build the prototype — often a clickable Figma or a scripted demo)
  - Stitcher (ensures scenes connect)
  - Writer (polishes copy / dialogue)
  - Asset collector (images, icons, data)
  - Interviewer (prepares questions for afternoon testing)
- Deadline: prototype ready by 1pm

### Afternoon — Validate
- **User interviews (5 × 45 min):** 1 test user at a time
  - Welcome (5 min)
  - Context questions (5 min)
  - Introduce prototype, observe (20 min)
  - Debrief (10 min)
  - Thank (5 min)
- Team observes from a second room; takes notes on a structured template

### End of Day — Synthesize
- **Patterns review (30 min):** clusters of reactions
- **Decision (15 min):** invest, pivot, or kill
- **30-60-90 plan (30 min):** next steps, owners, dates

## Deliverables (within 5 business days)

- Sprint report (10 pages)
- Storyboard (final version)
- Prototype artifact (Figma / scripted demo / HTML mockup)
- 5 user interview summaries
- Decision memo (invest / pivot / kill) with rationale
- 30-60-90 plan

## Strategic Fit Gate

At end of day 2 (after the supervote), the facilitator and Decider step aside to reconfirm:
- Does the chosen concept fit our Where-to-Play?
- Is pricing / commercial envelope realistic?
- Any red flags suggesting we should reshape?

If the fit is broken, revise the storyboard rather than proceed blindly.

## Thai Adaptation

- Day 1 morning: extend introductions + relationship time
- Supervote in Thai workshops: use anonymous written vote before Decider's public call (reduces face-saving dynamics)
- User interviews in Thai (translator if needed); facilitator may need to coach Thai interviewers on open-ended questioning
- Closure: allow for "consult internally" before final invest/pivot/kill decision

## Common Failure Modes

- **No Decider** — the sprint drifts; make sure a single person is empowered to break ties
- **Prototype too ambitious** — a day is not enough; scope must be ruthlessly narrow
- **Test users not real** — colleagues pretending to be users gives useless signal; invest in real recruits
- **No follow-through** — without a 30-60-90 owner, the sprint is entertainment

## Quality Bar

- Test users are representative (not internal)
- Prototype is specific enough to trigger meaningful reactions
- Decider makes a clear invest / pivot / kill call
- 30-60-90 plan has owners and dates
- Sprint report shipped within 5 business days
