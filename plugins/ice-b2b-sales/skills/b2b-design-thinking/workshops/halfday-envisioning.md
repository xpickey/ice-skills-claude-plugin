# Half-Day Envisioning Workshop Kit

A 4-hour workshop that takes a customer from context to top-3 solution concepts with a draft Why-Invest/Why-Now/Why-Us. Discipline: **DT-primary with ST embedded**.

## Objective

Leave the room with:
- Shared understanding of AS-IS journey and pain
- 3 prioritized HMWs
- Top 3 solution concepts with storyboards
- Draft Why-Invest / Why-Now / Why-Us
- Commitments for next 30 days

## Ideal Audience

6–12 stakeholders. Mix of executive sponsor, cross-functional managers, technical architects, and 1–2 end users.

## Pre-Workshop (4 hours of prep)

- Strategic fit check
- Stakeholder intelligence (all attendees)
- Pre-read: 2-page context brief
- Pre-work: each attendee sends 2 pain points and 1 aspiration
- Room / Miro setup
- Facilitator + note-taker + optional SME on bench

## Agenda (4 hours + break)

### 0:00–0:20 — Open (20 min)
- Welcome, agenda, outcomes
- Attendee introductions (1 min each)
- Hopes & fears (silent sticky + read aloud)
- Agree on working agreements (phones down, one voice, time-boxed)

### 0:20–1:20 — Discovery block (60 min)
- Empathy capture (20 min): pain points shared by attendees, written on stickies, clustered
- Journey map AS-IS (30 min): plenary co-creation on whiteboard / Miro
- Moments of truth identification (10 min): circle the 2–3 highest-pain stages

### 1:20–1:35 — Break (15 min)

### 1:35–2:20 — Problem framing (45 min)
- POV drafting (15 min): per pain, write "[persona] needs [need] because [insight]"
- HMW generation (15 min): 5–10 HMWs from POVs, silent sticky then share
- HMW prioritization (15 min): impact × feasibility dot vote; select top 3

### 2:20–3:05 — Ideation (45 min)
- Crazy 8s (15 min) against top HMW
- Analogous inspiration (10 min): "how does [other industry] solve this?"
- Tech-forward (10 min): "if AI / low-code could do anything, what would it do?"
- Share and cluster (10 min)

### 3:05–3:35 — Convergence (30 min)
- Impact-effort matrix (15 min): plot top 10–15 ideas
- Top 3 storyboards (15 min): 3-panel sketches per concept

### 3:35–4:05 — Value case draft (30 min)
- For the top concept, draft Why-Invest / Why-Now / Why-Us in three columns
- Capture client's language verbatim
- Quantify where possible (with caveats)

### 4:05–4:15 — Close + commitments (10 min)
- Recap: journey, HMWs, concepts, Why-I/N/U draft
- Commitments board: owner × action × date for next 30 days
- Thank you

## Deliverables (within 5 business days)

- Workshop report (5–8 pages)
- Cleaned-up journey map
- Top 3 concepts with storyboards
- Why-Invest / Why-Now / Why-Us one-pager
- 30-60-90 plan
- Updated account strategy + CRM

## Thai Adaptation

- 20-minute opening with refreshments (not 5 minutes)
- Silent + written ideation for 30–45% of ideation time
- Break small groups by function (finance / ops / IT) to protect hierarchy dynamics
- Language: Thai for empathy / journey, English for frameworks / tech
- Honor the "take away for consultation" norm — commitments can be conditional on internal consultation

## Materials Kit

- Pre-read (2 pages) sent 3 days prior
- Printed agenda per seat
- Journey map canvas (A0 printed or Miro template)
- POV / HMW template sheets
- Crazy 8s fold-paper per person
- Storyboard sheets (3-panel printed)
- Impact-effort matrix poster
- Why-I/N/U canvas (3 columns)
- Sharpies, dot stickers, timer
- Photographer / screenshot capture owner

## Facilitator Self-Check

- [ ] Strategic fit confirmed
- [ ] Pre-read sent and acknowledged
- [ ] Stakeholder intelligence complete
- [ ] Time-box for every activity set
- [ ] Thai adaptations considered
- [ ] Photo / digital archive process in place
- [ ] 5-day deliverable owner named

## Common Failure Modes

- **Ideation block too short** — 45 min is minimum; squeeze elsewhere if short
- **No storyboard** — ideas stay abstract; storyboards make them testable
- **Why-I/N/U skipped** — the whole workshop loses the sales narrative thread
- **No commitments** — end without named owners and dates = no follow-through
