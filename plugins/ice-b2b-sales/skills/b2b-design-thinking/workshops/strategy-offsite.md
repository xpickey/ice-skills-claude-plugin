# Strategy Offsite Kit (1-day ST-intensive)

A full-day strategic thinking session for leadership teams — internal firm strategy or client-facing board envisioning. Discipline: **ST-primary with DT embedded for customer validation**.

## Objective

Leave the day with:
- A refreshed Choice Cascade (Playing to Win)
- Portfolio view across three horizons
- Build-vs-buy-vs-partner decisions for H2/H3 options
- Short customer validation of top 2–3 bets
- Capital allocation commitments

## Ideal Audience

Leadership team of 8–15. For clients: CEO, CFO, CSO, heads of business units. For internal: firm partners, practice leaders, strategy lead.

## Pre-Offsite (2 weeks of prep)

- Strategic assumptions audit (current vs. reality)
- PESTEL scan
- Five Forces refresh
- Market data pack (TAM, SOM, growth rates, competitor moves)
- Pre-read (5 pages): current strategy summary + 3 key questions
- Pre-survey: each attendee ranks top strategic priorities
- External perspective: optional keynote speaker / analyst briefing

## Agenda (8 hours)

### 0:00–0:30 — Open (30 min)
- Welcome, purpose, agenda
- Aspiration re-statement (what does winning look like?)
- Working agreements (dissent welcome, time-boxed, Decider named)

### 0:30–2:00 — External scan (90 min)
- PESTEL key moves (30 min)
- Five Forces refresh (30 min)
- Competitor moves + disruption signals (30 min)
- Output: tailwinds, headwinds, wild cards

### 2:00–3:00 — Internal scan (60 min)
- VRIO assessment — which capabilities are truly unique? (30 min)
- Capability gaps named (20 min)
- Founder's Mentality / culture check (10 min)

### 3:00–4:00 — Lunch (60 min, working discussion)

### 4:00–5:30 — Portfolio (90 min)
- Three Horizons review of current initiatives (30 min)
- Capital allocation current vs. target (15 min)
- Build-vs-buy-vs-partner analysis for top H2/H3 options (45 min)

### 5:30–6:30 — Trade-offs (60 min)
- Options for the forward strategy (list 3–5)
- For each, explicit trade-offs named
- Criteria for choice (e.g., fit, risk, capital, speed)
- Score options against criteria

### 6:30–7:30 — Choice (60 min)
- Draft Choice Cascade (Aspiration → Where → How → Capabilities → Management Systems)
- Coherence test walk-through
- Decider breaks ties

### 7:30–7:50 — Customer validation quick-pulse (optional, 20 min)
- Phone 2–3 trusted customers / sponsor users
- 10-minute calls: "we are considering X — what's your reaction?"
- Live feedback captured in the room

### 7:50–8:00 — Close + commitments (10 min)
- Capital allocation change
- Owners for each strategic move
- Next board / management touchpoint
- Thank you

## Deliverables (within 10 business days)

- Strategy memo (10–15 pages):
  - Situation + complication
  - Options considered with trade-offs
  - Recommended Choice Cascade
  - Capital allocation
  - Capability gaps + plan to close
  - Risk register
  - 30-60-90 plan
- Strategy deck (15–20 slides) for board
- Balanced scorecard + strategy map (if BSC is used)
- Commitments tracker

## Thai Adaptation

- Thai leadership offsites benefit from overnight consultation — consider a 1.5-day format with dinner in between
- Senior voice first in each module; juniors invited explicitly to contradict (use silent sticky first)
- Decisions may be "subject to board consultation" — build this into timeline
- For state-owned enterprises or government: add explicit alignment check with DGA / ministry priorities

## Facilitator Self-Check

- [ ] Data pack (PESTEL, Five Forces, market) pre-built
- [ ] Pre-read + pre-survey distributed
- [ ] Decider role named
- [ ] Customer contacts lined up for validation pulse
- [ ] 10-day deliverable owner named
- [ ] External perspective considered (speaker, analyst)

## Common Failure Modes

- **No trade-off** — "we will do all of the above" = no strategy
- **No kill decision** — existing programs protected regardless of fit
- **Choice Cascade left unfinished** — coherence test skipped
- **No customer validation** — ST without DT produces strategically sound bets that fail on adoption
- **Deliverable drifts** — 10 days is generous; if slip, send interim memo

## Quality Bar

- Choice Cascade is coherent top-to-bottom
- Capital allocation change is named
- At least one explicit "stop" or "kill"
- Capability gaps have plans, not wishes
- Deliverables shipped on schedule
